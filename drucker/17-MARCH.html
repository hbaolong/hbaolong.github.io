The Purpose of Profit

Profit is the ultimate test of business performance.

profit : A profit is an amount of money that you gain when you are paid more for something than it cost you to make, get, or do it.

ultimate : happening at the end of a long process

performance : how well or badly you do sth; how well or badly sth works

Profit serves three purposes.

One is it measures the net effectiveness and soundness of a business’s efforts.

net effectiveness : 有效性、效率

soundness : 可靠性；安定性；完整性；坚固性

Another is the “risk premium” that covers the costs of staying in business—replacement, obsolescence, market risk and uncertainty.

premium : an amount of money that you pay once or regularly for an insurance policy. risk premium : 风险溢价

obsolescence : the state of becoming old-fashioned and no longer useful (= becoming obsolete ) 过时；淘汰；陈旧

Seen from this point of view, there is no such thing as “profit”;

there are only “costs of being in business” and “costs of staying in business.”

And the task of a business is to provide adequately for these “costs of staying

in business” by earning an adequate profit.

Finally, profit ensures the supply of future capital for innovation and expansion, either directly, by providing

the means of self-financing out of retained earnings, or indi-rectly, through providing sufficient inducement for new outside capital in

the form in which it is best suited to the enterprise’s objectives.

ACTION POINT:

Decide to pull the plug on an unprofitable business if it is not covering the cost required to stay in business or providing enough

capital for future growth.
