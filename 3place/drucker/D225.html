大纲

今天说几个零零碎碎的事情。

前几天和尹建莉公司的一个朋友喝酒，大家聊起教育孩子的事情，这位朋友说，他对孩子一点都不着

急，因为尹建莉老师说过一句话：“不要怕孩子小时候没出息，撑的越久的孩子，越有出息，你看到那

地了么？每年都长粮食的，肯定多不了，荒上几年，你再看看。肯定能长出好庄稼来。”

吴晓波老师经常讲一些企业和企业家的故事，他经常用养猪和养孩子来打比方：“有的人做企业，就像

养猪，养废了就卖了换钱；有的人做企业，就像养孩子，希望这个孩子慢慢地、健康地长大，然后按

自己的想法过完这一生。”

企业毕竟是一个经济体，对于所有人来说，做企业肯定是要赚钱的，特别是刚开始做企业的人，当企

业开始赚钱时，他们会把大部分精力放在利润上。

前几天群里一个朋友说了一句话很有意思：“原来赚钱会上瘾”。

而企业的发展是有其客观规律的，就像孩子，就像种地。

他们在发展初期，虽然可以产生一些结果，可是，他们还是要成长，成长还是需要不断增加输入，只

有足够的养分他们才可以健康成长，长大成人。

所以，德鲁克建议说：对于新公司来说，不要太在意利润，而应该把利润反哺到企业，让企业健康成

长。对于进入发展通道的企业来说，现金流比利润重要的多太多了。

Managing Cash in the New Venture

There is an old banker’s rule of thumb according to which one assumes that bills will have to be

paid sixty days earlier than expected and receivables will come in sixty days later.

thumb：

拇指

the short thick finger at the side of the hand, slightly apart from the other four

Entrepreneurs starting new ventures are rarely unmindful of money; on the contrary, they tend to

be greedy. They therefore focus on profits.

Entrepreneurs： 企业家（entrepreneur的复数）

unmindful：

不注意的；不留心的；漫不经心的

greedy：

贪婪的；贪心的；贪吃的；渴望的

of sb/sth ( formal ) not giving thought or attention to sb/sth

(for sth) wanting more money, power, food, etc. than you really need

But this is the wrong focus for a new venture, or rather, it comes last rather than first.

Cash flow, capital, and controls come much earlier.

Without them, the profit figures are fiction—good for twelve to eighteen months, perhaps, after

which they evaporate.

fiction：

小说

evaporate：

（使）蒸发，挥发

[ U ] a type of literature that describes imaginary people and events, not real ones

Growth has to be fed. In financial terms this means that growth in a new venture demands adding

financial resources rather than taking them out. The healthier a new venture and the faster it grows,

the more financial feeding it requires.

fed：想

The new venture needs cash-flow analysis, cash-flow forecasts, and cash management.

The fact that America’s new ventures of the last few years (with the significant exception of hightech companies) have been doing so much better than new ventures used to do is largely because

the new entrepreneurs in the United States have learned that entrepreneurship demands financial

management. Cash management is fairly easy if there are reliable cash-flow forecasts, with

“reliable” meaning “worst case” assumptions rather than hopes. If the forecast is overly

conservative, the worst that can happen is a temporary cash surplus.

ACTION POINT:

Develop “worst case” estimates of cash flow and cash forecasts for new ventures. Monitor

receivables and inventory levels closely.
