9 MARCH

Organize for Constant Change

Today’s certainties always become tomorrow’s absurdities.

One thing is certain for developed countries—and probably for the entire world—we face long years of profound changes. An organization must be organized for constant change. It will no longer be possible to consider entrepreneurial innovation as lying outside of management or even as peripheral to management. Entrepreneurial innovation will have to become the very heart and core of management. The organization’s function is entrepreneurial: to put knowledge to work—on tools, products, and processes; on the design of work; on knowledge itself.

Deliberate emphasis on innovation may be needed most where technological changes are least spectacular. Everyone in a pharmaceutical company knows that the company’s survival depends on its ability to replace three-quarters of its products with entirely new ones every ten years. But how many people in an insurance company realize that the company’s growth—perhaps even its survival—depends on the development of new forms of insurance? The less spectacular or prominent technological change is in a business, the greater the danger that the whole organization will ossify, and the more important, therefore, is the emphasis on innovation.

ACTION POINT:

Are you and your organization in danger of ossification? Decide how you and your organization can systematically innovate, and build this into your management process.

1. 找出文章中的生词，注音、解释和例句

以下是文章中的生词及其详细信息：

  • Organize
    • 注音：[ˈɔːɡənaɪz]
    • 中文解释：组织、安排
    • 英文解释：To arrange or plan systematically
    • 例句：The company organizes annual training sessions for its employees.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 organizare，意为“组织”。
  • Entrepreneurial
    • 注音：[ˌɒntrəprəˈnɜːrɪəl]
    • 中文解释：企业家的、创业的
    • 英文解释：Relating to the creation or running of a business
    • 例句：Entrepreneurial skills are essential for success in today’s fast-paced economy.
    • 词根/词源：源自法语 entrepreneur，意为“企业家”。
  • Innovation
    • 注音：[ˌɪnəˈveɪʃn]
    • 中文解释：创新
    • 英文解释：The introduction of new ideas, methods, or products
    • 例句：The company’s success is driven by its commitment to innovation.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 innovare，意为“更新、改革”。
  • Ossify
    • 注音：[ˈɒsɪfaɪ]
    • 中文解释：僵化、硬化
    • 英文解释：To become rigid or inflexible
    • 例句：The organization risks ossifying if it fails to adapt to new challenges.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 ossificare，意为“使成骨”。
  • Peripheral
    • 注音：[pəˈrɪfərəl]
    • 中文解释：次要的、边缘的
    • 英文解释：Of minor importance or not central
    • 例句：Customer service is not a peripheral concern for any business.
    • 词根/词源：源自希腊语 peripheria，意为“周围”。
  • Spectacular
    • 注音：[spekˈtæktəl]
    • 中文解释：壮观的、引人注目的
    • 英文解释：Extraordinary or impressive
    • 例句：The company’s growth has been nothing short of spectacular.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 spectaculum，意为“表演”。
  • Form
    • 注音：[fɔːm]
    • 中文解释：形式、形状
    • 英文解释：A particular way in which something exists or appears
    • 例句：The company has developed new forms of insurance to meet customer needs.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 forma，意为“形状、形式”。
  • Knowledge
    • 注音：[ˈnɒlɪdʒ]
    • 中文解释：知识
    • 英文解释：Facts, information, and skills acquired by a person
    • 例句：The organization’s success is built on its deep knowledge of the market.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 cognitio，意为“认识”。
  • Growth
    • 注音：[ɡrəʊθ]
    • 中文解释：增长、发展
    • 英文解释：The process of increasing in size, quantity, or value
    • 例句：The company has experienced rapid growth over the past decade.
    • 词根/词源：源自古英语 growan，意为“生长”。
  • Survival
    • 注音：[səˈvaɪvəl]
    • 中文解释：生存
    • 英文解释：The act or process of living or continuing to exist
    • 例句：The company’s survival depends on its ability to adapt to market changes.
    • 词根/词源：源自拉丁语 supervivere，意为“存活”。

问题1：细节理解题

The article suggests that in the future, entrepreneurial innovation will: A. Be considered peripheral to management B. Become the core of management C. Only be relevant to high-tech industries D. Decrease in importance

答案：B

问题2：推理判断题

Why does the article emphasize innovation in industries with less spectacular technological change? A. Because such industries are more likely to grow rapidly B. Because innovation is more critical to prevent ossification in these industries C. Because these industries rely solely on traditional methods D. Because technological change is easier to implement in these industries

答案：B

问题3：主旨大意题

The main idea of the article is that: A. Innovation is only important in high-tech industries B. Organizations must be organized for constant change and prioritize innovation C. Entrepreneurial innovation is unnecessary for long-term success D. Technological change is the only driver of organizational growth

答案：B

问题4：词义猜测题

The word “ossify” in the article most likely means: A. To become rigid or inflexible B. To grow rapidly C. To increase in flexibility D. To innovate

答案：A

问题5：细节理解题

According to the article, what is the primary function of an organization? A. To maintain traditional methods B. To put knowledge to work C. To focus on peripheral activities D. To avoid change

答案：B

4. 管理学角度的题目

以下是基于文章内容的3个管理学题目：

问题1

The article states that entrepreneurial innovation must become the core of management. Why is this important for organizations in today’s rapidly changing world?

参考答案： Entrepreneurial innovation is crucial because it enables organizations to adapt to constant change and stay competitive. By making innovation the core of management, organizations can systematically respond to new challenges and opportunities, ensuring long-term survival and growth.

问题2

The article warns about the risk of ossification in industries with less spectacular technological change. How can organizations prevent this?

参考答案： Organizations can prevent ossification by deliberately emphasizing innovation, even in industries where technological change is less obvious. This involves fostering a culture of creativity, encouraging the development of new products, services, and processes, and continuously challenging the status quo.

问题3

How does the article suggest organizations should prioritize innovation? Provide examples from the article to support your answer.

参考答案： The article suggests that innovation should be integrated into the core functions of management. For example, organizations should focus on putting knowledge to work by innovating tools, products, processes, and even the design of work itself. This requires a deliberate emphasis on innovation, even in industries where technological change is not prominent, to ensure sustained growth and adaptability.

5. 《论语》视角的关联

问题1

The article emphasizes the importance of constant change and innovation. How does this relate to the Confucian concept of “变易” (change and transformation)?

参考答案： The Confucian concept of “变易” emphasizes the importance of adapting to change and embracing transformation to achieve harmony and balance. Similarly, the article highlights the necessity of constant change and innovation for organizational survival and growth. Both ideas stress the importance of flexibility and adaptability in the face of evolving circumstances.

问题2

The article suggests that innovation should be the core of management. How does this align with the Confucian principle of “知者乐水” (the wise enjoy water), which symbolizes adaptability and flow?

参考答案： The Confucian principle of “知者乐水” symbolizes the importance of adaptability and continuous flow, much like how water changes shape to fit its environment. In the article, innovation is seen as the core of management, requiring organizations to adapt and evolve continuously. Both ideas emphasize the need for flexibility and adaptability to thrive in changing conditions.

问题3

The article warns against ossification in organizations. How does this relate to the Confucian idea of “温故而知新” (learning from the past to understand the new)?

参考答案： The Confucian idea of “温故而知新” emphasizes the importance of learning from the past while embracing new knowledge and ideas. In the article, the risk of ossification arises when organizations fail to innovate and adapt. By continuously learning from past experiences and embracing new opportunities, organizations can avoid僵化 and maintain vitality, aligning with the Confucian principle of balancing tradition and innovation.

东西方文化的一个区别

我发现了东西方文化的一个区别。今天就说说这个发现。

东方文化总是在”法祖”，而西方文化总是在”期来”。

也就是说，我们中华的文化总是认为古代是最好的，而西方人总是认为”希望在未来”。

在我们的文化里，过去总是比现在好，现在的一切努力，只是为了达到我们古人的那个高度。几千年来，我们反复注解《论语》，然后大家争论，“你的解读不是圣人之意，而我的更接近”。也就是说，大家的努力只是为了更加接近祖先。

一部《黄帝内经》几千年来，中医还是在那个框框下解读，唯恐出框。同样的还有《易经》，在我们的文化中，我们的文明出生是最完美的，之后就”漏”了，我们要做的就是不停修复，让自己更像古人。

而西方人，他们每天都在发现未来，他们觉得过去是确定的，他们要做的是成就未来。所以，他们每天都在创造和发明。对于不能理解、不能解释的，他们就做假设，然后空着，留给未来的人去处理。

而我们东方人，遇到不能理解的、不能解释的，则会去翻故纸堆，找找《易经》《论语》《黄帝内经》《道德经》等等等等。

对于西方人来说，今天，以及未来的每一天，都是全新的未知的，每天都在变化，所以，他们每天要做的就是迎接各种新的事情，然后把新的事情融合到自己的生活里。

而对于我们东方人来说，今天至少是昨天的延续，我们昨天已经做的足够好的，今天要做的是小心翼翼地像昨天学习就好了。

其实，我们也有一些祖先意识到这个问题了，他们也会发出一些振聋发聩的声音：上胡不法先王之法？！而《易经》里也说了”不可为典要，唯变所适”。

可是，没用，几千年后，我们还是在法先王之法，还是逐字逐句地理解圣贤的经典。在我们内心，我们还是觉得，我们是没法超越先人的，所以，我们害怕哪怕一点点的变化。

而这个时代，一切都在变化，唯一不变的就是变化。

你在留恋昨天么？还是在期待自然而来的明天？

听说人的全身细胞，每7年就会换一次，七年后你和七年前完全不是一个人了。

民国是好于现在的，大清是好于民国的，明朝是好于大清的，唐宋是优于大明的，而最优的则是三皇五帝的时候。
