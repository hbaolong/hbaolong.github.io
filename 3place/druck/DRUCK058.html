透过金庸读懂德鲁克

客户想要什么？

有个朋友想卖课，也就是做知识付费，他是金融领域的，所以开课讲金融报名的，有保险，有信托，

有基金，有养老，也有宏观经济。

我问他，你为啥要讲这么多呀。他说，金融人士对这些刚兴趣呀。

我又问他，你一个人讲的了这么多么？他说，我可以请人呀。

说的好像很有道理。

过了几天，我去问他，课卖的怎么样？他说一般，几十个吧。他也做了免费的直播课，免费课也就300

多人听，大家好像兴趣不是特别大。

然后我又问题，你觉得你的客户是谁？

他很兴奋地说：“800万保险从业者，他们是最爱学习的”。

我就很好奇了，既然有800万个目标客户，为什么才来300多人呢？

他说：“我也不知道”。

我又问他：”你觉得你的客户要想要什么呢？“

他说：”获客吧“。

然后我又问他：”你的课，能帮助业务员获客么？还有，这是你的想法？还是他们的想法？“

他说：”不知道“。

现在讲课的人很多，都快比上课的人呢多了，为什么有的课能卖上千万，有的课就算9.9，甚至免费，

也没几个人听呢？

”我的客户群体是800万保险业务员，他们需要金融知识“。

这是他们的想法么？

“你有客户么？”

“你了解你的客户么？”

“你的客户需要什么？以什么形式需要？”

“他们为什么要找你来学？”

不要自己回答这些问题了，找到你的“客户”，问他们吧。

27 FEBRUARY

Defining Business Purpose and Mission

What is our business?

Nothing may seem simpler or more obvious than to know what a company’s business is.

A steel mill makes steel; a railroad runs trains to carry freight and passengers; an insurance

company underwrites fire risks; a bank lends money.

steel：

钢

mill ：

常构成复合词

工厂；制造厂

underwrites：

a strong hard metal that is made of a mixture of iron and

( often in compounds

) a factory that produces a particular type of material

V-T If an institution or company underwrites an activity or underwrites the cost of it, they agree to provide any

money that is needed to cover losses or buy special equipment, often for an agreed-upon fee.

为…提供经济担保; 同意资助

Actually, “What is our business?” is almost always a difficult question and the right answer is

usually anything but obvious.

obvious：

明显的；显然的；易理解的

(to sb) (that…) easy to see or understand

A business is not defined by the company’s name, statutes, or articles of incorporation.

It is defined by the want the customer satisfies when she buys a product or a service.

To satisfy the customer is the mission and purpose of every business.

The question “What is our business?” can, therefore, be answered only by looking at the business

from the outside, from the point of view of the customer and the market.

What the customer sees, thinks, believes, and wants, at any given time, must be accepted by

management as an objective fact and must be taken as seriously as the reports of the salesperson,

the tests of the engineer, or the figures of the accountant.

accountant：

会计；会计师

a person whose job is to keep or check financial accounts

And management must make a conscious effort to get answers from the customer herself rather

than attempt to read her mind.

conscious ：

意识到；注意到

[ not before noun ] ~ of (doing) sth | ~ that aware of sth; noticing sth

ACTION POINT:

Talk to one customer every day this week.

Ask them how they see your company, what they think of it, what kind of company they believe it is

and what they want from it.

Use this feedback to better define your company’s mission.
