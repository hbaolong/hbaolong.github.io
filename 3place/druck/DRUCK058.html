Defining Business Purpose and Mission

What is our business?

Nothing may seem simpler or more obvious than to know what

Acompany’s business is. A steel mill makes steel; a railroad runs trains to carry

freight and passengers; an insurance company underwrites fire

risks; a bank lends money. Actually, “What is our business?” is almost

always a difficult question and the right answer is usually anything but

obvious.

A business is not defined by the company’s name, statutes, or articles of

incorporation. It is defined by the want the customer satisfies when she buys

a product or a service. To satisfy the customer is the mission and pur-pose of

every business. The question “What is our business?” can, there-fore, be

answered only by looking at the business from the outside, from the point of

view of the customer and the market. What the customer sees, thinks,

believes, and wants, at any given time, must be accepted by man-agement as

an objective fact and must be taken as seriously as the reports of the

salesperson, the tests of the engineer, or the figures of the accountant. And

management must make a conscious effort to get answers from the customer

herself rather than attempt to read her mind.

ACTION POINT:

Talk to one customer every day this week. Ask them how

they see your company, what they think of it, what kind of company they

believe it is and what they want from it. Use this feedback to better define

your company’s mission.

德鲁克阅读法分析步骤

1. 找出文本中的生词并提供中英文解释和例句：

  1. Business Purpose
    • 中文解释：企业的目的。
    • 英文解释：The reason for which a business exists and operates.
    • 例句：Understanding a company’s business purpose is essential for aligning its strategies with customer needs.
  2. Mission
    • 中文解释：使命。
    • 英文解释：A statement that defines the reason an organization exists and the goals it aims to achieve.
    • 例句：The company’s mission is to provide affordable and high-quality education to everyone.
  3. Customer
    • 中文解释：客户。
    • 英文解释：A person or organization that buys goods or services from a business.
    • 例句：The customer is always right, so we must prioritize their satisfaction.
  4. Satisfy
    • 中文解释：满足。
    • 英文解释：To fulfill a need, desire, or demand.
    • 例句：The primary goal of the business is to satisfy customer needs.
  5. Objective Fact
    • 中文解释：客观事实。
    • 英文解释：A fact that is not influenced by personal feelings or opinions.
    • 例句：The data collected from surveys are objective facts that can guide decision-making.
  6. Conscious Effort
    • 中文解释：有意识的努力。
    • 英文解释：A deliberate and intentional action or attempt to achieve a goal.
    • 例句：It requires a conscious effort to understand the customer’s perspective.
  7. Feedback
    • 中文解释：反馈。
    • 英文解释：Information or reactions provided about past events or actions.
    • 例句：Customer feedback is crucial for improving product quality.
  8. Define
    • 中文解释：定义。
    • 英文解释：To explain the meaning of something or describe its nature.
    • 例句：The company needs to clearly define its mission and values.
  9. Purpose
    • 中文解释：目的。
    • 英文解释：The reason for which something is done or created.
    • 例句：The purpose of this meeting is to discuss the new marketing strategy.
  10. Mission Statement
    • 中文解释：使命陈述。
    • 英文解释：A declaration that describes an organization’s purpose, goals, and values.
    • 例句：The company’s mission statement reflects its commitment to sustainability.

2. 用所有生词组成一个英文句子：

“Understanding the business purpose and mission of a company requires a deep understanding of its customers and their needs. The primary goal is to satisfy these needs, which are based on objective facts gathered through conscious efforts to seek feedback. By clearly defining its purpose and crafting a compelling mission statement, a company can align its strategies with customer expectations and achieve long-term success.”

5. 《论语》视角关联：

  1. 以客户为中心 《论语》中提到“己所不欲，勿施于人。”（《论语·颜渊》）这强调了尊重他人需求和感受的重要性。在管理学中，企业的使命和目的应以客户为中心，满足客户的需求，正如德鲁克所强调的。

  2. 持续改进与反馈 《论语》中“见贤思齐焉，见不贤而内自省也。”（《论语·里仁》）提醒人们要不断学习和反思。在管理学中，通过客户反馈不断改进产品和服务，体现了持续改进的精神。

  3. 明确的使命与目标 《论语》中“君子喻于义，小人喻于利。”（《论语·里仁》）强调了以义为先的重要性。在管理学中，明确的使命和目标不仅是追求利润，更是为了实现更高的社会价值。

通过以上分析，我们可以看到《论语》中的智慧与现代管理学中的原则有许多相通之处。领导者需要以客户为中心，持续改进，并明确企业的使命和目标，才能在激烈的市场竞争中取得成功。

透过金庸读懂德鲁克

客户想要什么？

有个朋友想卖课，也就是做知识付费，他是金融领域的，所以开课讲金融的报名课程，有保险，有信托，有基金，有养老，也有宏观经济。

我问他，你为啥要讲这么多呀。他说，金融人士对这些感兴趣呀。

我又问他，你一个人讲得了这么多么？他说，我可以请人呀。

说得好像很有道理。

过了几天，我去问他，课卖的怎么样？他说一般，几十个吧。他也做了免费的直播课，免费课也就300多人听，大家好像兴趣不是特别大。

然后我问他，你觉得你的客户是谁？

他很兴奋地说：“800万保险从业者，他们是最爱学习的”。

我就很好奇了，既然有800万个目标客户，为什么才来300多人呢？

他说：“我也不知道”。

我又问他：“你觉得你的客户想要什么呢？”

他说：“获客吧”。

然后我又问他：“你的课，能帮助业务员获客么？还有，这是你的想法？还是他们的想法？”

他说：“不知道”。

现在讲课的人很多，都快比上课的人多了，为什么有的课能卖上千万，有的课就算9.9，甚至免费，也没几个人听呢？

“我的客户群体是800万保险业务员，他们需要金融知识”。

这是他们的想法么？

“你有客户么？”

“你了解你的客户么？”

“你的客户需要什么？以什么形式需要？”

“他们为什么要找你来学？”

不要自己回答这些问题了，找到你的”客户”，问他们吧。
