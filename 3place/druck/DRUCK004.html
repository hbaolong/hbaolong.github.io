透过金庸读懂德鲁克

金庸笔下最厉害的招式

金庸的江湖中，武功多彩斑斓，大概梳理一下，有内功、剑术、刀法、下毒、暗器、拳脚、掌法、轻

功等等。

而作为一个武林高手，往往同时会多种武功，而且都达到一定的高度。

比如萧峰的降龙十八掌，打狗棒法；张无忌的乾坤大挪移，太极剑，太极拳；令狐冲的独孤九剑，吸

星大法和易筋经；杨过的玉女心经、黯然销魂掌。

这些武功，多的有几千招，如昆仑派和华山派联手的正反两仪刀法，共有4096招，天下武功繁杂，应

该这个是最了。

简单的也有，如峨眉派的“佛光普照”，就一招，运用“九阳功”为根基，直接一掌，死活就是它了。

在《倚天屠龙记》里，应该只有灭绝师太会这一招。因为这一招，其实比的是内功的高低，而不是招

式的精妙。

所以，用这招的人，做不了假，不像用“五大夫剑”，还可以装模作样地掐指而算。

那么，武功是招式多好？还是招式少好？

这个人因人而异。

降龙十八掌有十八掌，而逍遥游有三十六招。郭靖学降龙十八掌用了好几个月，而黄蓉学逍遥游只用

了不到2个时辰。所以，有的人适合学招数多的，有的人适合学招数少的。

一般来说，对于普通人，招数多一些比较好。在比武时，一方面可以使得眼花缭乱；一方面也可以让

自己充满自信。

毕竟，招数多的，有利于传承：纷繁复杂的招数，让学习的人充满蜜汁自信。感觉不再需要自己的努

力，就可以成为牛逼的人。

而招数少的武功。学习的人就知道，这个武功的使用，和自己的功力有直接的关系。

固定的招数也有一些毛病，就是会越传越少。比如泰山派就丢了很多剑法，青城派也是。后人不能理

解招式创建时的原理，一个劲的死记硬背，最终因为不能理解而忘记，因为忘记而更加不能理解，慢

慢地，这个门派也就式微了，甚至消失了。

即使招式不丢，也大多不懂招式之间的变化。这也是很多门派在创始人风光几年后就迅速小时的原

因。

有两种武功除外，一种是独孤九剑，一种是太极剑。

独孤九剑，本身就是一种剑意，是无招的招式，它没有固定的招式，而是根据对方的招式即时做出反

应。在各种场合下找出对方的弱点，让对手处于不断守护的转态，从而让自己不败，进而获胜。

所以，独孤九剑的核心不是剑法，而是发现对方的缺点。进而根据对方的缺点随时创造出新的招式出

来。

太极剑也一样，也是无招的，它只有一个原则，就是连绵不断，画圆成圈。你不断选择让自己最安全

的招式，使将出来。从来不败。

至于其他的招式，包括降龙十八掌，都在丢了几招后成为普通的掌法了，不再对别人有什么威胁。

这其实就是招式传承的风险以及应对方式：普通人学习招式，而聪明人学习剑意。只有学习了剑意的

人，才能做真正的传承，这一门武功才能传承下去。

至于反复锤炼的武功，例如龙抓手那样的，当然也有价值，不过，如果你不懂其中滋味，我怕你也不

能灵活应用，也难成一流高手。

Organizational Inertia

Inertia：

( usually disapproving ) lack of energy; lack of desire or ability to move or change

缺乏活力；惰性；保守

All organizations need a discipline that makes them face up to reality.

discipline：

[ U ] the practice of training people to obey rules and orders and punishing them if they do not; the controlled

behaviour or situation that results from this training

face up to：勇敢面对

训练；训导；纪律；风纪

All organizations need to know that virtually no program or activity will perform effectively for a

long time without modification and redesign.

virtually：

几乎；差不多；事实上；实际上

almost or very nearly, so that any slight difference is not important

Eventually every activity becomes obsolete.

obsolete：

no longer used because sth new has been invented SYN out of date

淘汰的；废弃的；过时的

Among organizations that ignore this fact, the worst offender is government.

offender：

犯罪者；违法者；罪犯

a person who commits a crime

Indeed, the inability to stop doing anything is the central disease of government and a major

reason why government today is sick.

inability：

无能；无力；不能

[ U sing. ] ~ (to do sth) the fact of not being able to do sth

Hospitals and universities are only a little better than government in getting rid of yesterday.

Businessmen are just as sentimental about yesterday as bureaucrats.

sentimental：

bureaucrats：

connected with your emotions, rather than reason

情感的（而非理性的）

N-COUNT Bureaucrats are officials who work in a large administrative system. You can refer to officials as

bureaucrats especially if you disapprove of them because they seem to follow rules and procedures too strictly.

官僚

They are just as likely to respond to the failure of a product or program by doubling the efforts

invested in it.

But they are, fortunately, unable to indulge freely in their predilections.

fortunately：

幸运地；交好运地；吉利地

indulge：

by good luck

in sth | ~ yourself (with sth) to allow yourself to have or do sth that you like, especially sth that is considered bad for you

沉湎，沉迷，沉溺（于…）

predilections：N-COUNT If you have a predilection for something, you have a strong liking for it. 爱好; 偏好

They stand under an objective discipline, the discipline of the market.

discipline：

[ U ] the practice of training people to obey rules and orders and punishing them if they do not; the controlled

behaviour or situation that results from this training

objective：

训练；训导；纪律；风纪

not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; considering only facts

客观的；就事论事的；不带个人感情的

They have an objective outside measurement, profitability.

profitability：n. 盈利能力；收益性；利益率

And so they are forced to slough off the unsuccessful and unproductive sooner or later.

slough：

蜕（皮）；使脱落

[ VN ] ~ sth (off) to lose a layer of dead skin, etc.

In other organizations—government, hospitals, the military, and so on—economics is only a

restraint.

restraint：

约束力；管制措施；制

约因素

[ C usually pl. ] ~ (on sb/sth) a rule, a fact, an idea, etc. that limits or controls what people can do

All organizations must be capable of change.

We need concepts and measurements that give to other kinds of organizations what the market

test and profitability yardstick give to business.

Those tests and yardsticks will be quite different.

yardsticks：

N-COUNT If you use someone or something as a yardstick, you use them as a standard for comparison when you

are judging other people or things.

准绳

ACTION POINT:

Make sure your nonprofit organization has rigorous tests and yardsticks to measure performance.

rigorous：

谨慎的；细致的；彻底的

done carefully and with a lot of attention to detail SYN thorough
