0001 bureau

Pronunciation

/ˈbjʊr.oʊ/ (BYOOR-oh)

Etymology & Root

  • French bureau “desk” ← bure “coarse woollen cloth” (originally desks covered with this cloth).

English-to-English explanation

A government department or agency; a chest of drawers.

Pronunciation

An official in a government department, often seen as overly concerned with procedure.

0004 discipline

Pronunciation

/ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪn/ (DIS-ə-plin)

Etymology & Root

  • Latin disciplina “teaching, learning”.

  • Root: disc- “learn”.

Collins English Dictionary definition

Training that produces obedience or self-control, often in the form of rules and punishments if these are broken.

Example:

All organizations need a discipline that makes them face up to reality.

English-to-English explanation

0019 virtually

Pronunciation

/ˈvɜːr.tʃu.ə.li/ (VUR-choo-ə-lee)

Etymology & Root

  • virtual + -ly ← Latin virtus “virtue, power”.

English-to-English explanation

Almost entirely; nearly; by means of computer simulation.

Example:

All organizations need to know that virtually no program or activity will perform effectively for a long time without modification and redesign.

0020 yardstick

Pronunciation

/ˈjɑːrd.stɪk/ (YARD-stik)

Etymology & Root

  • yard (measure) + stick.

English-to-English explanation

A standard used for comparison or judgment.

Example:

We need concepts and measurements that give to other kinds of organizations what the market test and profitability yardstick give to business.
