7 November The Failed Strategy

  1. Most of the people who persist in the wilderness leave nothing behind but bleached bones.

  2. When a strategy or an action doesn’t seem to be working, the rule is, “If at first you don’t succeed, try once more. Then do something else.”

  3. There is no joy in Heaven over empty churches,

  4. if you have no results, try a second time. Then look at it carefully and move on to something else.

  5. If at first you don’t succeed, sit down and ask what you have learned. Improve your approach and try once more. Maybe make a third effort. Then do something else.

8 November Strategic Planning

  1. Traditional planning asks: “What is most likely to happen?” Planning for uncertainty asks, instead: “What has already happened that will create the future?”

  2. Strategic planning is not a box of tricks, a bundle of techniques.

  3. It is analytical thinking and commitment of resources to action.

  4. It is the continuous process of making present entrepreneurial decisions systematically and with the greatest knowledge of their futurity, organizing systematically the efforts needed to carry out these decisions, and measuring the results of these decisions against the expectations through organized, systematic feedback.

  5. It is: “What do we have to do today to be ready for an uncertain tomorrow?”

  6. It is: “What futurity do we have to build into our present thinking and doing, what time spans do we have to consider, and how do we use this information to make a rational decision now?”

13 November Management by Objectives and Self-Control

  1. “Control” means the ability to direct oneself and one’s work.

  2. Objectives are the basis of “control” in the first sense; but they must never become the basis of “control” in the second, for this would defeat their purpose.

  3. within these limits every manager must be free to decide what he or she has to do.

14 November How to Use Objectives

  1. If objectives are only good intentions, they are worthless. They must degenerate into work. And work is always specific, always has—or should have—clear, unambiguous, measurable results, a deadline, and a specific assignment of accountability.

  2. objectives that become a straitjacket do harm. Objectives are always based on expectations. And expectations are, at best, informed guesses. The world does not stand still.

  3. no flight is ever operated without a schedule and flight plan.

  4. Objectives are not fate; they are direction. They are not commands; they are commitments.
