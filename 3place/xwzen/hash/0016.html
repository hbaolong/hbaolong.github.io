2025年12月28日

0001 adore

Pronunciation

/əˈdɔːr/ (uh-DOR) – stress on the second syllable.

Etymology & Root

  • Latin adorare “worship” ← ad- “to” + orare “speak/pray”.

  • Root: or- “mouth/prayer”.

English-to-English explanation

To love or like someone or something very much; to worship.

Example:

They adore figures, and that will please them.

0002 alight

Pronunciation

/əˈlaɪt/ (uh-LYT)

Etymology & Root

  • Old English ālīhtan “descend from horse/vehicle”.

  • Root: light- “descend”.

English-to-English explanation

To get down from a vehicle; to land (birds); glowing or lit up.

Example:

Having set their lamps alight, these would go off to sleep.

0003 ankle

Pronunciation

/ˈæŋ.kəl/ (ANG-kəl)

Etymology & Root

English-to-English explanation

The joint connecting the foot with the leg.

He twined himself around the little prince’s ankle, like a golden bracelet.

0004 assembly

Pronunciation
