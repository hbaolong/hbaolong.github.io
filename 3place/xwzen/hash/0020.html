0001 abyss

Pronunciation

/əˈbɪs/ (uh-BIS)

Etymology & Root

  • Greek abyssos “bottomless” ← a- “without” + byssos “bottom”.

English-to-English explanation

A very deep hole or chasm; a profound or immeasurable space.

Example:

I was holding him close in my arms as if he were a little child; and yet it seemed to me that he was rushing headlong toward an abyss from which I could do nothing to restrain him.

0002 amuse

Pronunciation

/əˈmjuːz/ (uh-MYOOZ)

Etymology & Root

  • Old French amuser “cause to muse/stare” ← a- + muse “muzzle/gaze”.

  • Root: muse- “stare/think”.

English-to-English explanation

To entertain or cause someone to laugh or smile.

Example:

I don’t amuse myself with balderdash.

0003 anniversary

Pronunciation

/ˌæn.ɪˈvɜːr.sər.i/ (an-i-VUR-sə-ree)

English-to-English explanation

The yearly return of a special date, especially a wedding or important event.

Example:

Tomorrow will be its anniversary.
