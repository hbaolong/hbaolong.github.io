《小王子》第五、六、七章单词学习

abruptly

Pronunciation
/əˈbrʌpt.li/ (uh-BRUPT-lee)

Etymology & Root
- From Latin abruptus “broken off” ← ab- “away” + rumpere “break”.
- Root: rupt- “break”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Suddenly and unexpectedly.

baobab

Pronunciation
/ˈbeɪ.əʊ.bæb/ (BAY-oh-bab)

Etymology & Root
- From Arabic bu hibab “fruit with many seeds”.
- Root: Arabic origin.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A bombacaceous tree, Adansonia digitata, native to Africa, that has a very thick trunk, large white flowers, and a gourdlike fruit with an edible pulp called monkey bread.

bore

Pronunciation
/bɔːr/ (BOR)

Etymology & Root
- Old English borian “pierce”; figurative sense from 1760s.
- Root: Germanic bur- “pierce”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
1. To cause (someone) to be weary through dullness or sameness.
2. A boring or tiresome person or thing.
3. To pierce or drill a hole in.

bush

Pronunciation
/bʊʃ/ (BOOSH)

Etymology & Root
- Old English busc “shrub”.
- Root: Germanic busk-.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A dense woody plant, smaller than a tree, with many branches arising from the lower part of the stem; shrub.

castle

Pronunciation
/ˈkɑː.səl/ (KAS-əl)

Etymology & Root
- Latin castellum “fortress”.
- Root: cast- “fort”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A fortified building or set of buildings, typically of the medieval period.

catastrophe

Pronunciation
/kəˈtæs.trə.fi/ (kə-TAS-trə-fee)

Etymology & Root
- Greek katastrophē “overturning”.
- Root: streph- “turn”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A sudden, extensive, or notable disaster or misfortune.

chance

Pronunciation
/tʃɑːns/ (CHANS)

Etymology & Root
- Old French cheance “fall of dice” ← Latin cadere “fall”.
- Root: cad- “fall”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
The unknown and unpredictable element that causes an event to result in a certain way rather than another; luck or fortune.

departure

Pronunciation
/dɪˈpɑː.tʃər/ (di-PAR-chər)

Etymology & Root
- Old French departir “divide” → “leave”.
- Root: part- “divide”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
The act or an instance of departing.

discipline

Pronunciation
/ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪn/ (DIS-ə-plin)

Etymology & Root
- Latin disciplina “teaching, learning”.
- Root: disc- “learn”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Training that produces obedience or self-control, often in the form of rules and punishments if these are broken.

distinguish

Pronunciation
/dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪʃ/ (di-STING-gwish)

Etymology & Root
- Latin distinguere “separate”.
- Root: stingu- “mark”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To make, show, or recognize a difference or differences (between or among); differentiate.

grave

Pronunciation
/ɡreɪv/ (GRAYV)

Etymology & Root
- Old English græf “dig” → “tomb”.
- Root: Germanic grab- “dig”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
1. A place for the burial of a corpse.
2. Serious or solemn.

herd

Pronunciation
/hɜːrd/ (HURD)

Etymology & Root
- Old English heord “group of animals”.
- Root: Germanic herdō.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A large group of mammals living and feeding together, esp a group of cattle, sheep, etc.

infest

Pronunciation
/ɪnˈfest/ (in-FEST)

Etymology & Root
- Latin infestare “attack”.
- Root: fest- “strike”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To inhabit or overrun in dangerously or unpleasantly large numbers.

inhabit

Pronunciation
/ɪnˈhæb.ɪt/ (in-HAB-it)

Etymology & Root
- Latin inhabitare “live in”.
- Root: hab- “dwell”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To live or dwell in; occupy.

inoffensive

Pronunciation
/ˌɪn.əˈfen.sɪv/ (in-ə-FEN-siv)

Etymology & Root
- in- + offensive ← Latin offendere “strike against”.
- Root: fend- “strike”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Not giving offence; unobjectionable.

magnificent

Pronunciation
/mæɡˈnɪf.ɪ.sənt/ (mag-NIF-i-sənt)

Etymology & Root
- Latin magnificus “great-doing”.
- Root: magn- “great”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Splendid or impressive in appearance; superb or very fine.

mental

Pronunciation
/ˈmen.təl/ (MEN-təl)

Etymology & Root
- Latin mentalis “of the mind”.
- Root: men- “think”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Of or involving the mind or an intellectual process.

moralist

Pronunciation
/ˈmɒr.əl.ɪst/ (MOR-ə-list)

Etymology & Root
- Moral + -ist ← Latin moralis “custom”.
- Root: mor- “custom”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Someone who has strong ideas about right and wrong behaviour, and who tries to make other people behave according to these ideas.

neglect

Pronunciation
/nɪˈɡlekt/ (ni-GLEKT)

Etymology & Root
- Latin neglegere “not pick up”.
- Root: leg- “gather”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To fail to give due care, attention, or time to.

oblige

Pronunciation
/əˈblaɪdʒ/ (uh-BLYJ)

Etymology & Root
- Latin obligare “bind to”.
- Root: lig- “bind”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To bind or constrain (someone to do something) by legal, moral, or physical means; to make indebted or grateful.

radish

Pronunciation
/ˈræd.ɪʃ/ (RAD-ish)

Etymology & Root
- Old English rædic ← Latin radix “root”.
- Root: rad- “root”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Any of various plants of the genus Raphanus, esp R. sativus of Europe and Asia, cultivated for its edible root.

resemble

Pronunciation
/rɪˈzem.bəl/ (ri-ZEM-bəl)

Etymology & Root
- Old French resembler ← Latin similis “like”.
- Root: simil- “like”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To possess some similarity to; be like.

reserve

Pronunciation
/rɪˈzɜːrv/ (ri-ZURV)

Etymology & Root
- Latin reservare “keep back”.
- Root: serv- “keep”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
To keep back or set aside, esp for future use or contingency; withhold.

rosebush

Pronunciation
/ˈroʊz.bʊʃ/ (ROHZ-bush)

Etymology & Root
- Rose + bush.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A rose plant growing as a bush rather than as a climber.

skirt

Pronunciation
/skɜːrt/ (SKURT)

Etymology & Root
- Old Norse skyrta “shirt”.
- Root: Germanic skurt- “short garment”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A garment hanging from the waist, worn chiefly by women and girls.

soil

Pronunciation
/sɔɪl/ (SOYL)

Etymology & Root
- Latin solum “ground”.
- Root: sol- “ground”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
The top layer of the land surface of the earth that is composed of disintegrated rock particles, humus, water, and air.

sprig

/sprɪɡ/ (SPRIG)

Etymology & Root
- Middle English sprigge “small twig”.
- Root: Germanic sprig- “twig”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
A shoot, twig, or sprout of a tree, shrub, etc; spray.

sprout

Pronunciation

- Root: Germanic sprūt- “shoot”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
(of a plant, seed, etc) to produce (new leaves, shoots, etc); to begin to grow or develop.

strict

Pronunciation
/strɪkt/ (STRIKT)

Etymology & Root
- Latin strictus “tight”.
- Root: strig- “tight”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
Adhering closely to specified rules, ordinances, etc.

tedious

Pronunciation
/ˈtiː.di.əs/ (TEE-dee-əs)

- Root: taed- “weary”.

Collins English Dictionary definition

timid

Pronunciation
/ˈtɪm.ɪd/ (TIM-id)

Etymology & Root
- Latin timidus “fearful”.
- Root: tim- “fear”.

Collins English Dictionary definition
