0001 absurd

Pronunciation

/əbˈsɜːrd/ (ab-SURD) – stress on the second syllable.

Etymology & Root

  • Latin absurdus “out of tune, senseless” ← ab- “away” + surdus “deaf, dull”.

  • Root: surd- “dull/deaf”.

English-to-English explanation

Ridiculously unreasonable, illogical, or inappropriate.

Example:

荒诞，荒诞的事物 （the absurd）

0002 accurate

Pronunciation

/ˈæk.jɚ.ət/ (AK-yər-ət)

Etymology & Root

  • Latin accuratus “done with care” ← ad- + cura “care”.

  • Root: cur- “care”.

English-to-English explanation

Correct in all details; exact.

Example:

I am concerned with matters of consequence: I am accurate.

0003 amuse

Pronunciation

/əˈmjuːz/ (uh-MYOOZ)

Etymology & Root

  • Old French amuser “cause to muse/stare” ← a- + muse “muzzle/gaze”.

  • Root: muse- “stare/think”.

English-to-English explanation

To entertain or cause someone to laugh or smile.

Example:

I don’t amuse myself with balderdash.

0004 ashamed

Example:

0005 balderdash

Pronunciation

/ˈbɑːl.dɚ.dæʃ/ (BAL-dər-dash)

Etymology & Root

  • 17th century, origin uncertain (possibly from “frothy liquid” → nonsense).

  • Root: Uncertain, likely imitative.

English-to-English explanation

Senseless talk or writing; nonsense.

Example:

I don’t amuse myself with balderdash.

0006 blest

Pronunciation

/blest/ (BLEST)

Etymology & Root

  • Archaic past of bless ← Old English blēdsian “mark with blood”.

  • Root: bled- “blood/bless”.

English-to-English explanation

Blessed; made holy or fortunate (archaic/poetic form of “blessed”).

Example:

What the little prince did not dare confess was that he was sorry most of all to leave this planet, because it was blest every day with 1440 sunsets!
