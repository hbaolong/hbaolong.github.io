  1. absorb

Pronunciation

/əbˈzɔːrb/ (uhb-ZORB) – stress on the second syllable.

Etymology & Root

  • Latin absorbere “swallow up” ← ab- “away” + sorbere “suck in”.

  • Root: sorb- “suck/drink”.

English-to-English explanation

To take in a liquid, gas, light, or energy; to fully engage someone’s attention or interest.

Example:

In Altadena, near Los Angeles, residents experienced a mudslide from flooding in a burn scar area that was less able to absorb water due to the wildfires that scorched the neighbourhood in January of this year, the BBC’s US partner CBS News reported.

  1. atmospheric

Pronunciation

/ˌæt.məsˈfer.ɪk/ (at-məs-FEER-ik) – stress on the third syllable.

Etymology & Root

  • Modern Latin atmosphericus ← Greek atmos “vapor” + sphaira “sphere”.

  • Root: atmo- “vapor” + spher- “sphere”.

English-to-English explanation

Relating to the atmosphere of the Earth or another planet; creating a particular mood or feeling.

Example:

Atmospheric rivers dumped heavy rains on large parts of California, causing floods and mudslides and leaving three dead as of Christmas night, according to local officials.
