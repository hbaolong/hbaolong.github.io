Yoko Ono (小野 洋子, born February 18, 1933, in Tokyo) is a Japanese multimedia artist, singer, songwriter, and peace activist who became world-famous both for her avant-garde art and as the wife and creative partner of John Lennon.

Quick profile

  • Full name: Yoko Ono Lennon

  • Age: 92 (as of 2025)

  • Background: Born into one of Japan’s wealthiest and most aristocratic families (her father was a banker whose family traced its lineage to a 9th-century emperor; her mother was from the Yasuda zaibatsu clan).

  • Education: Studied philosophy at Gakushūin University, then became the first woman admitted to study philosophy at Waseda University (she left after two semesters). Later moved to the U.S. and studied writing and poetry at Sarah Lawrence College.

  • lineage,英 [ˈlɪniɪdʒ] 美 [ˈlɪniɪdʒ] n. <正式>血统，世系

Main things she’s known for

  1. Avant-garde artist (1960s New York Fluxus movement)
    • Famous works: Cut Piece (1964) – she sat on stage while the audience cut away her clothing
    • Ceiling Painting (YES Painting) – the work John Lennon climbed a ladder to see at her 1966 London show; the word “YES” written tiny on the ceiling is what first made him like her
    • Bagism, instruction paintings, the “Grapefruit” book of conceptual instructions (1964)
  2. Relationship & creative partnership with John Lennon (1966–1980)
    • Met in November 1966 at her Indica Gallery show in London
    • Married March 20, 1969 in Gibraltar
    • Co-founded the Plastic Ono Band; co-wrote and recorded songs such as “Give Peace a Chance,” “Imagine” (she received co-writing credit in 2017), “Happy Xmas (War Is Over),” and many experimental tracks
    • Bed-Ins for Peace (1969), War Is Over! billboard campaign
    • Had one son together: Sean Taro Ono Lennon (born October 9, 1975)
  3. After John Lennon’s murder (December 8, 1980)
    • Became guardian of John’s legacy and estate (together with Sean)
    • Created the Strawberry Fields memorial in Central Park, the Imagine Peace Tower in Iceland (lit every year on John’s birthday), and continues global peace campaigns
    • Continued releasing music and exhibiting art well into her 80s and 90s
  4. Music career highlights
    • Multiple #1 hits on Billboard’s Hot Dance Club Songs chart between 2003–2017 (she holds the record for the oldest artist to reach #1 on that chart)
    • Collaborations with artists such as Lady Gaga, The Flaming Lips, tUnE-yArDs, etc.

Public perception

  • In the late 1960s–1970s she was heavily scapegoated by parts of the public and press as “the woman who broke up The Beatles” (a narrative that has largely been debunked by historians and the Beatles themselves in later years).
  • Today she is widely recognized as a pioneering feminist and conceptual artist, and her influence on Lennon’s later work (especially the move toward peace activism and experimental music) is acknowledged.
In short: Yoko Ono is a groundbreaking conceptual artist, a central figure in 20th-century avant-garde and peace movements, and, for better or worse, one of the most famous widows and collaborators in rock history.
