Scene 3

(Laertes and Ophelia enter a room in Polonius’s house.)

LAERTES: My bags are on board. Farewell.

And, sister, please write to me.

OPHELIA: Do you doubt that I would?

LAERTES: As for Hamlet, and his affections, Do not expect too much.

  • affection,英 [əˈfekʃn] 美 [əˈfekʃ(ə)n] n. 喜爱，关爱；爱恋，爱慕之情；精神状况，感情；疾患，病；影响

They are like violets in the spring,

  • violet,英 [ˈvaɪələt] 美 [ˈvaɪələt] n. 紫罗兰，堇菜；蓝紫色，紫罗兰色

Fast-growing and sweet, but not lasting, The perfume of a minute. No more.

  • perfume,英 [ˈpɜːfjuːm] 美 [pərˈfjuːm] n. 香水；芳香，香味，馨香；香精，香料

OPHELIA: No more than that?

LAERTES: No. Perhaps he loves you now.

But be careful. Remember his position.

His will is not his own.

Unlike other people, he may not do as he wishes.

The safety and well-being

Of this whole state depend on his choices. Therefore, he must first consider Denmark Before he can choose a wife.

If he says he loves you, keep all this in mind. If you lose your heart or your honor,

You might also lose your good reputation. Fear it, Ophelia. Fear it, my dear sister.

  • reputation,英 [ˌrepjuˈteɪʃ(ə)n] 美 [ˌrepjuˈteɪʃ(ə)n] n. 名誉，名声

Be careful of the danger of desire.

  • desire,英 [dɪˈzaɪə(r)] 美 [dɪˈzaɪər] n. 愿望，欲望，渴望；情欲，性欲

OPHELIA: I shall take your words to heart.

  • heart,英 [hɑːt] 美 [hɑːrt] n. 心，心脏；内心，感情；胸前；重点，核心；中心，中央；信心，勇气；（表示爱的）心形物；红桃牌，红心牌；菜心；同情，怜悯；爱情，情爱；（土地的）肥力

But, my good brother, do not show me The steep and thorny way to heaven

  • steep,英 [stiːp] 美 [stiːp] adj. （路、山等）陡峭的；（价格，需求）难以接受的，过高的；（增加或减少）急剧的，大幅度的

  • thorny,英 [ˈθɔːni] 美 [ˈθɔːrni] adj. 多刺的，带刺的；棘手的，麻烦的

If you don’t take your own advice.

(Polonius enters.)

POLONIUS: Still here, Laertes? Aboard! Aboard! The wind sits in the shoulder of your sail, And you are keeping everyone waiting.

  • wind,英 [wɪnd] 美 [wɪnd] n. 风，气流；风向，逼近的形势；呼吸，呼吸能力；肠胃气胀；管乐器，吹奏乐器；大话，空谈；放屁；弯，弯曲处；一圈，一转；（给钟表）上发条；（由风带来，表明附近有动物、人存在的）气味

(laying his hand on Laertes’s head): There!

My blessing on you! Here is some advice. Be friendly, but by no means vulgar.

  • vulgar,英 [ˈvʌlɡə(r)] 美 [ˈvʌlɡər] adj. 粗俗的，庸俗的，不雅的；（笑话、评论、动作等）下流的；（人）粗鲁的，无礼的；平民的，通俗语言的

Keep those friends you have, and

Tie them to your soul with hoops of steel. But do not give the hand of friendship

  • hoop,英 [huːps] n. （金属、木或类似材料制成的）箍，环（尤指用于箍桶或制框架）；圈形耳环（hoop earring）；（用于滚着玩的）滚圈；呼啦圈（hula hoop 的简称） 篮圈

  • steel,英 [stiːl] 美 [stiːl] n. 钢，钢铁；钢铁工业；（用来磨刀的）钢棒；钢制的，钢（铁）的；钢制器具；钢做的部分；<文>兵器；（钢铁般的）坚强，坚定

Too easily to every new person you meet. Give every man your ear, but few your voice. Listen to criticism, but do not judge others. Buy clothes as costly as you can afford— Good quality, but not gaudy—

  • criticism,英 [ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm] 美 [ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm] n. 批评，批判；意见；评论，评价；（对文学作品和历史文献的）考证

  • gaudy,英 [ˈɡɔːdi] 美 [ˈɡɔːdi] adj. 华而不实的；俗丽的

For a man’s clothing tells a lot about him. Neither a borrower nor a lender be,

For a loan can lose both itself and the friend, And borrowing dulls the edge of thrift.

  • loan,英 [ləʊn] 美 [loʊn] n. 贷款，借款；借用，借出

  • dull,英 [dʌl] 美 [dʌl] adj. 枯燥无聊的；无精打采的；不明亮的；（声音）不清晰的；阴沉的；不明显的，隐隐的；钝的；迟钝的，愚笨的；萧条的

  • thrift,英 [θrɪft] 美 [θrɪft] n. 节约，节俭；海石竹

  • edge,英 [edʒ] 美 [edʒ] n. 边，边缘；锋，刃；优势；影响力，奇特的品质；（声音里的）尖锐，怒气；（坏事的）边缘，临界点；棱

This above all: To your own self be true, And it must follow, as the night the day, You cannot then be false to any man.

Farewell. My blessings go with you!

LAERTES: I humbly take my leave, my lord.

  • humbly,英 [ˈhʌmbli] 美 [ˈhʌmbli] adv. 谦恭地，谦虚地

(to Ophelia): Farewell, Ophelia. Remember What I have said to you.

OPHELIA: It is locked in my memory,

And you yourself shall keep the key to it.

(Laertes exits.)

POLONIUS: What did he say to you, Ophelia?

OPHELIA: Something about the Lord Hamlet.

POLONIUS: I thought so.

I’m told that you and Hamlet

Have been spending much time alone lately. If this is so, I must tell you that you do not Understand what people might be saying. What is between you? Tell me the truth.

OPHELIA: My lord, he has let me know That he feels affection toward me.

  • affection,英 [əˈfekʃn] 美 [əˈfekʃ(ə)n] n. 喜爱，关爱；爱恋，爱慕之情；精神状况，感情；疾患，病；影响

POLONIUS: Affection? Ha! Are you fool enough To believe him?

OPHELIA: He has courted me honorably And has supported his words

  • court,英 [kɔːt] 美 [kɔːrt] n. 法院，法庭；球场；王宫，宫廷；朝臣；全体审判人员；庭院，院子；董事（会），理事（会）；（用于大型宅第和公寓街区的名称中）宅第，公寓大楼 v. 奉承，讨好；设法取得，追求；招致，引起（不愉快的事）；谈恋爱，求爱

  • honorably,英 [ˈɒnərəbli] 美 [ˈɑːnərəbli] adv. 体面地；值得尊敬地

With almost all the holy vows of heaven.

  • vow,英 [vaʊ] 美 [vaʊ] n. 誓约，诺言；（婚礼等上的）誓言 v. 立誓，发誓

POLONIUS: Such vows are like traps for birds.

  • trap,英 [træp] 美 [træp] n. （捕捉动物的）陷阱，网，夹子；圈套，诡计；伏击；牢笼，困境；<俚>嘴；（尤指旧时使用的）双轮轻便马车；（应该避免的）错误，问题；（赛狗开始时的）放狗隔栏；（高尔夫球场的）沙坑，凹地；容器，存贮装置；（水管）存水弯，U形管；抛靶器（将泥鸽等抛于空中以打靶）；<史>（射球戏用的）抛球器

Do not take him seriously. From now on,

You must not be so available. In fact,

Do not spend any time alone with him.

I don’t want you to even talk to him.

That’s an order. Change your ways.

OPHELIA: I shall obey, my lord.

(Polonius and Ophelia exit.)
