Scene 4

(Hamlet, Horatio, and Marcellus enter an area before the castle.)

HAMLET: The air seems to bite. It is very cold. What is the hour?

  • bite,英 [baɪt] 美 [baɪt] v. 咬，咬伤；叮，蜇；卡紧，咬住；产生不良影响；接受，相信；咬饵，上钩；（酸）腐蚀；<非正式>使生气，使烦恼；（物体）刺痛，勒痛；使伤心，使痛苦

HORATIO: I think it is almost 12.

MARCELLUS: No, it is past midnight.

HORATIO: Indeed? Then it is almost the time That the spirit has been coming to walk.

(The Ghost enters.)

HORATIO: Look, my lord, it comes!

HAMLET: May the angels defend us!

  • angel,英 [ˈeɪndʒl] 美 [ˈeɪndʒl] n. 天使；仁慈的人，善人；宝贝，乖乖；（尤指仁慈的）精灵，保护神；<非正式>仙波，雷达回响

  • defend,英 [dɪˈfend] 美 [dɪˈfend] v. 保卫，防御；捍卫，维护；（在比赛中）防守，防卫；参加卫冕赛，蝉联；做……的辩护律师，为……辩解

Whether you mean us evil or good,

I will speak to you. I’ll call you Hamlet, King, Father, Royal Dane.

  • royal,英 [ˈrɔɪəl] 美 [ˈrɔɪəl] adj. 国王的，女王的，王室的；（用于机构或组织名称）皇家的 ；盛大的，隆重的；<非正式>（用于强调某人或某物极度糟糕）极其的，极度的 n. <非正式>王室成员；（鸣钟术）敲奉十口钟的变换响

  • dane,英 [deɪn] 美 [deɪn] n. 丹麦人

Oh, answer me! Tell why your holy bones Have burst their burial clothes.

  • holy,英 [ˈhəʊli] 美 [ˈhoʊli] adj. 神圣的；圣洁的；<非正式>（表示惊讶、恐慌等）天啊，上帝啊；<非正式>用于强调

  • bone,英 [bəʊn] 美 [boʊn] n. 骨，骨头；骨质；基本结构，基本（bones）

  • burst,英 [bɜːst] 美 [bɜːrst] v. 爆炸，爆裂，胀开；冲，闯，突然出现；猛然打开；充满，满怀（be bursting with）；决堤；突然活跃起来；突然发生，突然发作；分页，断纸 n. 突发，迸发；爆裂，裂口；一阵短促的射击

Why has your tomb opened its marble jaws To cast you up again? What does this mean, That you rise up to visit in full armor?

  • tomb,英 [tuːm] 美 [tuːm] n. 坟墓，冢；土棺，石棺；墓碑；<文>死亡（the tomb）

  • marble,英 [ˈmɑːb(ə)l] 美 [ˈmɑːrb(ə)l] n. 大理石；大理石雕像；

  • jaws,英 [dʒɔːz] 美 [dʒɔːz] n. 口；狭口；咽喉

  • jaw,英 [dʒɔː] 美 [dʒɔː] n. 颌，颚；下巴；（机器的）钳口；危险的局面；口部；<非正式> 闲聊；<非正式>饶舌，喋喋不休；<非正式>闲聊

Say, why is this? What should we do? (The Ghost motions to Hamlet.)

  • motion,英 [ˈməʊʃn] 美 [ˈmoʊʃ(ə)n] v. 打手势，示意

HORATIO: It beckons you to draw nearer.

  • beckon,英 [ˈbekən] 美 [ˈbekən] v. （招手）示意，召唤；吸引，引诱；可能发生

  • draw,英 [drɔː] 美 [drɔː],（向特定方向）移动；

MARCELLUS: Do not go after it!

HORATIO: No, by no means.

  • by no means,绝不，决不，一点也不：用于强调某件事情不可能发生或不可能是真的。

HAMLET: It will not speak unless I follow it.

HORATIO: Do not, my lord.

HAMLET: Why, what should I fear?

My life is not worth the price of a pin.

  • pin,英 [pɪn] 美 [pɪn] n. （尤指做衣服时固定布料用的）别针，大头针；<美>胸针，饰针；徽章，像章；钉，楔，销，栓；

And for my soul, what can it do to that? It waves me forth again. I’ll follow it.

  • wave,英 [weɪv] 美 [weɪv] v. 挥手；挥手示意；挥舞，挥动；飘扬，摆动，摇晃；使（头发）略呈波形；（威胁地）举起（某物）

  • forth,英 [fɔːθ] 美 [fɔːrθ] 向前

HORATIO: What if it leads you toward danger Or into madness? Beware!

  • beware,英 [bɪˈweə(r)] 美 [bɪˈwer] v. 当心，提防

HAMLET: It waves to me still.

(to the Ghost): Go on. I’ll follow you.

MARCELLUS (holding him back): You shall not go, my lord.

HAMLET: Hold off your hands.

HORATIO: Listen to us. You shall not go.

HAMLET: My fate cries out,

  • fate,英 [feɪt] 美 [feɪt] n. 命运

(My fate cries out,)And makes each vein in this body As brave as a lion.

  • vein,英 [veɪn] 美 [veɪn] n. 静脉；（植物的）叶脉，（昆虫的）翅脉；（干酪、石头等的）纹理，纹路；矿脉，矿层，岩

  • brave,英 [breɪv] 美 [breɪv] adj. 勇敢的，无畏的；非常好的；华丽的，盛装的

(The Ghost beckons.)

  • beckon,英 [ˈbekən] 美 [ˈbekən] v. （招手）示意，召唤；吸引，引诱；可能发生

Let go, gentlemen.

(breaking free): I’ll make a ghost of anyone Who tries to stop me! I say, away!

  • breaking free,挣脱束缚：摆脱限制、束缚或困境，追求自由和独立。

(to the Ghost): Go on. I’ll follow you.

(The Ghost and Hamlet exit.)

MARCELLUS: Let us go after him.

HORATIO: What good will that do?

MARCELLUS: Something is rotten in the state of Denmark.

  • rotten,英 [ˈrɒt(ə)n] 美 [ˈrɑːt(ə)n] adj. 腐烂的，发臭的；（道德、社会或政治）腐败的，堕落的；

HORATIO: Heaven will take care of it!

MARCELLUS: No, let’s follow him.

(Horatio and Marcellus exit.)
