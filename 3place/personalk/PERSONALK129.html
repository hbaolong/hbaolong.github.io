They will earn their pay by occasionally denouncing some terrorists and accredit themselves with the revolutionary side by taking part in acts of violence against government officials. When this double-crossing has been practised for many years, as in the case of Malinovsky—who went on with it from 1902 until his execution in 1918—it is no longer possible to say, even with a complete knowledge of the facts, which side the man betrayed and which he served.
