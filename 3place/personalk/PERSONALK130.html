Tarski shows that by keeping the two languages apart, the Paradox of the Liar is avoided. We arrived at the same result by showing that if a factual assertion is made by a sentence p, ‘p is true’ is not a sentence. For the purpose of the present argument this result may be taken to be expressed by Tarski’s theorem that ‘p is true’ belongs to a different language than p—a language in which to every asserted sentence of the original language there corresponds the name of that sentence, i.e. the same sentence in quotation marks.
