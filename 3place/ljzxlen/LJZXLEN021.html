    4.11 The totality of true propositions is natural science in total (or the totality of the natural sciences).
什么是自然科学？

4.111 Philosophy is not one of the natural sciences.

    • 哲学不属于自然科学。

      (The word ‘philosophy’ must mean something that stands above or below, but not next to, the natural sciences.)

    • ’哲学’这个词意味着它和自然科学是有所区别的，而不是一样的。

4.112 The purpose of philosophy is the logical clarification of thoughts.

    • 哲学的目的是对思想进行逻辑上的清晰的表达。

  • clarification,英 [ˌklærəfɪˈkeɪʃn] 美 [ˌklærəfɪˈkeɪʃn] n. 澄清，阐明；净化

    Philosophy is not a set of teachings but an activity.

    • 哲学不是一整套的说教，而是一种活动。

      A philosophical work consists essentially of elucidations.

  • elucidation,英 [ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃn] 美 [ɪˌluːsɪˈdeɪʃn] n. 说明；阐明

    • 一部哲学著作主要由阐释组成

      Philosophy results not in ‘philosophical propositions’ but in propositions becoming clear.

    • 哲学的结果不是‘哲学的命题’，而是命题变得更加清晰。
哲学不是给你定义‘中医是什么’，而是引导你去思考什么是中医。
 Philosophy should make clear and delimit sharply thoughts that are otherwise, as it were, cloudy and blurred.
    • 哲学是用来把模糊不清的思想明确地搞清楚，并且划定界线的东西。

  • delimit,英 [diːˈlɪmɪt] 美 [diːˈlɪmɪt] vt. 划界；定界限

  • blur,英 [blɜː(r)] 美 [blɜːr] n. 模糊不清的事物；模糊的记忆，记不清的事情；污迹 v. （使）看不清楚，记不清；（使）难以区分；玷污

  • otherwise,英 [ˈʌðəwaɪz] 美 [ˈʌðərwaɪz] adv. 否则，不然；除此以外，在其他方面；不同地，另外地；以其他方式，用别的方法 adj. 不是那样的，另外情况下的

4.1121 Psychology is no more akin to philosophy than is any other natural science.Theory of knowledge is the philosophy of psychology.Does not my study of sign-language correspond to the study of thought processes, which philosophers considered so essential to philosophy of logic? Only they usually got entangled in inessential psychological investigations, and there is an analogous danger as well with my method.

    • 对于哲学来说，心理学并不比其他科学更亲近。认知论事心理学的哲学。我所研究的记号语言，并不对应思想行为的研究，就像很多哲学家认为的逻辑哲学那样。只是，他们经常陷入这些无关紧要的哲学研究。对于我的方法（逻辑哲学），也会同样的危险。

  • psychology,英 [saɪˈkɒlədʒi] 美 [saɪˈkɑːlədʒi] n. 心理学；心理特点，心理状态；心理影响；心计，善解人意

  • entangle,英 [ɪnˈtæŋɡ(ə)l] 美 [ɪnˈtæŋɡ(ə)l] vt. 使纠缠；卷入；使混乱

  • inessential,英 [ˌɪnɪˈsenʃl] 美 [ˌɪnɪˈsenʃl] adj. 无关紧要的 n. 无关紧要的东西

  • investigation,英 [ɪnˌvestɪˈɡeɪʃ(ə)n] 美 [ɪnˌvestɪˈɡeɪʃ(ə)n] n. 调查，审查；科学研究，学术研究

  • analogous,英 [əˈnæləɡəs] 美 [əˈnæləɡəs] adj. 相似的，类似的；（器官）同功的

4.1122 Darwinian theory has no more to do with philosophy than has any other hypothesis of natural science.

    • 像其他的自然科学的假说一样，达尔文理论对哲学没有任何意义。

  • darwinian,英 [dɑːˈwɪniən] 美 [dɑːrˈwɪniən] adj. 达尔文的；达尔文学说的 n. 达尔文主义者；进化论者

  • hypothesis,英 [haɪˈpɒθəsɪs] 美 [haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs] n. 假说，假设；（凭空的）猜想，猜测；前提

4.113 Philosophy limits the disputable realm of natural science.

    • 哲学限制了自然科学的讨论范围。
中医限制了自然科学的有效范围

  • disputable,英 [dɪˈspjuːtəb(ə)l] 美 [dɪˈspjuːtəb(ə)l] adj. 有争议的，有待讨论的

  • realm,英 [relm] 美 [relm] n. （知识、活动、思想的）领域，范围；<正式>王国；（动物学）界（对地球表面所作的最主要的生物地理划分）

4.114 It should delimit the thinkable and thereby the unthinkable.

    • 它（哲学）应该划定可以想象的领域，从而确定不可以想象的领域。

  • delimit,英 [diːˈlɪmɪt] 美 [diːˈlɪmɪt] vt. 划界；定界限

    It should limit the unthinkable from within through the thinkable.

    • 它（哲学）应该通过可想象的事物来限定不可现象的部分。

4.115 It will indicate the unsayable by clearly representing the sayable.

  • indicate,英 [ˈɪndɪkeɪt] 美 [ˈɪndɪkeɪt] v. 表明，标示；象征，暗示；间接提及，示意；指示，指出；<英>（用灯光或手臂）打行车转向信号；有必要，被建议

    • 它（哲学）会通过明确表达那些是可以说的，进而确定哪些是不可以说的。
哲学是在科学和神学之间进行了分类：可说的，不可说的。

4.116 Everything that can be thought at all can be thought clearly. Everything that can be stated can be stated clearly.

    • 所有可以被思考的，都可以被明确思考；所有可以被描述的，都可以被明确描述。

      4.12 A proposition can represent the whole of reality, but it cannot represent what it must have in common with reality to be able to represent it—the logical form.

    • 一个命题可以展示真个显示世界，但它不能展示它是如何展示这个世界的：也就是它的逻辑形式。

      To be able to represent the logical form, we would have to be able to position ourselves with a proposition outside logic, that is, outside the world.

    • 如果要展示逻辑形式，我们就必须让自己放在这个逻辑之外的命题中，也就是放在这个世界之外。
这个世界的上帝是‘逻辑’。

4.121 A proposition cannot represent the logical form; the logical form mirrors itself in the proposition.

    • 明天不能展示逻辑形式，逻辑形式通过命题来表达自己。

      What mirrors itself in language, language cannot represent.

    • 它通过语言来反映自己，语言却不能表达。

      What expresses itself in language we cannot express by means of language.

    • 它通过语言来反映自己，我们却不能用语言来表达它。

      A proposition shows the logical form of reality.

    • 命题现实现实的逻辑形式。它（命题）展示它（逻辑形式）的一些细节。

      It features it.

4.1211 A proposition ‘fa’ thus shows that the object a occurs in its sense, two propositions ‘fa’ and ‘ga’ show that they are both about the same object.If two propositions contradict one another, then their structure shows this; likewise, if one follows from the other. And so on.

    • 一个命题‘fa’显示了一个对象的一个发生，两个命题‘fa’和‘ga’显示了同一个对象的两个发生。如果，两个命题是相互矛盾的，它们的结构就会显示这个矛盾。当然，如果它们是相互依存的，它们的结构也会显示它们的互相依存。
  • contradict,英 [ˌkɒntrəˈdɪkt] 美 [ˌkɑːntrəˈdɪkt] v. 反驳，否认；相抵触，相矛盾；发生矛盾

4.1212 What can be shown cannot be said.

    • 那些被显示出来的，是数不出来的。

4.1213 Now we understand, too, our feeling that we are in possession of a correct logical conception once everything is in order in our sign-language.

    • 现在我们理解了，只有当所有的事情在我们的记号语言中是井然有序的时，我们才感觉我们拥有正确的逻辑概念。
  • possession,英 [pəˈzeʃ(ə)n] 美 [pəˈzeʃn] n. <正式> 拥有，持有；个人财产，所有物；持有违禁物，私藏毒品（或武器）；（对球的）控制，球权；进攻；领地，殖民地；鬼魂附体，着魔；（观点或情感的）支配，控制

4.122 We can talk in a certain sense of formal properties of objects and states-of-things, or of properties of the structure of facts, and in the same sense of formal relations and relations of structures.

    • 我们可以以一定的方式讨论对象的正式属性和事物状态，或者事实的结构状态的属性。在这里，形式关系和结构关系是一样的。

      (Instead of ‘property of a structure’ I also say ‘internal property’; instead of ‘relation of structures’ ‘internal relation’.

    • 我经常用‘内部属性’代替‘结构属性’，用‘内部关系’代替‘结构之间的关系’。

      I introduce these expressions to show the ground of the widespread confusion among philosophers between internal relations and real (external) relations.)

    • 我引入‘内部属性’、’内部关系’这些表达方式，是因为哲学家们普遍的那些在内部关系和现实（外部）关系之间困惑。

  • the ground of,……的基础/根据/理由：用于表示某事物的基础、根据或理由。

  • widespread,英 [ˈwaɪdspred] 美 [ˌwaɪdˈspred] adj. 普遍的，广泛的

    The obtaining of such internal properties and relations, however, cannot be asserted by means of propositions, but shows itself in the propositions that represent the relevant states-of-things and concern the relevant objects.

    • 这些内部属性和关系的获得，并不能被命题自己宣称，而是当命题表达相关的状态或者和相关的对象时的自我表达。

  • relevant,英 [ˈreləvənt] 美 [ˈreləvənt] adj. 有关的，切题的；正确的，适宜的；有价值的，有意义的

4.1221 An internal property of a fact we can also call a feature of the fact. (In the sense in which we speak, say, of facial features.)

    • 事实的内部属性，我们也可以称呼它为事实的特征。（我们也可以称呼为面部特征）

4.123 A property is internal if it is unthinkable that its object does not have it.

  • property,英 [ˈprɒpəti] 美 [ˈprɑːpərti] n. 所有物，财产；地产，房地产；房地产股票（或投资）（properties）；<法律> 所有权，处置权；特性，性质

    • 如果，我们不能想象一个对象不能没有某个特性，那么，这个特性就是这个对象内在的。

      (This blue colour and that one stand in the internal relation of lighter and darker eo ipso. It is unthinkable that these two objects should not stand in this relation.)

  • eo ipso,由此可见：表示通过某个事实或证据可以得出某个结论。

    (Here to the shifting use of the words ‘property’ and ‘relation’ there corresponds the shifting use of the word ‘object’.)

    • 在这里，’属性’和‘关系’两个词的变化用法对应着‘对象’用法的变化。

4.124 The obtaining of an internal property of a possible state of affairs is not expressed by a proposition, but expresses itself in the proposition that represents the state of affairs by an internal property of the proposition.

    • 对于一个事件中，一个属性的可能性中，哪些内在特性的获得，是不需要被命题表达的。而是在表达事件状态的命题中，被命题的内部特征所自然表达的。

      It would be just as nonsensical to ascribe a formal property to a proposition as to deny its ascription.

4.1241 Forms cannot be distinguished from one another by saying that one has this and another has that property, since this presupposes that it makes sense to predicate the two properties to the two forms.

4.125 The obtaining of an internal relation between possible states of affairs expresses itself linguistically by an internal relation between the propositions that represent them.

  • linguistical,英 [lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkəl] 美 [lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪkl] adj. 语言学的；语言学上的

    • 事件的状态之间的可能性之间的内部关系，在语言上，通过命题之间的内部关系来表达

4.1251 This resolves the dispute ‘whether all relations are internal or external’.

    • 这解决了‘是否所有的关系都是内部的，或者外部的’的争论。
  • dispute,英 [dɪˈspjuːt] 美 [dɪˈspjuːt] n. 争论，辩论，纠纷v. 对……提出质疑，否认；争论，辩论；争夺，竞争；<旧>阻止（着陆），抵抗（进攻）

4.1252 Series that are ordered by internal relations I call form-series.

    • 内部关系排序后的序列，我称之为：结构序列。

      The number series is ordered not by an external but by an internal relation.

    • 序列，是由内部而不是外部关系来排序的。

      Just as in the series of propositions‘aRb’,‘(∃x):aRx.xRb’,‘(∃x,y):aRx.xRy.yRb’,and so forth.(If b stands in one of these relations to a, then I call b a successor of a.)

  • successor,英 [səkˈsesə(r)] 美 [səkˈsesər] n. 继承者，接替的事物；（组织、机器等的）接替物

4.126 In the sense in which we speak of formal properties, we can also talk of formal concepts.

    • 我们讨论形式属性的方法，也可以讨论形式概念。

      (I introduce this expression to make clear the ground of the confusion of formal concepts with real concepts, which pervades the whole of the old logic.)

  • pervade,英 [pəˈveɪd] 美 [pərˈveɪd] vt. <正式>遍及，弥漫

    • 我引入这些表达式（形式属性、形式概念）是为了厘清这些被整个旧的逻辑弄得模糊的概念：形式概念和真实概念。

      That something falls under a formal concept, as one of its objects, cannot be expressed by a proposition. Rather, it shows itself in the sign for the object itself. (A name shows that it signifies an object, a numerical sign that it signifies a number, etc.)

      Formal concepts, unlike real concepts, cannot be represented by a function.

      For its marks, the formal properties, are not expressed by functions.

      The expression of a formal property is a feature of certain symbols.

      The sign of the marks of a formal concept is thus a characteristic feature of all symbols whose meanings fall under the concept.

      The expression of a formal concept is thus a propositional variable, in which only this characteristic feature is constant.

4.127 The propositional variable signifies the formal concept, and its values signify the objects that fall under the concept.

  • signify,英 [ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪ] 美 [ˈsɪɡnɪfaɪ] v. 意味着，象征；<正式> 表达，显示（感情、意愿等）；<正式> 要紧，有重要性；<美，非正式>（作为一种游戏或习俗，美国黑人之间）相互吹牛，相互辱骂

    • 命题的变量表达形式概念，它们的值表达概念下的对象。

4.1271 Every variable is the sign of a formal concept.For every variable represents a constant form that all its values have, and which can be taken as a formal property of these values.

4.1272 The variable name ‘x’ is thus the real sign of the pseudo-concept object.Wherever the word ‘object’ (‘thing’, ‘entity’, etc.) is correctly used, it is expressed in a concept-script by a variable name.For example, in the proposition ‘There are 2 objects which…’ by ‘(∃x,y)…’.Wherever it is used differently, that is, as a real concept-word, nonsensical pseudo-propositions arise.So one cannot say, for example, ‘There are objects’ as one might say ‘There are books’. Nor is ‘There are 100 objects’ or ‘There are ℵ0 objects’ any better.And it is nonsensical to speak of the number of all objects.The same applies to the words ‘complex’, ‘fact’, ‘function’, ‘number’, etc.They all signify formal concepts and are represented in a concept-script by variables, not by functions or classes (as Frege and Russell held).Expressions such as ‘1 is a number’, ‘there is only one zero’ and the like are nonsensical.(It is just as nonsensical to say ‘There is only one 1’ as it would be to say ‘2 + 2 at 3 o’clock equals 4’.)

4.12721 A formal concept is already given with an object that falls under it. So one cannot introduce as primitive ideas the objects of a formal concept and the formal concept itself. So one cannot, for example, introduce as primitive ideas the concept of a function as well as specific functions (as Russell does), or the concept of number and particular numbers.

4.1273 If we want to express the general proposition ‘b is a successor of a’ in a concept-script, then we need for this an expression for the general term of the form-series:

The general term of a form-series can only be expressed by a variable, since the concept ‘term of this form-series’ is a formal concept. (Frege and Russell overlooked this: the way in which they want to express general propositions such as the above is therefore false; it contains a vicious circle.)

We can determine the general term of a form-series by giving its first term and the general form of the operation that generates the next term from the preceding term.

4.1274 The question as to the existence of a formal concept is nonsensical. For no proposition can answer such a question.

(So, for example, one cannot ask: ‘Are there unanalysable subject–predicate propositions?’)

4.128 Logical forms are numberless.

    • 逻辑形式是无限的。

      That is why there are no pre-eminent numbers in logic and why there is no philosophical monism or dualism, etc.

  • eminent,英 [ˈemɪnənt] 美 [ˈemɪnənt] adj. （在某领域或职业中）卓越的，出众的；突出的，明显的

  • monism,英 [ˈmɒnɪzəm; ˈməʊnɪzəm] 美 [ˈmɑːnɪzəmˌˈmoʊnɪzəm] n. 一元论；一元发生说（等于 monogenesis）

  • dualism,英 [ˈdjuːəlɪzəm] 美 [ˈduːəlɪzəm] n. 二元论；双重性；二神教
