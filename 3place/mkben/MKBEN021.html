Act 3 Scene 1

running scene 10

Enter Banquo

BANQUO Thou hast it now: king, Cawdor, Glamis, all

As the weyard women promised, and I fear

Thou played’st most foully3 for’t. Yet it was said

It should not stand in thy posterity4,

But that myself should be the root and father

Of many kings. If there come truth from them —

As upon thee, Macbeth, their speeches shine7 —

Why, by the verities on thee made good8,

May they not be my oracles as well,

And set me up in hope? But hush, no more.

Sennet sounded. Enter Macbeth as King, Lady [Macbeth as Queen],

Lennox, Ross, Lords and Attendants

MACBETH Here’s our chief guest.

LADY MACBETH If he had been forgotten,

It had been as a gap in our great feast

And all-thing unbecoming.14

To Banquo

MACBETH Tonight we hold a solemn15 supper, sir,

And I’ll request your presence.

BANQUO Let your highness

Command upon18 me, to the which my duties

Are with a most indissoluble tie

Forever knit.20

MACBETH Ride you this afternoon?

BANQUO Ay, my good lord.

MACBETH We should have else23 desired your good advice —

Which still hath been both grave and prosperous24 —

In this day’s council25: but we’ll take tomorrow.

Is’t far you ride?

BANQUO As far, my lord, as will fill up the time

’Twixt this and supper: go not my horse the better28,

I must become a borrower of the night

For a dark hour or twain.30

MACBETH Fail not our feast.

BANQUO My lord, I will not.

MACBETH We hear our bloody cousins are bestowed33

In England and in Ireland, not confessing

Their cruel parricide35, filling their hearers

With strange invention36: but of that tomorrow,

When therewithal we shall have cause of state37

Craving us jointly. Hie you to horse. Adieu,

Till you return at night. Goes Fleance with you?

BANQUO Ay, my good lord. Our time does call upon’s.

MACBETH I wish your horses swift and sure of foot,

And so I do commend42 you to their backs. Farewell.

Exit Banquo

Let every man be master of his time

Till seven at night. To make society44

The sweeter welcome, we will keep ourself

Till supper-time alone: while46 then, God be with you!

Exeunt Lords. [Macbeth and a Servant remain]

Sirrah, a word with you. Attend those men47

Our pleasure?

SERVANT They are, my lord, without49 the palace gate.

MACBETH Bring them before us.

Exit Servant

To be thus51 is nothing, but to be safely thus:

Our fears in Banquo stick52 deep,

And in his royalty of nature reigns that

Which would be feared. ’Tis much he dares,

And to that dauntless temper55 of his mind,

He hath a wisdom that doth guide his valour

To act in safety. There is none but he

Whose being58 I do fear: and under him

My genius is rebuked59, as it is said

Mark Antony’s was by Caesar. He chid60 the sisters

When first they put the name of king upon me,

And bade them speak to him: then prophet-like

They hailed him father to a line of kings:

Upon my head they placed a fruitless64 crown,

And put a barren sceptre65 in my grip,

Thence to be wrenched with an unlineal66 hand,

No son of mine succeeding. If’t be so,

For Banquo’s issue have I filed68 my mind:

For them the gracious69 Duncan have I murdered:

Put rancours in the vessel70 of my peace

Only for them, and mine eternal jewel71

Given to the common enemy of man72

To make them kings: the seeds of Banquo kings.

Rather than so, come fate into the list74,

And champion me to th’utterance!75— Who’s there?

Enter Servant and two Murderers

To Servant

Now go to the door, and stay there till we call.—

Exit Servant

Was it not yesterday we spoke together?

MURDERERS It was, so please your highness.

MACBETH Well then, now have you considered of my79

speeches? Know that it was he in the times past which held80

you so under fortune, which you thought had been our

innocent self: this I made good to you in our last conference82,

passed in probation with you how you were borne in hand83,

how crossed, the instruments, who wrought84 with them, and

all things else that might to half a soul and to a notion85 crazed

say ‘Thus did Banquo.’

FIRST MURDERER You made it known to us.

MACBETH I did so, and went further, which is now our point of

second meeting. Do you find your patience so predominant

in your nature that you can let this go? Are you so gospelled90

o pray for this good man and for his issue92, whose heavy

hand hath bowed you to the grave and beggared yours92 for

ever?

FIRST MURDERER We are men, my liege.

MACBETH Ay, in the catalogue ye go for95 men,

As hounds and greyhounds, mongrels, spaniels, curs96,

Shoughs, water-rugs and demi-wolves are clept97

All by the name of dogs: the valued file98

Distinguishes the swift, the slow, the subtle99,

The housekeeper100, the hunter, every one

According to the gift which bounteous101 nature

Hath in him closed102, whereby he does receive

Particular addition from the bill103

That writes them all alike: and so of men.

Now, if you have a station in the file105,

Not i’th’worst rank106 of manhood, say’t,

And I will put that business in your bosoms

Whose execution108 takes your enemy off,

Grapples109 you to the heart and love of us,

Who wear our health but sickly in his life110,

Which in his death were perfect.111

SECOND MURDERER I am one, my liege,

Whom the vile blows and buffets113 of the world

Hath so incensed that I am reckless what

I do to spite the world.

FIRST MURDERER And I another,

So weary with disasters, tugged with117 fortune,

That I would set118 my life on any chance

To mend it or be rid on’t.119

MACBETH Both of you know Banquo was your enemy.

MURDERERS True, my lord.

MACBETH So is he mine, and in such bloody distance122

That every minute of his being thrusts123

Against my near’st of life124: and though I could

With barefaced power sweep him from my sight

And bid my will avouch it126, yet I must not,

For127 certain friends that are both his and mine,

Whose loves I may not drop, but wail128 his fall

Who I myself struck down. And thence it is

That I to your assistance do make love130,

Masking the business from the common eye

For sundry132 weighty reasons.

SECOND MURDERER We shall, my lord,

Perform what you command us.

FIRST MURDERER Though our lives—135

MACBETH Your spirits shine through you. Within this hour at most

I will advise137 you where to plant yourselves,

Acquaint you with the perfect spy o’th’time138,

The moment on’t, for’t must be done tonight,

And something from the palace, always thought140

That I require a clearness. And with him —

To leave no rubs nor botches142 in the work —

Fleance his son, that keeps him company,

Whose absence is no less material144 to me

Than is his father’s, must embrace the fate

Of that dark hour. Resolve yourselves apart146:

I’ll come to you anon.

MURDERERS We are resolved, my lord.

MACBETH I’ll call upon you straight: abide149 within.—

[Murderers may exit]

It is concluded. Banquo, thy soul’s flight,

If it find heaven, must find it out tonight.

Exeunt
