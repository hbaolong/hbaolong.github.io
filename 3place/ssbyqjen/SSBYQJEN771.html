TALBOT A maid, they say.

BEDFORD A maid? And be so martial?

BURGUNDY Pray God she prove not masculine 383 ere long,

If underneath the standard 384 of the French

She carry armour 385 as she hath begun.

TALBOT Well, let them practise and converse 386 with spirits.

God is our fortress, in whose conquering name

Let us resolve to scale their flinty 387 bulwarks.

BEDFORD Ascend, brave Talbot, we will follow thee.

TALBOT Not all together: better far, I guess,

That we do make our entrance several 388 ways:

That, if it chance the one of us do fail,

The other yet may rise against their force.

BEDFORD Agreed: I’ll to yond 389 corner.

BURGUNDY And I to this.

TALBOT And here will Talbot mount, or make his grave.

Now, Salisbury, for thee, and for the right

Of English Henry, shall this night appear

How much in duty I am bound to both.

SENTINELS Arm! Arm! The enemy doth make assault!

[English soldiers, having scaled the walls ] cry: ‘Saint George,’ ‘A Talbot.’
