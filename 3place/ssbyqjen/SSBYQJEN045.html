注释

1 根据对皇家版《莎士比亚全集》的统计，莎剧中散文比例高于诗的只有3部，依次为《快乐的温莎巧妇》、《无事生非》（Much Ado About Nothing , 70％）、《第十二夜》（Twelfth Night , 60％）；诗歌比例在70％以上的有28部；其余7部的诗歌比例介于70％—50％之间。

2 原文是“…the hero-villain of The Merry Wives of Windsor is a nameless impostor masquerading as the great Sir John Falstaff”，见其所著Shakespeare: The Invention of the Human (New York: Riverhead Books, 1998), p. 315。在这本厚达745页的书中，他只给了这出戏不到4页的篇幅。

3 David Crystal andandandand Ben Crystal, Shakespeare’s Word: A Glossary andandandand Language Companion (London: Penguin Books, 2002), p. 280.

4 原文：Hang him, dishonest varlet! We cannot misuse him enough.

We’ll leave a proof, by that which we will do,

Wives may be merry, and yet honest too.

We do not act that often jest and laugh,

’Tis old but true: still swine eat all the draff.
