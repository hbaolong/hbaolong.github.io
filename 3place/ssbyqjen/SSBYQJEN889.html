Scene 2 ／ running scene 2

Cornets 218 . Enter King Henry [VIII ], leaning on the Cardinal [Wolsey ]’s shoulder, the Nobles, [Wolsey’s Secretary ] and Sir Thomas Lovell: the Cardinal places himself under the King’s feet 219 on his right side

KING HENRY VIII My life itself, and the best heart 220 of it,

Thanks you for this great care: I stood i’th’level 221

Of a full-charged 222 confederacy 223 , and give thanks

To you that choked it. Let be called before us

That gentleman of Buckingham’s: in person

I’ll hear him his confessions justify 224 ,

And point by point the treasons of his master

He shall again relate.

A noise within crying ‘Room for the Queen, ushered by the Duke of Norfolk’. Enter the Queen [Katherine ], Norfolk and Suffolk: she kneels. [The ] King riseth from his state 225 , takes her up, kisses and placeth her by him

QUEEN KATHERINE Nay, we 226 must longer kneel: I am a suitor 227 .

KING HENRY VIII Arise, and take place 228 by us: half your suit

The Queen moves to his side

Never name to us: you have half our power:

The other moiety 229 ere you ask is given:

Repeat your will 230 and take it.

QUEEN KATHERINE Thank 231 your majesty.

That you would love yourself, and in that love

Not unconsidered leave your honour, nor

The dignity 232 of your office, is the point

Of my petition.

KING HENRY VIII Lady mine, proceed.

QUEEN KATHERINE I am solicited 233 , not by a few,

And those of true 234 condition, that your subjects

Are in great grievance 235 : there have been commissions 236

Sent down among ’em which hath flawed 237 the heart

Of all their loyalties: wherein, although,

My good lord cardinal, they vent reproaches

Most bitterly on you, as putter-on 238

Of these exactions 239 , yet the king our master —

Whose honour heaven shield from soil 240 — even he escapes not

Language unmannerly, yea, such which breaks

The sides of loyalty, and almost appears

In loud rebellion.

NORFOLK Not ‘almost appears’,

It doth appear: for, upon these taxations,

The clothiers 241 all, not able to maintain

The many to them longing 242 , have put off 243

The spinsters 244 , carders 245 , fullers 246 , weavers, who,

Unfit for other life 247 , compelled by hunger

And lack of other means, in desperate manner

Daring th’event to th’teeth 248 , are all in uproar,

And danger 249 serves 250 among them.

KING HENRY VIII Taxation?

Wherein, and what taxation? My lord cardinal,

You that are blamed for it alike with us,

Know you of this taxation?

CARDINAL WOLSEY Please you, sir,

I know but of a single part 251 in aught 252

Pertains to th’state, and front but in that file

Where others tell steps with me 253 .

QUEEN KATHERINE No, my lord?

You know no more than others? But you frame

Things that are known alike, which are not wholesome

To those which would not know them, and yet must

Perforce be their acquaintance 254 . These exactions,

Whereof my sovereign would have note 255 , they are

Most pestilent to th’hearing, and to bear 256 ’em

The back is sacrifice to th’load 257 . They say

They are devised by you, or else you suffer

Too hard an exclamation 258 .

KING HENRY VIII Still ‘exaction’:

The nature of it? In what kind 259 , let’s know,

Is this exaction?

QUEEN KATHERINE I am much too venturous 260

In tempting of 261 your patience, but am boldened

Under 262 your promised pardon. The subjects’ grief 263

Comes through commissions, which compels from each

The sixth part of his substance 264 , to be levied

Without delay, and the pretence 265 for this

Is named your wars in France: this makes bold mouths:

Tongues spit their duties out, and cold hearts freeze

Allegiance in them: their curses now

Live where their prayers 266 did: and it’s come to pass

This tractable 267 obedience is a slave

To each incensèd will 268 . I would 269 your highness

Would give it quick consideration, for

There is no primer baseness 270 .

KING HENRY VIII By my life,

This is against our pleasure 271 .

CARDINAL WOLSEY And for me,

I have no further gone in this than by

A single voice 272 , and that not passed 273 me but

By learnèd approbation 274 of the judges: if I am

Traduced 275 by ignorant tongues, which neither know

My faculties 276 nor person, yet will be

The chronicles of my doing, let me say

’Tis but the fate of place 277 , and the rough brake 278

That virtue must go through: we must not stint 279

Our necessary actions, in the fear

To cope 280 malicious censurers 281 , which ever,

As rav’nous fishes, do a vessel follow

That is new trimmed 282 , but benefit no further

Than vainly longing 283 . What we oft do best,

By sick interpreters, once weak ones, is

Not ours, or not allowed 284 : what worst, as oft,

Hitting a grosser quality, is cried up

For our best act 285 : if we shall stand still,

In 286 fear our motion 287 will be mocked or carped at 288 ,

We should take root here where we sit,

Or sit state-statues 289 only.

KING HENRY VIII Things done well,

And with a care, exempt themselves from fear:

Things done without example 290 , in their issue 291

Are to be feared. Have you a precedent

Of 292 this commission? I believe not any.

We must not rend our subjects from our laws,

And stick them in our will 293 . Sixth part of each?

A trembling 294 contribution; why, we take

From every tree lop 295 , bark, and part o’th’timber:

And though we leave it with a root, thus hacked

The air will drink the sap 296 . To every county

Where this is questioned 297 send our letters, with

Free pardon to each man that has denied

The force of this commission: pray, look to’t;

I put it to your care.

CARDINAL WOLSEY A word with you.

To the Secretary

Let there be letters writ to every shire,

Of the king’s grace 298 and pardon.— The grievèd commons 299

Aside to Secretary

Hardly conceive 300 of me. Let it be noised 301

That through our 302 intercession this revokement 303

And pardon comes: I shall anon 304 advise you

Further in the proceeding.

Exit Secretary

Enter Surveyor

QUEEN KATHERINE I am sorry that the Duke of Buckingham

To the King

Is run in 305 your displeasure.

KING HENRY VIII It grieves many:

The gentleman is learnèd, and a most rare 306 speaker,

To nature none more bound 307 : his training such

That he may furnish and instruct great teachers,

And never seek for aid out of 308 himself: yet see,

When these so noble benefits shall prove

Not well disposed 309 , the mind growing once corrupt,

They turn to vicious 310 forms, ten times more ugly

Than ever they were fair. This man so complete 311 ,

Who was enrolled ’mongst wonders — and when we,

Almost with ravished 312 listening, could not find

His hour of speech a minute — he, my lady,

Hath into monstrous 313 habits 314 put the graces

That once were his, and is become as black

As if besmeared in hell. Sit by us: you shall hear —

This was his gentleman in trust 315 — of him

Things to strike honour sad.— Bid him recount

To Wolsey

The fore-recited practices 316 , whereof

We cannot feel too little, hear too much 317 .

CARDINAL WOLSEY Stand forth, and with bold spirit relate what you

To the Surveyor

Most like a careful 318 subject have collected 319

Out of the Duke of Buckingham.

KING HENRY VIII Speak freely.

SURVEYOR First, it was usual with him — every day

It would infect his speech — that if the king

Should without issue 320 die, he’ll carry 321 it so

To make the sceptre 322 his. These very words

I’ve heard him utter to his son-in-law,

Lord Aberga’nny, to whom by oath he menaced 323

Revenge upon the cardinal.

CARDINAL WOLSEY Please your highness note

His dangerous conception 324 in this point,

Not friended by his wish to your high person 325 :

His will is most malignant, and it stretches

Beyond you to your friends 326 .

QUEEN KATHERINE My learnèd lord cardinal,

Deliver 327 all with charity.

KING HENRY VIII Speak on:

To the Surveyor

How grounded he his title to the crown

Upon our fail? 328 To this point 329 hast thou heard him

At any time speak aught?

SURVEYOR He was brought to this

By a vain prophecy of Nicholas Hopkins.

KING HENRY VIII What was that Hopkins?

SURVEYOR Sir, a Chartreux 330 friar,

His confessor, who fed him every minute

With words of sovereignty.

KING HENRY VIII How know’st thou this?

SURVEYOR Not long before your highness sped to France,

The Duke being at the Rose 331 , within the parish

St Lawrence Poultney 332 , did of me demand

What was the speech 333 among the Londoners

Concerning the French journey. I replied

Men feared the French would prove perfidious 334

To the king’s danger 335 : presently 336 the duke

Said ’twas the fear indeed and that he doubted 337

’Twould prove the verity of certain words

Spoke by a holy monk that oft, says he,

’Hath sent to me, wishing me to permit

John de la Car, my chaplain, a choice 338 hour

To hear from him a matter of some moment 339 :

Whom after under the confession’s seal

He solemnly had sworn that what he spoke

My chaplain to no creature living but

To me should utter, with demure confidence 340

This pausingly ensued: ’’Neither the king nor’s heirs,

Tell you the duke, shall prosper: bid him strive

To gain the love o’th’commonalty 341 : the duke

Shall govern England.’’’

QUEEN KATHERINE If I know you well,

You were the duke’s surveyor, and lost your office

On the complaint o’th’tenants: take good heed

You charge not in your spleen 342 a noble person

And spoil your nobler 343 soul: I say, take heed:

Yes, heartily beseech you.

KING HENRY VIII Let him on 344 :

Go forward.

To the Surveyor

SURVEYOR On my soul, I’ll speak but 345 truth.

I told my lord the duke, by th’devil’s illusions

The monk might be deceived, and that ’twas dangerous

For him to ruminate on this so far, until

It forged 346 him some design, which being believed,

It was much like to do 347 : he answered, ’Tush,

It can do me no damage’, adding further,

That had the king in his last sickness failed 348 ,

The cardinal’s and Sir Thomas Lovell’s heads

Should have gone off.

KING HENRY VIII Ha? What, so rank 349 ? Ah, ha!

There’s mischief in this man: canst thou say further?

SURVEYOR I can, my liege.

KING HENRY VIII Proceed.

SURVEYOR Being at Greenwich 350 ,

After your highness had reproved the duke

About Sir William Bulmer—

KING HENRY VIII I remember

Of such a time: being my sworn servant,

The duke retained him his 351 . But on: what hence?

SURVEYOR ‘If’, quoth he, ‘I for this had been committed 352’ —

As to the Tower, I thought, — ’I would have played

The part my father meant to act upon

Th’usurper Richard 353 , who, being at Salisbury 354 ,

Made suit 355 to come in’s presence: which if granted,

As he made semblance of his duty 356 , would

Have put his knife to him.’

KING HENRY VIII A giant traitor.

CARDINAL WOLSEY Now, madam, may his highness live in freedom,

And this man out of prison?

QUEEN KATHERINE God mend all 357 .

KING HENRY VIII There’s something more would out of thee: what say’st?

SURVEYOR After ‘the duke his father’, with ‘the knife’,

He stretched him 358 , and with one hand on his dagger,

Another spread on’s breast, mounting 359 his eyes,

He did discharge a horrible oath, whose tenor

Was, were he evil used 360 , he would outgo 361

His father by as much as a performance

Does an irresolute 362 purpose.

KING HENRY VIII There’s his period 363 :

To sheathe his knife in us: he is attached 364 :

Call him to present 365 trial: if he may

Find mercy in the law, ’tis his: if none,

Let him not seek’t of us: by day and night,

He’s traitor to th’height 366 .

Exeunt
