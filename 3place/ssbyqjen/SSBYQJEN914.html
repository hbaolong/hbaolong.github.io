Scene 2 411 / running scene 6

Enter Simonides with attendance, and Thaisa

SIMONIDES Are the knights ready to begin the triumph 412 ?

FIRST LORD They are, my liege,

And stay 413 your coming to present themselves.

SIMONIDES Return 414 them we are ready, and our daughter,

In honour of whose birth these triumphs are,

Sits here like beauty’s child, whom Nature gat 415

For men to see and, seeing, wonder at.

[Exit an Attendant ]

THAISA It pleaseth you, my royal father, to express

My commendations great 416 , whose merit’s less.

SIMONIDES It’s fit it should be so, for princes 417 are

A model 418 which heaven makes like to itself:

As jewels lose their glory if neglected,

So princes their renowns 419 if not respected.

’Tis now your honour 420 , daughter, to entertain 421

The labour of each knight in his device 422 .

THAISA Which to preservemine honour I’ll perform.

The First Knight passes by

His Attendant presents his shield to Thaisa

SIMONIDES Who is the first that doth prefer 423 himself?

THAISA A knight of Sparta 424 , my renownèd father,

And the device he bears upon his shield

Is a black Ethiop 425 reaching at the sun,

The word 426 : Lux tua vita mihi 427 .

SIMONIDES He loves you well that holds his life of 428 you.

The Second Knight

Passes by and his Attendant presents his shield to Thaisa

Who is the second that presents himself?

THAISA A prince of Macedon 429 , my royal father,

And the device he bears upon his shield

Is an armed knight that’s conquered by a lady.

The motto thus in Spanish 430 : Piùe per dolcezza che per forza 431 .

The Third Knight

Passes by and his Attendant presents his shield to Thaisa

SIMONIDES And with the third?

THAISA The third, of Antioch,

And his device a wreath of chivalry 432 .

The word: Me pompae provexit apex 433 .

The Fourth Knight

Passes by and his Attendant presents his shield to Thaisa

SIMONIDES What is the fourth?

THAISA A burning torch that’s turnèd upside down,

The word: Qui me alit me extinguit 434 .

SIMONIDES Which shows that beauty hath his power and will,

Which can as well inflame as it can kill.

The Fifth Knight

Passes by and his Attendant presents his shield to Thaisa

THAISA The fifth, an hand environèd 435 with clouds,

Holding out gold, that’s by the touchstone 436 tried 437 :

The motto thus: Sic spectanda fides 438 .

The Sixth Knight [Pericles ]

Passes by, wearing the rusty armour

He presents his own device 439 to Thaisa

SIMONIDES And what’s the sixth and last, the which the knight

Himself with such a graceful courtesy delivered?

THAISA He seems to be a stranger 440 , but his present 441 is

A withered branch, that’s only green at top.

The motto: In hac spe vivo 442 .

SIMONIDES A pretty moral.

From the dejected state wherein he is

He hopes by you his fortunes yet may flourish.

FIRST LORD He had need mean better than his outward show

Can any way speak in his just commend 443 :

For by his rusty outside he appears

To have practised more the whipstock 444 than the lance.

SECOND LORD He well may be a stranger, for he comes

To an honoured triumph strangely 445 furnishèd 446 .

THIRD LORD And on set purpose 447 let his armour rust

Until this day, to scour 448 it in the dust.

SIMONIDES Opinion’s but a fool that makes us scan

The outward habit for the inward man 449 .

But stay, the knights are coming —

We will withdraw into the gallery.

[Exeunt ]

Great shouts, and all cry ‘The mean 450 knight!’
