Scene 1 1 / running scene 1

Alarum 2 . Enter Plantagenet, [the Duke of York, ] Edward, Richard, Norfolk, Montague, Warwick [with white roses 3 in their hats, ] and Soldiers

WARWICK I wonder how the king escaped our hands.

YORK While we pursued the horsemen of the north,

He slyly stole away and left his men:

Whereat the great lord of Northumberland,

Whose warlike ears could never brook retreat 4 ,

Cheered up the drooping 5 army, and himself,

Lord Clifford and Lord Stafford, all abreast,

Charged our main battle’s 6 front, and breaking in,

Were by the swords of common soldiers slain.

EDWARD Lord Stafford’s father, Duke of Buckingham,

Is either slain or wounded dangerous 7 .

I cleft 8 his beaver 9 with a downright 10 blow.

That this is true, father, behold his blood.

MONTAGUE And, brother 11 , here’s the Earl of Wiltshire’s blood,

Whom I encountered 12 as the battles joined.

RICHARD Speak thou 13 for me and tell them what I did.

Shows Somerset’s head

YORK Richard hath best deserved of all my sons.—

But is your grace dead, my lord of Somerset?

NORFOLK Such hope have all the line of John of Gaunt 14 .

RICHARD Thus do I hope to shake King Henry’s head.

WARWICK And so do I.— Victorious Prince of York,

Before 15 I see thee seated in that throne

Which now the house of Lancaster usurps,

I vow by heaven these eyes shall never close.

This is the palace of the fearful 16 king,

And this the regal seat: possess 17 it, York,

For this is thine and not King Henry’s heirs’.

YORK Assist me, then, sweet Warwick, and I will,

For hither we have broken in by force.

NORFOLK We’ll all assist you, he that flies shall die.

YORK Thanks, gentle 18 Norfolk.— Stay by me, my lords,

And soldiers, stay and lodge by me this night.

They go up [to the throne ]

WARWICK And when the king comes, offer 19 him no violence,

Unless he seek to thrust you out perforce 20 .

YORK The queen this day here holds her parliament,

But little thinks we shall be of her council.

By words or blows here let us win our right.

RICHARD Armed as we are, let’s stay within this house.

WARWICK The bloody parliament shall this be called,

Unless Plantagenet, Duke of York, be king,

And bashful 21 Henry deposed, whose cowardice

Hath made us bywords 22 to our enemies.

YORK Then leave me not, my lords: be resolute.

I mean to take possession of my right.

WARWICK Neither the king, nor he that loves him best,

The proudest he that holds up 23 Lancaster,

Dares stir a wing, if Warwick shake his bells 24 .

I’ll plant 25 Plantagenet, root him up who dares.

Resolve thee, Richard: claim the English crown.

Flourish 26 . Enter King Henry, Clifford, Northumberland, Westmorland, Exeter [with red roses 27 in their hats, ] and the rest

KING HENRY VI My lords, look where the sturdy 28 rebel sits,

Even in the chair of state 29 : belike 30 he means,

Backed by the power of Warwick, that false peer 31 ,

To aspire unto the crown and reign as king.

Earl of Northumberland, he slew thy father,

And thine, Lord Clifford, and you both have vowed revenge

On him, his sons, his favourites and his friends.

NORTHUMBERLAND If I be not 32 , heavens be revenged on me!

CLIFFORD The hope thereof makes Clifford mourn in steel 33 .

WESTMORLAND What, shall we suffer 34 this? Let’s pluck him down.

My heart for anger burns. I cannot brook 35 it.

KING HENRY VI Be patient, gentle Earl of Westmorland.

CLIFFORD Patience is for poltroons 36 , such as he.

He durst not 37 sit there had your father lived.

My gracious lord, here in the parliament

Let us assail 38 the family of York.

NORTHUMBERLAND Well hast thou spoken, cousin 39 : be it so.

KING HENRY VI Ah, know you not the city 40 favours them,

And they have troops of soldiers at their beck 41 ?

WESTMORLAND But when the duke is slain, they’ll quickly fly.

KING HENRY VI Far be the thought of this from Henry’s heart,

To make a shambles 42 of the parliament house.—

Cousin of Exeter, frowns, words and threats

Shall be the war that Henry means to use.—

Thou factious 43 Duke of York, descend my throne,

And kneel for grace and mercy at my feet.

I am thy sovereign.

YORK I am thine.

EXETER For shame, come down. He made thee Duke of York 44 .

YORK It was my inheritance, as the earldom 45 was.

EXETER Thy father was a traitor to the crown 46 .

WARWICK Exeter, thou art a traitor to the crown

In following this usurping Henry.

CLIFFORD Whom should he follow but his natural king?

WARWICK True, Clifford, that’s Richard Duke of York.

KING HENRY VI And shall I stand, and thou sit in my throne?

YORK It must and shall be so. Content thyself 47 .

WARWICK Be Duke of Lancaster, let him be king.

To King Henry

WESTMORLAND He is both king and Duke of Lancaster,

And that the Lord of Westmorland shall maintain 48 .

WARWICK And Warwick shall disprove it. You forget

That we are those which chased you from the field 49

And slew your fathers, and with colours 50 spread

Marched through the city to the palace gates.

NORTHUMBERLAND Yes, Warwick, I remember it to my grief.

And by his 51 soul, thou and thy house shall rue it.

WESTMORLAND Plantagenet, of thee and these thy sons,

Thy kinsmen and thy friends, I’ll have more lives

Than drops of blood were in my father’s veins.

CLIFFORD Urge 52 it no more, lest that instead of words,

I send thee, Warwick, such a messenger 53

As shall revenge his death before I stir.

WARWICK Poor Clifford, how I scorn his worthless threats.

YORK Will you we 54 show our title to the crown?

If not, our swords shall plead 55 it in the field.

KING HENRY VI What title hast thou, traitor, to the crown?

Thy father was, as thou art, Duke of York 56 :

Thy grandfather, Roger Mortimer, Earl of March:

I am the son of Henry the Fifth,

Who made the dauphin 57 and the French to stoop 58

And seized upon their towns and provinces.

WARWICK Talk not of France, sith 59 thou hast lost it all.

KING HENRY VI The Lord Protector 60 lost it and not I:

When I was crowned, I was but nine months old.

RICHARD You are old enough now, and yet 61 , methinks, you lose.—

Father, tear the crown from the usurper’s head.

EDWARD Sweet father, do so, set it on your head.

MONTAGUE Good brother, as thou lov’st and honourest arms,

Let’s fight it out and not stand cavilling 62 thus.

RICHARD Sound drums and trumpets, and the king will fly.

YORK Sons, peace!

KING HENRY VI Peace, thou, and give King Henry leave 63 to speak.

WARWICK Plantagenet shall speak first: hear him, lords,

And be you silent and attentive too,

For he that interrupts him shall not live.

KING HENRY VI Think’st thou that I will leave my kingly throne,

Wherein my grandsire and my father sat?

No: first shall war unpeople 64 this my realm;

Ay, and their colours, often borne in France,

And now in England to our heart’s great sorrow,

Shall be my winding-sheet 65 . Why faint you 66 , lords?

My title’s good, and better far than his.

WARWICK Prove it, Henry, and thou shalt be king.

KING HENRY VI Henry the Fourth by conquest got the crown.

YORK ’Twas by rebellion against his king 67 .

KING HENRY VI I know not what to say, my title’s weak.—

Aside

Tell me, may not a king adopt an heir?

YORK What then?

KING HENRY VI An if 68 he may, then am I lawful king,

For Richard, in the view of many lords,

Resigned the crown to Henry the Fourth,

Whose heir my father was, and I am his.

YORK He rose against him, being 69 his sovereign,

And made him to resign his crown perforce.

WARWICK Suppose, my lords, he did it unconstrained 70 ,

Think you ’twere prejudicial to his crown 71 ?

EXETER No, for he could not so resign his crown,

But that the next heir 72 should succeed and reign.

KING HENRY VI Art thou against us, Duke of Exeter?

EXETER His 73 is the right, and therefore pardon me.

YORK Why whisper you, my lords, and answer not?

EXETER My conscience tells me he is lawful king.

KING HENRY VI All will revolt from me and turn to him.

Aside?

NORTHUMBERLAND Plantagenet, for all the claim thou lay’st,

To York

Think not that Henry shall be so deposed.

WARWICK Deposed he shall be, in despite 74 of all.

NORTHUMBERLAND Thou art deceived 75 . ’Tis not thy southern power 76 ,

Of Essex, Norfolk, Suffolk, nor of Kent,

Which makes thee thus presumptuous and proud,

Can set the duke up 77 in despite of me.

CLIFFORD King Henry, be thy title right or wrong,

Lord Clifford vows to fight in thy defence:

May that ground gape and swallow me alive,

Where I shall kneel to him that slew my father!

KING HENRY VI O Clifford, how thy words revive my heart!

YORK Henry of Lancaster, resign thy crown.

What mutter you, or what conspire you, lords?

WARWICK Do right unto this princely Duke of York,

Or I will fill the house with armèd men,

And over the chair of state, where now he sits,

Write up his title with usurping blood 78 .

He stamps with his foot and the Soldiers show themselves

KING HENRY VI My lord of Warwick, hear me but one word:

Let me for this my lifetime reign as king.

YORK Confirm the crown to me and to mine heirs,

And thou shalt reign in quiet while thou liv’st.

KING HENRY VI I am content. Richard Plantagenet,

Enjoy the kingdom after my decease.

CLIFFORD What wrong is this unto the prince your son!

WARWICK What good is this to England and himself!

WESTMORLAND Base 79 , fearful and despairing Henry!

CLIFFORD How hast thou injured both thyself and us!

WESTMORLAND I cannot stay to hear these articles 80 .

NORTHUMBERLAND Nor I.

CLIFFORD Come, cousin, let us tell the queen these news.

To Northumberland

WESTMORLAND Farewell, faint-hearted and degenerate king,

In whose cold 81 blood no spark of honour bides.

NORTHUMBERLAND Be thou a prey unto the House of York,

And die in bands 82 for this unmanly deed.

CLIFFORD In dreadful war mayst thou be overcome,

Or live in peace abandoned and despised.

[Exeunt Northumberland, Clifford and Westmorland ]

WARWICK Turn this way, Henry, and regard them not.

EXETER They seek revenge and therefore will not yield.

KING HENRY VI Ah, Exeter.

WARWICK Why should you sigh, my lord?

KING HENRY VI Not for myself, Lord Warwick, but my son,

Whom I unnaturally shall disinherit.

But be it as it may.— I here entail 83

To York

The crown to thee and to thine heirs for ever,

Conditionally 84 , that here thou take an oath

To cease this civil war, and, whilst I live,

To honour me as thy king and sovereign,

And neither by treason nor hostility

To seek to put me down 85 and reign thyself.

YORK This oath I willingly take and will perform.

WARWICK Long live King Henry!— Plantagenet embrace him.

KING HENRY VI And long live thou and these thy forward 86 sons!

YORK Now York and Lancaster are reconciled.

EXETER Accursed be he that seeks to make them foes.

Sennet 87 . Here they come down 88

YORK Farewell, my gracious lord, I’ll to my castle.

[Exeunt York, his sons and their Soldiers ]

WARWICK And I’ll keep 89 London with my soldiers.

[Exit ]

NORFOLK And I to Norfolk with my followers.

[Exit ]

MONTAGUE And I unto the sea 90 from whence I came.

[Exit ]

KING HENRY VI And I with grief and sorrow to the court.

Enter the Queen [Margaret, with Prince Edward ]

EXETER Here comes the queen, whose looks bewray 91 her anger.

I’ll steal away.

Starts to leave

KING HENRY VI Exeter, so will I.

QUEEN MARGARET Nay, go not from me, I will follow thee.

KING HENRY VI Be patient, gentle queen, and I will stay.

QUEEN MARGARET Who can be patient in such extremes?

Ah, wretched man, would 92 I had died a maid 93

And never seen thee, never borne thee son,

Seeing thou hast proved so unnatural a father.

Hath he deserved to lose his birthright thus?

Hadst thou but loved him half so well as I,

Or felt that pain 94 which I did for him once,

Or nourished him as I did with my blood 95 ,

Thou wouldst have left thy dearest heart-blood there,

Rather than have made that savage duke thine heir

And disinherited thine only son.

PRINCE EDWARD Father, you cannot disinherit me:

If you be king, why should not I succeed?

KING HENRY VI Pardon me, Margaret.— Pardon me, sweet son.

The Earl of Warwick and the duke enforced me.

QUEEN MARGARET Enforced thee? Art thou king, and wilt be forced?

I shame to hear thee speak. Ah, timorous wretch,

Thou hast undone 96 thyself, thy son and me,

And given unto the House of York such head 97

As thou shalt reign but by their sufferance 98 .

To entail him and his heirs unto the crown,

What is it, but to make thy sepulchre 99

And creep into it far before thy time?

Warwick is chancellor and the lord of Calais,

Stern Falconbridge commands the narrow seas 100 ,

The duke 101 is made protector of the realm,

And yet shalt thou be safe? Such safety finds

The trembling lamb environèd 102 with wolves.

Had I been there, which am a silly 103 woman,

The soldiers should have tossed 104 me on their pikes 105

Before I would have granted 106 to that act.

But thou preferr’st thy life before thine honour.

And seeing thou dost, I here divorce myself

Both from thy table, Henry, and thy bed 107 ,

Until that act of parliament be repealed

Whereby my son is disinherited.

The northern lords that have forsworn 108 thy colours

Will follow mine, if once they see them spread:

And spread they shall be, to thy foul disgrace

And utter ruin of the House of York.

Thus do I leave thee.— Come, son, let’s away.

Our army is ready; come, we’ll after them.

KING HENRY VI Stay, gentle Margaret, and hear me speak.

QUEEN MARGARET Thou hast spoke too much already. Get thee gone.

KING HENRY VI Gentle son Edward, thou wilt stay with me?

QUEEN MARGARET Ay, to be murdered by his enemies.

PRINCE EDWARD When I return with victory from the field

I’ll see your grace: till then I’ll follow her.

QUEEN MARGARET Come, son, away. We may not linger thus.

[Exeunt Queen Margaret and Prince Edward ]

KING HENRY VI Poor queen, how love to me and to her son

Hath made her break out into terms of rage.

Revenged may she be on that hateful duke,

Whose haughty spirit, wingèd with desire,

Will cost 109 my crown, and like an empty 110 eagle

Tire 111 on the flesh of me and of my son.

The loss of those three lords torments my heart:

I’ll write unto them and entreat them fair 112 .

Come, cousin you shall be the messenger.

EXETER And I, I hope, shall reconcile them all.

Flourish. Exeunt
