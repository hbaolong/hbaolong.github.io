Scene 4 ／ running scene 3

Enter Duke and Friar Thomas

DUKE No, holy father, throw away that thought 190 :

Believe not that the dribbling dart 191 of love

Can pierce a complete 192 bosom. Why I desire thee

To give me secret harbour 193 hath a purpose

More grave and wrinkled 194 than the aims and ends

Of burning youth.

FRIAR THOMAS May your grace speak of it?

DUKE My holy sir, none better knows than you

How I have ever loved the life removed 195 ,

And held in idle price 196 to haunt assemblies 197

Where youth and cost 198 and witless bravery keeps 199 .

I have delivered to Lord Angelo —

A man of stricture 200 and firm abstinence —

My absolute power and place here in Vienna,

And he supposes me travelled to Poland,

For so I have strewed 201 it in the common ear,

And so it is received. Now, pious sir,

You will demand 202 of me why I do this.

FRIAR THOMAS Gladly, my lord.

DUKE We have strict statutes and most biting laws,

The needful bits and curbs 203 to headstrong weeds 204 ,

Which for this fourteen years we have let slip 205 ,

Even like an o’ergrown 206 lion in a cave

That goes not out to prey. Now, as fond 207 fathers,

Having bound up the threat’ning twigs of birch 208 ,

Only to stick it in their children’s sight

For terror, not to use, in time the rod 209

Becomes more mocked than feared: so our decrees,

Dead to infliction, to themselves are dead 210 ,

And liberty plucks justice by the nose 211 ,

The baby beats the nurse, and quite athwart 212

Goes all decorum 213 .

FRIAR THOMAS It rested in your grace

To unloose this tied-up justice when you pleased:

And it in you more dreadful 214 would have seemed

Than in Lord Angelo.

DUKE I do fear, too dreadful.

Sith 215 ’twas my fault to give the people scope,

’Twould be my tyranny to strike and gall 216 them

For what I bid them do, for we bid this be done,

When evil deeds have their permissive pass 217

And not the punishment. Therefore indeed, my father,

I have on Angelo imposed the office 218 ,

Who may in th’ambush 219 of my name strike home 220 ,

And yet my nature never in the fight

To do in slander 221 . And to behold his sway 222 ,

I will, as ’twere a brother of your order 223 ,

Visit both prince 224 and people: therefore, I prithee,

Supply me with the habit 225 and instruct me

How I may formally 226 in person bear me 227

Like a true friar. More reasons for this action

At our more leisure shall I render you;

Only, this one: Lord Angelo is precise 228 ,

Stands at a guard with 229 envy 230 , scarce confesses

That his blood flows, or that his appetite

Is more to bread than stone 231 . Hence shall we see,

If power change purpose 232 , what our seemers be.

Exeunt
