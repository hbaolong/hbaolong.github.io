Scene 6 / running scene 17 continues

Alarum: excursions, wherein Talbot’s son [John ] is hemmed about [by French soldiers ], and Talbot rescues him

TALBOT Saint George and victory! Fight, soldiers, fight:

The Regent 1114 hath with Talbot broke his word

And left us to the rage of France his 1115 sword.

Where is John Talbot? Pause, and take thy breath:

I gave thee life and rescued thee from death.

JOHN O, twice my father, twice am I thy son:

The life thou gav’st me first was lost and done,

Till with thy warlike sword, despite of fate,

To my determined 1116 time thou gav’st new date 1117 .

TALBOT When from the dauphin’s crest 1118 thy sword struck fire,

It warmed thy father’s heart with proud desire

Of bold-faced victory. Then leaden age,

Quickened 1119 with youthful spleen 1120 and warlike rage,

Beat down Alençon, Orléans, Burgundy,

And from the pride 1121 of Gallia 1122 rescued thee.

The ireful 1123 bastard Orléans, that drew blood

From thee, my boy, and had the maidenhood 1124

Of thy first fight, I soon encountered,

And interchanging blows I quickly shed

Some of his bastard blood, and in disgrace 1125

Bespoke him thus: ’Contaminated, base

And misbegotten 1126 blood I spill of thine,

Mean 1127 and right poor, for that pure blood of mine

Which thou didst force from Talbot, my brave boy.’

Here, purposing 1128 the Bastard to destroy,

Came in strong rescue. Speak, thy father’s care:

Art thou not weary, John? How dost thou fare?

Wilt thou yet leave the battle, boy, and fly,

Now thou art sealed 1129 the son of chivalry?

Fly, to revenge my death when I am dead:

The help of one stands me in little stead.

O, too much folly is it, well I wot 1130 ,

To hazard all our lives in one small boat.

If I today die not with Frenchmen’s rage,

Tomorrow I shall die with mickle 1131 age.

By me they nothing gain, and if I stay

’Tis but the short’ning of my life one day.

In thee thy mother dies, our household’s name,

My death’s revenge, thy youth, and England’s fame:

All these and more we hazard by thy stay;

All these are saved if thou wilt fly away.

JOHN The sword of Orléans hath not made me smart 1132 :

These words of yours draw life-blood from my heart.

On that advantage 1133 , bought with such a shame,

To save a paltry life and slay bright fame,

Before young Talbot from old Talbot fly,

The 1134 coward horse that bears me fall and die:

And like 1135 me to the peasant boys of France,

To be shame’s scorn 1136 and subject of mischance 1137 .

Surely, by all the glory you have won,

An if I fly, I am not Talbot’s son.

Then talk no more of flight, it is no boot 1138 :

If son to Talbot, die at Talbot’s foot.

TALBOT Then follow thou thy desp’rate sire of Crete,

Thou Icarus 1139 : thy life to me is sweet:

If thou wilt fight, fight by thy father’s side,

And commendable proved, let’s die in pride 1140 .

Exeunt
