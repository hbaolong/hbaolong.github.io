Scene 3 556 / running scene 6

Enter Hotspur, solus 557 , reading a letter

HOTSPUR ‘But for mine own part, my lord, I could be well contented to be there, in respect of the love I bear your house 558 .’ He could be contented: why is he not, then? In respect of the love he bears our house. He shows in this, he loves his own barn better than he loves our house. Let me see some more. ‘The purpose you undertake is dangerous’ — why, that’s certain: ‘tis dangerous to take a cold, to sleep, to drink. But I tell you, my lord fool, out of this nettle, danger, we pluck this flower, safety. ’The purpose you undertake is dangerous, the friends you have named uncertain 559 , the time itself unsorted 560 and your whole plot too light for the counterpoise 561 of so great an opposition.’ Say you so, say you so? I say unto you again, you are a shallow cowardly hind 562 , and you lie. What a lack-brain 563 is this? I protest 564 , our plot is as good a plot as ever was laid; our friends true and constant: a good plot, good friends, and full of expectation 565 . An excellent plot, very good friends. What a frosty-spirited rogue is this? Why, my lord of York 566 commends the plot and the general course of the action. By this hand if I were now by this rascal, I could brain him with his lady’s fan 567 . Is there not my father, my uncle and myself, Lord Edmund Mortimer, my lord of York and Owen Glendower? Is there not besides the Douglas 568 ? Have I not all their letters to meet me in arms by the ninth of the next month? And are they not some of them set forward already? What a pagan rascal is this? An infidel! Ha, you shall see now in very sincerity of 569 fear and cold heart, will he to the king and lay open all our proceedings. O, I could divide myself and go to buffets 570 , for moving 571 such a dish of skimmed milk 572 with so honourable an action 573 ! Hang him. Let him tell the king we are prepared 574 . I will set forwards tonight.

I will not love myself. Do you not love me?

Nay, tell me if thou speak’st in jest or no.

HOTSPUR Come, wilt thou see me ride?

And when I am a-horseback, I will swear

This evening must I leave thee, gentle Kate.

But yet a woman: and for secrecy,

HOTSPUR Not an inch further. But hark you, Kate:

Whither I go, thither shall you go too.
