Scene 4 525 / running scene 8

Enter Duke Humphrey [Gloucester ] and his Men in mourning cloaks

GLOUCESTER Thus sometimes hath the brightest day a cloud:

And after summer evermore succeeds 526

Barren winter, with his wrathful nipping cold:

So cares and joys abound, as seasons fleet 527 .

Sirs, what’s o’clock?

SERVANT Ten, my lord.

GLOUCESTER Ten is the hour that was appointed me

To watch the coming of my punished duchess:

Uneath 528 may she endure the flinty 529 streets,

To tread them with her tender-feeling feet.

Sweet Nell, ill can thy noble mind abrook 530

The abject 531 people gazing on thy face,

With envious 532 looks laughing at thy shame,

That erst 533 did follow thy proud 534 chariot-wheels

When thou didst ride in triumph 535 through the streets.

But soft, I think she comes: and I’ll prepare

My tear-stained eyes to see her miseries.

Enter the Duchess [Eleanor barefoot ] in a white sheet, [with verses pinned on her back 536 ] and a taper 537 burning in her hand, with [Sir John Stanley, ] the Sheriff and Officers

SERVANT So please your grace, we’ll take her from the sheriff.

GLOUCESTER No, stir not for your lives, let her pass by.

ELEANOR Come you, my lord, to see my open shame?

Now thou dost penance too. Look how they gaze,

See how the giddy 538 multitude do point,

And nod their heads, and throw 539 their eyes on thee.

Ah, Gloucester, hide thee from their hateful 540 looks,

And, in thy closet 541 pent up, rue 542 my shame,

And ban 543 thine enemies, both mine and thine.

GLOUCESTER Be patient, gentle Nell: forget this grief.

ELEANOR Ah, Gloucester, teach me to forget myself,

For whilst I think I am thy married wife

And thou a prince, Protector of this land,

Methinks I should not thus be led along,

Mailed up 544 in shame, with papers on my back,

And followed with a rabble that rejoice

To see my tears and hear my deep-fet 545 groans.

The ruthless flint doth cut my tender feet,

And when I start 546 , the envious people laugh

And bid me be advisèd 547 how I tread.

Ah, Humphrey, can I bear this shameful yoke?

Trowest thou 548 that e’er I’ll look upon the world,

Or count them happy that enjoys the sun?

No: dark shall be my light, and night my day.

To think upon my pomp 549 shall be my hell.

Sometime I’ll say, I am Duke Humphrey’s wife,

And he a prince and ruler of the land:

Yet so he ruled and such a prince he was,

As 550 he stood by, whilst I, his forlorn duchess,

Was made a wonder 551 and a pointing-stock 552

To every idle 553 rascal 554 follower.

But be thou mild 555 and blush not at my shame,

Nor stir at nothing till the axe of death

Hang over thee, as sure it shortly will.

For Suffolk, he that can do all in all

With her 556 that hateth thee and hates us all,

And York and impious Beaufort, that false priest,

Have all limed 557 bushes to betray thy wings,

And fly thou how thou canst 558 , they’ll tangle thee.

But fear not thou, until thy foot be snared,

Nor never seek prevention of thy foes.

GLOUCESTER Ah, Nell, forbear 559 : thou aimest 560 all awry 561 .

I must offend before I be attainted 562 :

And had I twenty times so many foes,

And each of them had twenty times their power,

All these could not procure me any scathe 563 ,

So long as I am loyal, true and crimeless.

Wouldst have me rescue thee from this reproach?

Why yet thy scandal were not 564 wiped away

But I in danger for the breach of law.

Thy greatest help is quiet 565 , gentle Nell:

I pray thee sort 566 thy heart to patience,

These few days’ wonder 567 will be quickly worn 568 .

Enter a Herald

HERALD I summon your grace to his majesty’s parliament,

Holden 569 at Bury 570 the first of this next month.

GLOUCESTER And my consent ne’er asked herein before?

This is close dealing 571 . Well, I will be there.—

[Exit Herald ]

My Nell, I take my leave: and, Master Sheriff,

Let not her penance exceed the king’s commission 572 .

SHERIFF An’t please your grace, here my commission stays 573 ,

And Sir John Stanley is appointed now

To take her with him to the Isle of Man.

GLOUCESTER Must you, Sir John, protect 574 my lady here?

STANLEY So am I given in charge, may’t please your grace.

GLOUCESTER Entreat 575 her not the worse in that I pray

You use her well: the world may laugh again,

And I may live to do you kindness if

You do it her. And so, Sir John, farewell.

He begins to leave

ELEANOR What, gone, my lord, and bid me not farewell?

GLOUCESTER Witness my tears, I cannot stay to speak.

Exeunt Gloucester [and his Men ]

ELEANOR Art thou gone too? All comfort go with thee,

For none abides with me: my joy is death:

Death, at whose name I oft have been afeared 576 ,

Because I wished this world’s eternity 577 .

Stanley, I prithee go, and take me hence:

I care not whither, for I beg no favour,

Only convey me where thou art commanded.

STANLEY Why, madam, that is to the Isle of Man,

There to be used 578 according to your state 579 .

ELEANOR That’s bad enough, for I am but reproach 580 :

And shall I then be used reproachfully?

STANLEY Like to a duchess, and Duke Humphrey’s lady:

According to that state you shall be used.

ELEANOR Sheriff, farewell, and better than I fare 581 ,

Although thou hast been conduct 582 of my shame.

SHERIFF It is my office 583 ; and, madam, pardon me.

ELEANOR Ay, ay, farewell, thy office is discharged 584 :

Come, Stanley, shall we go?

STANLEY Madam, your penance done, throw off this sheet,

And go we to attire 585 you for our journey.

ELEANOR My shame will not be shifted 586 with my sheet:

No, it will hang upon my richest robes

And show itself, attire me how I can.

Go, lead the way: I long to see my prison

Exeunt
