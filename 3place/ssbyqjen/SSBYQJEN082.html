Enter Provost [and a ] Servant

SERVANT He’s 417 hearing of a cause: he will come straight,

I’ll tell him of you.

PROVOST Pray you, do.— I’ll know

[Exit Servant ]

His pleasure 418 , maybe he will relent. Alas,

He 419 hath but as offended 420 in a dream!

All sects 421 , all ages smack 422 of this vice, and he

To die for’t?

Enter Angelo

ANGELO Now, what’s the matter, provost?

PROVOST Is it your will Claudio shall die tomorrow?

ANGELO Did not I tell thee yea? Hadst thou not order?

Why dost thou ask again?

PROVOST Lest 423 I might be too rash.

Under your good correction 424 , I have seen

When, after execution, judgement hath

Repented o’er his doom 425 .

ANGELO Go to, let that be mine 426 .

Do you your office, or give up your place,

And you shall well be spared 427 .

PROVOST I crave your honour’s pardon.

What shall be done, sir, with the groaning 428 Juliet?

She’s very near her hour 429 .

ANGELO Dispose of 430 her

To some more fitter place, and that with speed.

[Enter Servant ]

SERVANT Here is the sister of the man condemned

Desires access to you.

ANGELO Hath he a sister?

PROVOST Ay, my good lord, a very virtuous maid,

And to be shortly of a sisterhood 431 ,

If not already.

ANGELO Well, let her be admitted.

[Exit Servant ]

See you the fornicatress 432 be removed.

Let her have needful but not lavish means.

There shall be order 433 for’t.

Enter Lucio and Isabella

PROVOST ’Save your honour!

ANGELO Stay a little while.—

To Provost

You’re welcome: what’s your will?

To Isabella

ISABELLA I am a woeful suitor 434 to your honour,

Please but your honour hear me.

ANGELO Well, what’s your suit?

ISABELLA There is a vice that most I do abhor,

And most desire should meet the blow of justice,

For which I would not plead, but that I must,

For which I must not plead, but that I am

At war ’twixt will and will not 435 .

ANGELO Well, the matter 436 ?

ISABELLA I have a brother is condemned to die:

I do beseech you, let it be his fault,

And not my brother 437 .

PROVOST Heaven give thee moving 438 graces!

Aside

ANGELO Condemn the fault and not the actor of it?

Why, every fault’s condemned ere it be done 439 :

Mine were the very cipher of a function 440 ,

To fine 441 the faults whose fine stands in record 442 ,

And let go by 443 the actor.

ISABELLA O, just but severe law!

I had a brother, then. Heaven keep your honour.

LUCIO Give’t not o’er so 444 . To him again, entreat him,

Aside to Isabella

Kneel down before him, hang upon his gown.

You are too cold. If you should need a pin 445 ,

You could not with more tame a tongue desire it.

To him, I say!

ISABELLA Must he needs die?

To Angelo

ANGELO Maiden, no remedy.

ISABELLA Yes, I do think that you might pardon him,

And neither heaven nor man grieve at the mercy.

ANGELO I will not do’t.

ISABELLA But can you, if you would 446 ?

ANGELO Look what 447 I will not, that I cannot do.

ISABELLA But might you do’t, and do the world no wrong,

If so your heart were touched with that remorse 448

As mine is to him?

ANGELO He’s sentenced, ’tis too late.

LUCIO You are too cold.

Aside to Isabella

ISABELLA Too late? Why, no, I that do speak a word

May call it back again. Well, believe this:

No ceremony that to great ones ’longs 449 ,

Not the king’s crown, nor the deputed sword 450 ,

The marshal 451 ’s truncheon 452 , nor the judge’s robe,

Become 453 them with one half so good a grace 454

As mercy does.

If he had been as you 455 and you as he,

You would have slipped like him, but he, like you,

Would not have been so stern.

ANGELO Pray you, be gone.

ISABELLA I would to heaven I had your potency 456 ,

And you were Isabel. Should it then be thus?

No, I would tell 457 what ’twere to be a judge,

And what a prisoner.

LUCIO Ay, touch him, there’s the vein 458 .

Aside to Isabella

ANGELO Your brother is a forfeit of 459 the law,

And you but waste your words.

ISABELLA Alas, alas!

Why, all the souls that were 460 were forfeit once,

And he 461 that might the vantage 462 best have took

Found out the remedy. How would you be,

If he, which is the top of judgement, should

But judge you as you are? O, think on that,

And mercy then will breathe within your lips

Like man new made 463 .

ANGELO Be you content, fair maid,

It is the law, not I, condemn your brother.

Were he my kinsman, brother, or my son,

It should be thus with him. He must die tomorrow.

ISABELLA Tomorrow? O, that’s sudden! Spare him, spare him!

He’s not prepared for death. Even for our kitchens

We kill the fowl of season 464 : shall we serve heaven

With less respect than we do minister 465

To our gross 466 selves? Good, good my lord, bethink you 467 ;

Who is it that hath died for this offence?

There’s many have committed it.

LUCIO Ay, well said.

Aside to Isabella

ANGELO The law hath not been dead, though it hath slept:

Those many had not dared to do that evil,

If the first that did th’edict infringe

Had answered for his deed 468 . Now ’tis awake,

Takes note of what is done, and, like a prophet,

Looks in a glass 469 that shows what future evils,

Either now 470 , or by remissness new-conceived 471 ,

And so in progress to be hatched and born 472 ,

Are now to have no successive degrees 473 ,

But ere 474 they live to end 475 .

ISABELLA Yet show some pity.

ANGELO I show it most of all when I show justice:

For then I pity those I do not know,

Which a dismissed 476 offence would after gall 477 ,

And do him right that, answering one foul wrong,

Lives not to act another. Be satisfied;

Your brother dies tomorrow; be content.

ISABELLA So you must be the first that gives this sentence,

And he, that suffers. O, it is excellent

To have a giant’s strength, but it is tyrannous

To use it like a giant.

LUCIO That’s well said.

Aside to Isabella

ISABELLA Could great men thunder

As Jove 478 himself does, Jove would ne’er be quiet 479 ,

For every pelting 480 , petty officer

Would use his heaven for thunder,

Nothing but thunder! Merciful heaven,

Thou rather with thy sharp and sulphurous bolt 481

Splits the unwedgeable 482 and gnarlèd oak 483

Than the soft myrtle 484 : but man, proud man,

Dressed in a little brief 485 authority,

Most ignorant of what he’s most assured 486 —

His glassy 487 essence — like an angry ape

Plays such fantastic 488 tricks before high heaven

As makes the angels weep, who, with our spleens 489 ,

Would all themselves laugh mortal 490 .

LUCIO O, to him, to him, wench: he will relent.

Aside to Isabella

He’s coming 491 , I perceive’t.

PROVOST Pray heaven she win him.

Aside

ISABELLA We cannot weigh our brother with ourself 492 .

Great men may jest with 493 saints, ’tis wit 494 in them,

But in the less foul profanation 495 .

LUCIO Thou’rt i’th’right, girl, more o’that.

Aside to Isabella

ISABELLA That in the captain’s but a choleric 496 word,

Which in the soldier is flat 497 blasphemy.

LUCIO Art avised 498 o’that? More on’t.

Aside to Isabella

ANGELO Why do you put 499 these sayings upon me?

ISABELLA Because authority, though it err like others,

Hath yet a kind of medicine in itself

That skins 500 the vice o’th’top. Go to your bosom,

Knock there, and ask your heart what it doth know

That’s like my brother’s fault. If it confess

A natural guiltiness 501 such as is his,

Let it not sound 502 a thought upon your tongue

Against my brother’s life.

ANGELO She speaks, and ’tis such sense 503

Aside

That my sense 504 breeds 505 with it.— Fare you well.

Starts to go

ISABELLA Gentle my lord, turn back.

ANGELO I will bethink me. Come again tomorrow.

ISABELLA Hark how I’ll bribe you: good my lord, turn back.

ANGELO How? Bribe me?

ISABELLA Ay, with such gifts that heaven shall share with you.

LUCIO You had marred all else 506 .

Aside to Isabella

ISABELLA Not with fond 507 sicles 508 of the tested 509 gold,

Or stones 510 whose rates 511 are either rich or poor

As fancy 512 values them, but with true 513 prayers

That shall be up at heaven and enter there

Ere sunrise, prayers from preservèd 514 souls,

From fasting maids 515 whose minds are dedicate 516

To nothing temporal 517 .

ANGELO Well, come to me tomorrow.

LUCIO Go to, ’tis well; away!

Aside to Isabella

ISABELLA Heaven keep your honour safe.

ANGELO Amen.

Aside

For I am that way going to temptation,

Where prayers cross 518 .

ISABELLA At what hour tomorrow

Shall I attend your lordship?

ANGELO At any time ’fore noon.

ISABELLA ’Save 519 your honour!

[Exeunt Isabella, Lucio and Provost ]

ANGELO From thee, even from thy virtue.

What’s this? What’s this? Is this her fault or mine?

The tempter or the tempted, who sins most? Ha?

Not she — nor doth she tempt — but it is I

That, lying by the violet 520 in the sun,

Do as the carrion 521 does, not as the flower,

Corrupt with virtuous season 522 . Can it be

That modesty 523 may more betray our sense

Than woman’s lightness 524 ? Having waste ground 525 enough,

Shall we desire to raze the sanctuary 526

And pitch 527 our evils there? O, fie, fie, fie!

What dost thou? Or what art thou, Angelo?

Dost thou desire her foully 528 for those things

That make her good? O, let her brother live!

Thieves for their robbery have authority

When judges steal themselves. What, do I love her,

That I desire to hear her speak again,

And feast upon her eyes? What is’t I dream on?

O cunning enemy 529 , that to catch a saint

With saints dost bait thy hook! Most dangerous

Is that temptation that doth goad us on

To sin in loving virtue: never could the strumpet,

With all her double vigour 530 , art and nature 531 ,

Once stir my temper 532 , but this virtuous maid

Subdues me quite. Ever till now,

When men were fond 533 , I smiled and wondered how.
