Scene 2 ／ running scene 2

Enter Lucio 78 and two other Gentlemen

LUCIO If the duke with the other dukes come not to composition 79 with the King of Hungary, why then all the dukes fall upon 80 the king.

FIRST GENTLEMAN Heaven grant us its peace, but not the King of Hungary’s 81 !

SECOND GENTLEMAN Amen.

LUCIO Thou concludest like the sanctimonious 82 pirate, that went to sea with the Ten Commandments, but scraped one out of the table 83 .

SECOND GENTLEMAN ‘Thou shalt not steal’?

LUCIO Ay, that he razed 84 .

FIRST GENTLEMAN Why, ’twas a commandment to command the captain and all the rest from their functions 85 : they put forth 86 to steal. There’s not a soldier of us all that, in the thanksgiving before meat 87 , do relish the petition 88 well that prays for peace.

SECOND GENTLEMAN I never heard any soldier dislike it.

LUCIO I believe thee; for I think thou never wast where grace was said.

SECOND GENTLEMAN No? A dozen times at least.

FIRST GENTLEMAN What, in metre 89 ?

LUCIO In any proportion 90 or in any language.

FIRST GENTLEMAN I think, or in any religion.

LUCIO Ay, why not? Grace is grace, despite of all controversy 91 : as for example, thou thyself art a wicked villain, despite of all grace.

FIRST GENTLEMAN Well, there went but a pair of shears between us 92 .

LUCIO I grant, as there may between the lists 93 and the velvet. Thou art the list.

FIRST GENTLEMAN And thou the velvet. Thou art good velvet; thou’rt a three-piled piece 94 , I warrant thee. I had as lief 95 be a list of an English kersey 96 as be piled 97 , as thou art piled, for a French velvet 98 . Do I speak feelingly 99 now?

LUCIO I think thou dost, and indeed, with most painful feeling 100 of thy speech. I will, out of thine own confession 101 , learn to begin thy health, but, whilst I live, forget to drink after thee 102 .

FIRST GENTLEMAN I think I have done myself wrong 103 , have I not?

SECOND GENTLEMAN Yes, that thou hast, whether thou art tainted or free 104 .

Enter Bawd [Mistress Overdone 105 ]

LUCIO Behold, behold, where Madam Mitigation 106 comes! I have purchased as many diseases under her roof as come to—

SECOND GENTLEMAN To what, I pray?

LUCIO Judge 107 .

SECOND GENTLEMAN To three thousand dolours 108 a year.

FIRST GENTLEMAN Ay, and more.

LUCIO A French crown 109 more.

FIRST GENTLEMAN Thou art always figuring 110 diseases in me, but thou art full of error, I am sound 111 .

LUCIO Nay, not as one would say, healthy: but so sound as things that are hollow; thy bones are hollow 112 , impiety 113 has made a feast of thee.

FIRST GENTLEMAN How now! Which of your hips has the most profound 114 sciatica 115 ?

To Mistress Overdone

MISTRESS OVERDONE Well, well. There’s one yonder arrested and carried to prison was worth five thousand of you all.

SECOND GENTLEMAN Who’s that, I pray thee?

MISTRESS OVERDONE Marry 116 , sir, that’s Claudio, Signior Claudio.

FIRST GENTLEMAN Claudio to prison? ’Tis not so.

MISTRESS OVERDONE Nay, but I know ’tis so. I saw him arrested, saw him carried away, and, which is more, within these three days his head to be chopped off.

LUCIO But, after 117 all this fooling, I would not have it so. Art thou sure of this?

MISTRESS OVERDONE I am too sure of it. And it is for getting Madam Julietta with child.

LUCIO Believe me, this may be: he promised to meet me two hours since, and he was ever precise 118 in promise-keeping.

SECOND GENTLEMAN Besides, you know, it draws something near to the speech we had to such a purpose 119 .

FIRST GENTLEMAN But most of all agreeing with the proclamation 120 .

LUCIO Away! Let’s go learn the truth of it.

Exeunt [Lucio and Gentlemen ]

MISTRESS OVERDONE Thus, what with the war, what with the sweat 121 , what with the gallows and what with poverty, I am custom-shrunk 122 .

Enter Clown [Pompey 123 ]

How now? What’s the news with you?

POMPEY Yonder man 124 is carried to prison.

MISTRESS OVERDONE Well, what has he done 125 ?

POMPEY A woman.

MISTRESS OVERDONE But what’s his offence?

POMPEY Groping for trouts in a peculiar river 126 .

MISTRESS OVERDONE What, is there a maid 127 with child by him?

POMPEY No, but there’s a woman with maid by him. You have not heard of the proclamation, have you?

MISTRESS OVERDONE What proclamation, man?

POMPEY All houses in the suburbs 128 of Vienna must be plucked down.

MISTRESS OVERDONE And what shall become of those in the city?

POMPEY They shall stand for seed 129 : they had gone down too, but that a wise burgher 130 put in 131 for them.

MISTRESS OVERDONE But shall all our houses of resort 132 in the suburbs be pulled down?

POMPEY To the ground, mistress.

MISTRESS OVERDONE Why, here’s a change indeed in the commonwealth! What shall become of me?

POMPEY Come, fear you not: good counsellors 133 lack no clients. Though you change your place, you need not change your trade: I’ll be your tapster 134 still. Courage! There will be pity taken on you; you that have worn your eyes almost out in the service 135 , you will be considered.

MISTRESS OVERDONE What’s to do here, Thomas 136 tapster? Let’s withdraw.

POMPEY Here comes Signior Claudio, led by the provost to prison, and there’s Madam Juliet.

Exeunt
