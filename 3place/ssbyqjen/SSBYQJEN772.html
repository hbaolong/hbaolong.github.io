Enter Talbot, Bedford, Burgundy, [a Captain, and others ]

BEDFORD The day begins to break, and night is fled,

Whose pitchy 422 mantle over-veiled the earth.

Here sound retreat, and cease our hot pursuit.

Retreat 423 [sounded ]

TALBOT Bring forth the body of old Salisbury,

And here advance 424 it in the market-place,

The middle centre of this cursèd town.

Now have I paid my vow 425 unto his soul:

For every drop of blood was drawn from him,

There hath at least five Frenchmen died tonight.

And that hereafter ages may behold

What ruin 426 happened in revenge of him,

Within their chiefest temple I’ll erect

A tomb, wherein his corpse shall be interred:

Upon the which, that everyone may read,

Shall be engraved the sack of Orléans,

The treacherous manner of his mournful 427 death,

And what a terror he had been to France.

But, lords, in all our bloody massacre,

I muse 428 we met not with the dauphin’s grace,

His new-come champion 429 , virtuous 430 Joan of Arc,

Nor any of his false confederates.

BEDFORD ’Tis thought, Lord Talbot, when the fight began,

Roused on the sudden from their drowsy beds,

They did amongst the troops of armèd men

Leap o’er the walls for refuge in the field.

BURGUNDY Myself, as far as I could well discern

For smoke and dusky vapours of the night,

Am sure I scared the dauphin and his trull 431 ,

When arm in arm they both came swiftly running,

Like to a pair of loving turtle-doves 432

That could not live asunder day or night.

After that things are set in order here,

We’ll follow them with all the power 433 we have.

Enter a Messenger

MESSENGER All hail, my lords! Which of this princely train

Call ye the warlike Talbot, for his acts

So much applauded through the realm of France?

TALBOT Here is the Talbot: who would speak with him?

MESSENGER The virtuous lady, Countess of Auvergne,

With modesty admiring thy renown,

By me entreats, great lord, thou wouldst vouchsafe 434

To visit her poor 435 castle where she lies 436 ,

That she may boast she hath beheld the man

Whose glory fills the world with loud report 437 .

BURGUNDY Is it even so? Nay, then I see our wars

Will turn unto a peaceful comic sport 438 ,

When ladies crave to be encountered with 439 .

You may not, my lord, despise 440 her gentle 441 suit.

TALBOT Ne’er trust me then: for when a world 442 of men

Could not prevail with all their oratory 443 ,

Yet hath a woman’s kindness overruled 444 :

And therefore tell her I return great thanks,

And in submission will attend on 445 her.

Will not your honours bear me company?

BEDFORD No, truly, ’tis more than manners will:

And I have heard it said, unbidden 446 guests

Are often welcomest when they are gone.
