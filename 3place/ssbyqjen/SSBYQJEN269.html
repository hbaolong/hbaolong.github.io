Scene 4 158 / running scene 4

Enter Valentine and Viola [as Cesario ] in man’s attire

VALENTINE If the duke continue these favours towards you, Cesario, you are like to be much advanced 159 . He hath known you but three days, and already you are no stranger.

VIOLA You either fear his humour 160 or my negligence, that you call in question the continuance of his love. Is he inconstant, sir, in his favours?

VALENTINE No, believe me.

Enter Duke [Orsino ], Curio and Attendants

VIOLA I thank you. Here comes the count.

ORSINO Who saw Cesario, ho?

VIOLA On your attendance 161 , my lord, here.

ORSINO Stand you awhile aloof 162 .— Cesario,

To Attendants, who stand aside

Thou know’st no less but all 163 . I have unclasped

To thee the book even of my secret soul:

Therefore, good youth, address thy gait 164 unto her,

Be not denied access, stand at her doors,

And tell them 165 there thy fixèd foot shall grow 166

Till thou have audience 167 .

VIOLA Sure, my noble lord,

If she be so abandoned to her sorrow

As it is spoke 168 , she never will admit me.

ORSINO Be clamorous and leap all civil bounds 169

Rather than make unprofited return.

VIOLA Say I do speak with her, my lord, what then?

ORSINO O, then unfold the passion of my love,

Surprise 170 her with discourse of my dear faith 171 ;

It shall become 172 thee well to act my woes.

She will attend 173 it better in thy youth

Than in a nuncio’s 174 of more grave aspect 175 .

VIOLA I think not so, my lord.

ORSINO Dear lad 176 , believe it;

For they shall yet belie 177 thy happy years,

That say thou art a man: Diana’s lip 178

Is not more smooth and rubious 179 , thy small pipe 180

Is as the maiden’s organ, shrill and sound 181 ,

And all is semblative 182 a woman’s part 183 .

I know thy constellation 184 is right apt

For this affair.— Some four or five attend him.

To Attendants

All, if you will, for I myself am best

When least in company. Prosper well in this,

And thou shalt live as freely 185 as thy lord,

To call his fortunes thine.

VIOLA I’ll do my best

To woo your lady.— Yet, a barful strife 186 !

Aside

Whoe’er I woo, myself would be his wife.

Exeunt
