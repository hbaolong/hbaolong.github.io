Scene 1 1 / running scene 1

Enter Orsino 2 Duke of Illyria, Curio 3 and other Lords

Music plays

ORSINO If music be the food of love, play on,

Give me excess of it, that surfeiting 4 ,

The appetite 5 may sicken and so die.

That strain again, it had a dying fall 6 :

O, it came o’er my ear like the sweet sound 7

That breathes upon a bank of violets,

Stealing and giving odour. Enough, no more,

’Tis not so sweet now as it was before.

Music stops

O spirit of love, how quick and fresh 8 art thou

That, notwithstanding thy capacity 9 ,

Receiveth as the sea 10 . Nought enters there,

Of what validity and pitch 11 soe’er,

But falls into abatement 12 and low price

Even in a minute. So full of shapes 13 is fancy 14

That it alone is high fantastical 15 .

CURIO Will you go hunt, my lord?

ORSINO What, Curio?

CURIO The hart 16 .

ORSINO Why so I do, the noblest that I have.

O, when mine eyes did see Olivia first,

Methought she purged the air of pestilence 17 .

That instant was I turned into a hart,

And my desires, like fell 18 and cruel hounds,

E’er since pursue me 19 .

Enter Valentine 20

How now, what news from her?

VALENTINE So please my lord, I might not be admitted,

But from her handmaid do return this answer:

The element 21 itself, till seven years’ heat 22 ,

Shall not behold her face at ample 23 view,

But like a cloistress 24 she will veilèd walk,

And water once a day her chamber round

With eye-offending brine 25 — all this to season 26

A brother’s dead love, which she would keep fresh

And lasting in her sad remembrance.

ORSINO O, she that hath a heart of that fine frame

To pay this debt of love but to a brother,

How will she love when the rich golden shaft 27

Hath killed the flock of all affections else 28

That live in her — when liver, brain and heart 29 ,

These sovereign thrones, are all supplied 30 , and filled

Her sweet perfections 31 with one self 32 king!

Away before me, to sweet beds of flowers.

Love thoughts lie rich when canopied with bowers.

Exeunt
