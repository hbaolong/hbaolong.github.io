Scene 5 ／ running scene 4

Enter Isabella and Francisca, a nun

ISABELLA And have you nuns no further privileges?

FRANCISCA Are not these large enough?

ISABELLA Yes, truly. I speak not as desiring more,

But rather wishing a more strict restraint

Upon the sisterhood, the votarists 233 of Saint Clare 234 .

LUCIO Ho? Peace be in this place!

Within

ISABELLA Who’s that which calls?

FRANCISCA It is a man’s voice. Gentle Isabella,

Turn you the key, and know his business of him;

You may, I may not: you are yet unsworn 235 .

When you have vowed, you must not speak with men

But in the presence of the prioress 236 .

Then, if you speak, you must not show your face,

Or, if you show your face, you must not speak.

He calls again. I pray you, answer him.

[Exit ]

ISABELLA Peace and prosperity! Who is’t that calls?

[Enter Lucio ]

LUCIO Hail, virgin, if you be, as those cheek-roses 237

Proclaim you are no less. Can you so stead 238 me

As bring me to the sight of Isabella,

A novice 239 of this place and the fair sister

To her unhappy 240 brother Claudio?

ISABELLA Why her unhappy brother? Let me ask,

The rather for 241 I now must make you know

I am that Isabella and his sister.

LUCIO Gentle and fair, your brother kindly 242 greets you.

Not to be weary 243 with you, he’s in prison.

ISABELLA Woe me! For what?

LUCIO For that which, if myself might be his judge,

He should receive his punishment in thanks 244 :

He hath got his friend 245 with child.

ISABELLA Sir, make me not your story 246 .

LUCIO ’Tis true.

I would not, though ’tis my familiar 247 sin

With maids to seem the lapwing 248 and to jest,

Tongue far from heart 249 , play 250 with all virgins so.

I hold you as a thing enskied 251 and sainted

By your renouncement 252 , an immortal spirit,

And to be talked with in sincerity,

As with a saint.

ISABELLA You do blaspheme the good in mocking me 253 .

LUCIO Do not believe it. Fewness 254 and truth, ’tis thus:

Your brother and his lover have embraced 255 .

As those that feed grow full, as blossoming time

That from the seedness 256 the bare fallow 257 brings

To teeming foison 258 , even so her plenteous womb

Expresseth 259 his full tilth and husbandry 260 .

ISABELLA Someone with child by him? My cousin 261 Juliet?

LUCIO Is she your cousin?

ISABELLA Adoptedly, as school-maids change their names

By vain though apt affection 262 .

LUCIO She it is.

ISABELLA O, let him marry her.

LUCIO This is the point.

The duke is very strangely gone from hence,

Bore many gentlemen, myself being one,

In hand 263 and hope of action: but we do learn

By those that know the very nerves of state,

His giving-out 264 were of an infinite distance

From his true-meant design 265 . Upon 266 his place,

And with full line 267 of his authority,

Governs Lord Angelo, a man whose blood

Is very snow-broth 268 : one who never feels

The wanton 269 stings and motions 270 of the sense 271 ,

But doth rebate 272 and blunt his natural edge 273

With profits 274 of the mind, study and fast 275 .

He — to give fear to use 276 and liberty,

Which have for long run by the hideous 277 law,

As mice by lions — hath picked out an act,

Under whose heavy sense 278 your brother’s life

Falls into forfeit 279 . He arrests him on it,

And follows close the rigour of the statute

To make him an example. All hope is gone,

Unless you have the grace 280 by your fair prayer

To soften Angelo: and that’s my pith 281 of business

’Twixt you and your poor brother.

ISABELLA Doth he so seek his life?

LUCIO Has censured 282 him already,

And, as I hear, the provost hath a warrant

For’s execution.

ISABELLA Alas, what poor ability’s in me

To do him good?

LUCIO Assay 283 the power you have.

ISABELLA My power? Alas, I doubt—

LUCIO Our doubts are traitors,

And make us lose the good we oft might win

By fearing to attempt. Go to Lord Angelo,

And let him learn to know, when maidens sue 284 ,

Men give 285 like gods: but when they weep and kneel,

All their petitions are as freely theirs

As they themselves would owe them 286 .

ISABELLA I’ll see what I can do.

LUCIO But speedily.

ISABELLA I will about it straight,

No longer staying but to give the mother 287

Notice of my affair. I humbly thank you:

Commend me to my brother. Soon at night 288

I’ll send him certain word of my success 289 .

LUCIO I take my leave of you.

ISABELLA Good sir, adieu.

Exeunt [separately ]
