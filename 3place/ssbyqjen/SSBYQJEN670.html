Scene 4 324 ／running scene 4

Enter King, Aumerle, Green and Bagot

KING RICHARD We did observe 325 .—— Cousin Aumerle,

How far brought you high 326 Hereford on his way?

AUMERLE I brought high Hereford, if you call him so,

But to the next 327 highway, and there I left him.

KING RICHARD And say, what store 328 of parting tears were shed?

AUMERLE Faith, none for me 329 , except the north-east wind,

Which then blew bitterly against our face,

Awaked the sleepy rheum 330 , and so by chance

Did grace our hollow 331 parting with a tear.

KING RICHARD What said our cousin when you parted with him?

AUMERLE ‘Farewell’. And, for 332 my heart disdainèd that my tongue

Should so profane the word, that taught me craft 333

To counterfeit 334 oppression of such grief

That word seemed buried in my sorrow’s grave.

Marry 335 , would the word ‘farewell’ have lengthened hours

And added years to his short banishment,

He should have had a volume of farewells,

But since it would not, he had none of me.

KING RICHARD He is our cousin 336 , cousin, but ’tis doubt 337 ,

When time shall call him home from banishment,

Whether our kinsman come to see his friends 338 .

Ourself and Bushy, Bagot here, and Green

Observed his courtship to the common people.

How he did seem to dive into their hearts

With humble and familiar courtesy,

What reverence 339 he did throw away on slaves,

Wooing poor craftsmen with the craft of smiles

And patient underbearing 340 of his fortune,

As ’twere to banish their affects with him 341 .

Off goes his bonnet 342 to an oyster-wench 343 .

A brace of draymen 344 bid God speed him well

And had the tribute of his supple 345 knee,

With ‘Thanks, my countrymen, my loving friends’,

As were our England in reversion 346 his,

And he our subjects’ next degree in hope 347 .

GREEN Well, he is gone, and with him go these thoughts.

Now for the rebels which stand out 348 in Ireland.

Expedient manage 349 must be made, my liege,

Ere further leisure 350 yield them further means

For their advantage and your highness’ loss.

KING RICHARD We will ourself in person to this war,

And, for our coffers with too great a court

And liberal largesse 351 are grown somewhat light,

We are enforced to farm 352 our royal realm,

The revenue whereof shall furnish us

For our affairs in hand. If that come short 353 ,

Our substitutes 354 at home shall have blank charters 355 ,

Whereto, when they shall know what men are rich,

They shall subscribe them 356 for large sums of gold

And send them 357 after to supply our wants,

For we will make for Ireland presently 358 .

Enter Bushy

Bushy, what news?

BUSHY Old John of Gaunt is very sick, my lord,

Suddenly taken, and hath sent post haste

To entreat your majesty to visit him.

KING RICHARD Where lies he?

BUSHY At Ely House 359 .

KING RICHARD Now put it, heaven, in his physician’s mind

To help him to his grave immediately!

The lining 360 of his coffers shall make coats

To deck our soldiers for these Irish wars.

Come, gentlemen, let’s all go visit him.

Pray heaven we may make haste, and come too late!

[Exeunt ]
