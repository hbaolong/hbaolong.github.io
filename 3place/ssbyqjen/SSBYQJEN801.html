Scene 4 306 / running scene 4

Enter the Witch [Margaret Jordan ], the two priests [Hume and Southwell ] and Bullingbrook

HUME Come, my masters, the duchess, I tell you, expects performance of your promises.

BULLINGBROOK Master Hume, we are therefore provided 307 : will her ladyship behold and hear our exorcisms 308 ?

HUME Ay, what else? Fear you not her courage.

BULLINGBROOK I have heard her reported to be a woman of an invincible spirit: but it shall be convenient, Master Hume, that you be by her aloft 309 , while we be busy below: and so, I pray you, go in God’s name and leave us.

Exit Hume

Mother Jordan, be you prostrate and grovel on the earth. John Southwell, read you, and let us to our work.

She lies down upon her face

Enter Eleanor aloft [Hume following ]

ELEANOR Well said 310 , my masters, and welcome all. To this gear 311 the sooner the better.

BULLINGBROOK Patience, good lady: wizards know their times:

Deep night, dark night, the silent of the night,

The time of night when Troy was set on fire 312 ,

The time when screech-owls 313 cry and bandogs 314 howl,

And spirits walk, and ghosts break up 315 their graves:

That time best fits the work we have in hand.

Madam, sit you and fear not: whom we raise,

We will make fast 316 within a hallowed verge 317 .

Here [they ] do the ceremonies belonging 318 , and make the circle: Bullingbrook or Southwell reads, ‘Conjuro te’ 319 , etc. It thunders and lightens terribly: then the Spirit [Asnath 320 ] riseth 321

ASNATH Adsum 322 .

MARGARET JORDAN Asnath,

By the eternal God, whose name and power

Thou tremblest at, answer that 323 I shall ask:

For till thou speak, thou shalt not pass from hence.

ASNATH Ask what thou wilt: that 324 I had said and done.

BULLINGBROOK ‘First of the king: what shall of him become?’

Reads

ASNATH The duke yet lives that Henry shall depose:

But him outlive, and die a violent death.

As the Spirit speaks, Southwell writes the answer

BULLINGBROOK ‘What fates await the Duke of Suffolk?’

Reads

ASNATH By water shall he die, and take his end.

BULLINGBROOK ‘What shall befall the Duke of Somerset?’

Reads

ASNATH Let him shun castles;

Safer shall he be upon the sandy plains

Than where castles mounted 325 stand.

Have done, for more I hardly can endure.

BULLINGBROOK Descend to darkness and the burning lake!

False 326 fiend, avoid 327 !

Thunder and lightning. Exit Spirit

Enter the Duke of York and the Duke of Buckingham with their guard [Sir Humphrey Stafford as Captain ] and break in

YORK Lay hands upon these traitors and their trash 328 :

Beldam 329 , I think we watched you at an inch 330 .

To Jordan

What, madam, are you there? The king and commonweal

Are deeply indebted for this piece of pains 331 :

My Lord Protector will, I doubt it not,

See you well guerdoned 332 for these good deserts 333 .

ELEANOR Not half so bad as thine to England’s king,

Injurious 334 duke, that threatest 335 where’s no cause.

BUCKINGHAM True, madam, none at all: what call you this?

Pointing to the papers

Away with them: let them be clapped up close 336

And kept asunder 337 .— You, madam, shall with us.

To Eleanor

Stafford, take her to 338 thee.

[Exeunt above Eleanor and Hume, guarded ]

We’ll see your trinkets 339 here all forthcoming 340 .

All, away!

Exeunt [below guard with Margaret Jordan, Southwell and Bullingbrook ]

YORK Lord Buckingham, methinks you watched her well:

A pretty 341 plot 342 , well chosen to build upon.

Now pray, my lord, let’s see the devil’s writ 343 .

Buckingham gives him the papers

What have we here?

’The duke yet lives, that Henry shall depose

Reads

But him outlive, and die a violent death.’

Why, this is just 344

‘Aio Aeacidam, Romanos vincere posse 345 .’

Well, to the rest:

‘Tell me what fate awaits the Duke of Suffolk?’

‘By water shall he die, and take his end.’

‘What shall betide the Duke of Somerset?’

’Let him shun castles:

Safer shall he be upon the sandy plains

Than where castles mounted stand.’

Come, come, my lords,

These oracles are hardly attained 346 ,

And hardly 347 understood.

The king is now in progress 348 towards St Albans,

With him the husband of this lovely lady:

Thither goes these news as fast as horse can carry them:

A sorry breakfast for my Lord Protector.

BUCKINGHAM Your grace shall give me leave, my lord of York,

To be the post 349 in hope of his reward.

YORK At your pleasure, my good lord. Who’s within there, ho!

Calling within

Enter a Servingman

Invite my lords of Salisbury and Warwick

To sup 350 with me tomorrow night. Away
