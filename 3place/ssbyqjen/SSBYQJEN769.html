Scene 5 / running scene 4 continues

Here an alarum again, and Talbot pursueth the Dauphin, and driveth him: then enter Joan la Pucelle, driving Englishmen before her, [and exeunt ]. Then enter Talbot

TALBOT Where is my strength, my valour, and my force?

Our English troops retire, I cannot stay 331 them:

A woman clad in armour chaseth them.

Enter [Joan la ] Pucelle

Here, here she comes. I’ll have a bout 332 with thee:

Devil or devil’s dam 333 , I’ll conjure 334 thee:

Blood will I draw on thee — thou art a witch 335 —

And straightway give thy soul to him 336 thou serv’st.

PUCELLE Come, come, ’tis only I that must disgrace thee.

Here they fight

TALBOT Heavens, can you suffer hell so to prevail?

My breast I’ll burst with straining of my courage 337

And from my shoulders crack my arms asunder.

But I will chastise this high-minded 338 strumpet.

They fight again

PUCELLE Talbot, farewell: thy hour is not yet come:

I must go victual 339 Orléans forthwith.

A short alarum: then [the French ] enter the town with soldiers

O’ertake me if thou canst: I scorn thy strength.

Go, go, cheer up thy hungry-starvèd men:

Help Salisbury to make his testament 340 :

This day is ours, as many more shall be.

Exit

TALBOT My thoughts are whirlèd like a potter’s wheel:

I know not where I am, nor what I do:

A witch by fear, not force, like Hannibal 341 ,

Drives back our troops and conquers as she lists 342 :

So bees with smoke and doves with noisome 343 stench

Are from their hives and houses driven away.

They called us, for our fierceness, English dogs:

Now, like to whelps 344 , we crying run away.

A short alarum

Hark, countrymen: either renew the fight,

Or tear the lions out of England’s coat 345 ;

Renounce your soil, give 346 sheep in lions’ stead 347 :

Sheep run not half so treacherous 348 from the wolf,

Or horse or oxen from the leopard,

As you fly from your oft-subduèd 349 slaves.

Alarum. Here another skirmish

It will not be 350 : retire into your trenches:

You all consented unto 351 Salisbury’s death,

For none would strike a stroke in his revenge 352 .

Pucelle is entered into Orléans,

In spite of us or aught that we could do.

O would 353 I were to die with Salisbury!

The shame hereof will make me hide my head.

Exit Talbot

Alarum: retreat: flourish
