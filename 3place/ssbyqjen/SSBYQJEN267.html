Scene 2 / running scene 2

Enter Viola 33 , a Captain and Sailors

VIOLA What country, friends, is this?

CAPTAIN This is Illyria, lady.

VIOLA And what should I do in Illyria?

My brother he is in Elysium 34 .

Perchance 35 he is not drowned: what think you, sailors?

CAPTAIN It is perchance that you yourself were saved.

VIOLA O, my poor brother! And so perchance may he be.

CAPTAIN True, madam, and to comfort you with chance 36 ,

Assure yourself, after our ship did split,

When you and those poor number saved with you

Hung on our driving 37 boat, I saw your brother,

Most provident 38 in peril, bind himself —

Courage and hope both teaching him the practice 39 —

To a strong mast that lived 40 upon the sea,

Where, like Arion 41 on the dolphin’s back,

I saw him hold acquaintance with 42 the waves

So long as I could see.

VIOLA For saying so, there’s gold.

Gives money

Mine own escape unfoldeth to my hope 43 ,

Whereto thy speech serves for authority,

The like of him 44 . Know’st thou this country?

CAPTAIN Ay, madam, well, for I was bred and born

Not three hours’ travel from this very place.

VIOLA Who governs here?

CAPTAIN A noble duke, in nature as in name.

VIOLA What is his name?

CAPTAIN Orsino.

VIOLA Orsino. I have heard my father name him.

He was a bachelor then.

CAPTAIN And so is now, or was so very late 45 ,

For but a month ago I went from hence,

And then ’twas fresh in murmur 46 — as you know,

What great ones do, the less will prattle of 47 —

That he did seek the love of fair Olivia.

VIOLA What’s she?

CAPTAIN A virtuous maid, the daughter of a count

That died some twelvemonth since, then leaving her

In the protection of his son, her brother,

Who shortly also died, for whose dear love,

They say, she hath abjured the sight

And company of men.

VIOLA O that I served that lady,

And might not be delivered to the world

Till I had made mine own occasion mellow,

What my estate is 48 .

CAPTAIN That were hard to compass 49 ,

Because she will admit no kind of suit 50 ,

No, not 51 the duke’s.

VIOLA There is a fair behaviour 52 in thee, captain,

And though that 53 nature with a beauteous wall

Doth oft close in 54 pollution, yet of thee

I will believe thou hast a mind that suits

With 55 this thy fair and outward character 56 .

I prithee — and I’ll pay thee bounteously —

Conceal me what I am, and be my aid

For such disguise as haply shall become

The form of my intent 57 . I’ll serve this duke.

Thou shalt present me as an eunuch 58 to him.

It may be worth thy pains, for I can sing

And speak to him in many sorts of music

That will allow 59 me very worth 60 his service.

What else may hap 61 , to time I will commit,

Only shape thou thy silence to my wit 62 .

CAPTAIN Be you his eunuch, and your mute I’ll be:

When my tongue blabs, then let mine eyes not see.

VIOLA I thank thee. Lead me on.

Exeunt
