Scene 1 420 / running scene 4

Enter a Carrier 421 with a lantern in his hand

FIRST CARRIER Heigh-ho! An’t be not four by the day 422 , I’ll be hanged. Charles’ wain 423 is over the new chimney, and yet our horse not packed 424 . What, ostler 425 !

OSTLER Anon, anon 426 .

Within

FIRST CARRIER I prithee, Tom 427 , beat Cut’s saddle 428 , put a few flocks 429 in the point 430 . The poor jade 431 is wrung in the withers 432 out of all cess 433 .

Enter another Carrier

SECOND CARRIER Peas and beans 434 are as dank here as a dog 435 , and this is the next way to give poor jades the bots 436 . This house 437 is turned upside down since Robin the ostler died.

FIRST CARRIER Poor fellow, never joyed 438 since the price of oats rose. It was the death of him.

SECOND CARRIER I think this is the most villainous house in all London Road for fleas: I am stung like a tench 439 .

FIRST CARRIER Like a tench? There is ne’er a king in Christendom could be better bit than I have been since the first cock 440 .

SECOND CARRIER Why, you will allow us ne’er a jordan 441 , and then we leak 442 in your chimney 443 , and your chamber-lye 444 breeds fleas like a loach 445 .

FIRST CARRIER What, ostler! Come away 446 and be hanged! Come away!

SECOND CARRIER I have a gammon of bacon 447 and two razes 448 of ginger, to be delivered as far as Charing Cross 449 .

FIRST CARRIER The turkeys in my pannier 450 are quite starved. What, ostler! A plague on thee 451 ! Hast 452 thou never an eye in thy head? Canst not hear? An ’twere not as good a deed as drink to break the pate of thee, I am a very villain. Come, and be hanged! Hast no faith 453 in thee?

Enter Gadshill

GADSHILL Good morrow, carriers. What’s o’clock?

FIRST CARRIER I think it be two o’clock 454 .

GADSHILL I prithee lend me thy lantern to see my gelding 455 in the stable.

FIRST CARRIER Nay, soft, I pray ye; I know a trick worth two of that 456 .

GADSHILL I prithee lend me thine.

To the Second Carrier

SECOND CARRIER Ay, when? Can’st tell? 457 Lend me thy lantern, quoth a 458 ? Marry, I’ll see thee hanged first.

GADSHILL Sirrah carrier, what time do you mean to come to London?

SECOND CARRIER Time enough to go to bed with a candle 459 , I warrant 460 thee.— Come, neighbour Mugs 461 , we’ll call 462 up the gentlemen. They will along with company, for they have great charge 463 .

Exeunt [Carriers ]

Enter Chamberlain 464

GADSHILL What, ho, Chamberlain?

CHAMBERLAIN At hand, quoth pick-purse 465 .

GADSHILL That’s even as 466 fair as — at hand, quoth the chamberlain, for thou variest no more from picking of purses than giving direction doth from labouring 467 . Thou lay’st the plot how 468 .

CHAMBERLAIN Good morrow, Master Gadshill. It holds current that 469 I told you yesternight: there’s a franklin 470 in the wild of Kent 471 hath brought three hundred marks with him in gold 472 . I heard him tell it to one of his company last night at supper, a kind of auditor 473 , one that hath abundance of charge 474 too, God knows what. They are up already, and call for eggs and butter 475 . They will away presently 476 .

GADSHILL Sirrah, if they meet not with Saint Nicholas’ clerks 477 , I’ll give thee this neck 478 .

CHAMBERLAIN No, I’ll none of it: I prithee keep that for the hangman, for I know thou worship’st Saint Nicholas as truly as a man of falsehood may.

GADSHILL What talkest thou to me of the hangman? If I hang, I’ll make a fat pair of gallows, for if I hang, old Sir John hangs with me, and thou know’st he’s no starveling 479 . Tut, there are other Trojans 480 that thou dream’st not of, the which 481 for sport sake are content to do the profession 482 some grace; that would, if matters should be looked into, for their own credit sake, make all whole 483 . I am joined with no foot-land rakers 484 , no long-staff sixpenny strikers 485 , none of these mad mustachio purple-hued malt-worms 486 , but with nobility and tranquillity 487 , burgomasters 488 and great oneyers 489 , such as can hold in 490 , such as will strike sooner than speak, and speak sooner than drink, and drink sooner than pray. And yet, I lie, for they pray continually unto their saint, the commonwealth 491 ; or rather not to pray to her, but prey on her, for they ride 492 up and down on her and make her their boots 493 .

CHAMBERLAIN What, the commonwealth their boots? Will she hold out water in foul way 494 ?

GADSHILL She will, she will; justice hath liquored 495 her. We steal as in a castle 496 , cocksure 497 . We have the receipt of 498 fern-seed, we walk invisible.

CHAMBERLAIN Nay, I think rather you are more beholding to the night than to fern-seed for your walking invisible.

GADSHILL Give me thy hand. Thou shalt have a share in our purpose, as I am a true 499 man.

CHAMBERLAIN Nay, rather let me have it, as you are a false thief.

GADSHILL Go to. Homo 500 is a common name to 501 all men. Bid the ostler bring the gelding out of the stable. Farewell, ye muddy 502 knave.

Exeunt
