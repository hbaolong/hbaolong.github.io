Scene 2 388 / running scene 5

Flourish. Enter the King, the Queen, Clifford, Northum [berland ] and young Prince, with Drum and Trumpets 389

York’s head is set above the city gates

QUEEN MARGARET Welcome, my lord, to this brave 390 town of York.

Yonder’s the head of that arch-enemy

That sought to be encompassed with your crown:

Doth not the object cheer your heart, my lord?

KING HENRY VI Ay, as the rocks cheer them that fear their wreck 391 :

To see this sight, it irks 392 my very soul.

Withhold revenge, dear God! ’tis not my fault,

Nor wittingly have I infringed my vow.

CLIFFORD My gracious liege, this too much lenity 393

And harmful pity must be laid aside.

To whom do lions cast their gentle looks?

Not to the beast that would usurp their den.

Whose hand is that the forest bear doth lick?

Not his that spoils 394 her young before her face.

Who scapes the lurking serpent’s mortal sting?

Not he that sets his foot upon her back.

The smallest worm will turn being trodden on,

And doves will peck in safeguard of their brood.

Ambitious York doth level 395 at thy crown,

Thou smiling while he knit 396 his angry brows:

He, but a duke, would have his son a king,

And raise 397 his issue 398 , like a loving sire 399 ;

Thou, being a king, blest with a goodly 400 son,

Didst yield consent to disinherit him,

Which argued thee 401 a most unloving father.

Unreasonable 402 creatures feed their young;

And though man’s face be fearful 403 to their eyes,

Yet, in protection of their tender 404 ones,

Who hath not seen them, even with those wings

Which sometime they have used with fearful flight,

Make war with him that climbed unto their nest,

Offering their own lives in their young’s defence?

For shame, my liege, make them your precedent 405 !

Were it not pity 406 that this goodly boy

Should lose his birthright by his father’s fault,

And long hereafter say unto his child,

’What my great-grandfather and his grandsire got

My careless father fondly 407 gave away’?

Ah, what a shame 408 were this! Look on the boy;

And let his manly face, which promiseth

Successful fortune, steel thy melting 409 heart

To hold thine own and leave thine own with him.

KING HENRY VI Full well hath Clifford played the orator,

Inferring 410 arguments of mighty force:

But, Clifford, tell me, didst thou never hear

That things ill-got 411 had ever bad success 412 ?

And happy 413 always was it for that son

Whose father for his hoarding 414 went to hell?

I’ll leave my son my virtuous deeds behind,

And would my father had left me no more:

For all the rest is held at such a rate 415

As brings a thousand-fold more care 416 to keep 417

Than in possession any jot of pleasure.

Ah, cousin York, would thy best friends did know

How it doth grieve me that thy head is here!

QUEEN MARGARET My lord, cheer up your spirits: our foes are nigh 418 ,

And this soft courage makes your followers faint 419 .

You promised knighthood to our forward 420 son:

Unsheathe your sword, and dub 421 him presently 422 .

Edward, kneel down.

KING HENRY VI Edward Plantagenet, arise a knight;

And learn this lesson, draw thy sword in right 423 .

PRINCE EDWARD My gracious father, by your kingly leave,

I’ll draw it as apparent 424 to the crown,

And in that quarrel use it to the death.

CLIFFORD Why, that is spoken like a toward 425 prince.

Enter a Messenger

MESSENGER Royal commanders, be in readiness:

For with a band of thirty thousand men

Comes Warwick, backing of 426 the Duke of York;

And in the towns, as they do march along,

Proclaims him king, and many fly to him.

Deraign 427 your battle 428 , for they are at hand.

[Exit ]

CLIFFORD I would your highness would depart the field:

The queen hath best success when you are absent.

QUEEN MARGARET Ay, good my lord, and leave us to our fortune.

KING HENRY VI Why, that’s my fortune too: therefore I’ll stay.

NORTHUMBERLAND Be it with resolution then to fight.

PRINCE EDWARD My royal father, cheer these noble lords

And hearten those that fight in your defence.

Unsheathe your sword, good father, cry ‘Saint George!’

March. Enter Edward, Warwick, Richard, Clarence [George ] 429 , Norfolk, Montague and Soldiers

EDWARD Now, perjured Henry, wilt thou kneel for grace,

And set thy diadem upon my head,

Or bide 430 the mortal 431 fortune of the field?

QUEEN MARGARET Go, rate 432 thy minions 433 , proud insulting boy.

Becomes it thee to be thus bold in terms

Before thy sovereign and thy lawful king?

EDWARD I am his king, and he should bow his knee:

I was adopted heir by his consent.

Since when, his oath is broke, for as I hear,

You that are king, though he do wear the crown,

Have caused him, by new act of parliament,

To blot out me and put his own son in.

CLIFFORD And reason too:

Who should succeed the father but the son?

RICHARD Are you there, butcher? O, I cannot speak!

CLIFFORD Ay, crookback, here I stand to answer thee,

Or any he 434 , the proudest of thy sort 435 .

RICHARD ’Twas you that killed young Rutland, was it not?

CLIFFORD Ay, and old York, and yet not satisfied.

RICHARD For God’s sake, lords, give signal to the fight.

WARWICK What say’st thou, Henry, wilt thou yield the crown?

QUEEN MARGARET Why, how now, long-tongued 436 Warwick, dare you speak?

When you and I met at St Albans last,

Your legs did better service than your hands 437 .

WARWICK Then ’twas my turn to fly, and now ’tis thine.

CLIFFORD You said so much before and yet you fled.

WARWICK ’Twas not your valour, Clifford, drove me thence.

NORTHUMBERLAND No, nor your manhood that durst 438 make you stay.

RICHARD Northumberland, I hold thee reverently 439 .

Break off the parley 440 , for scarce I can refrain 441

The execution 442 of my big-swol’n 443 heart

Upon that Clifford, that cruel child-killer.

CLIFFORD I slew thy father. Call’st thou him a child?

RICHARD Ay, like a dastard 444 and a treacherous coward,

As thou didst kill our tender 445 brother Rutland.

But ere sunset, I’ll make thee curse the deed.

KING HENRY VI Have done with words, my lords, and hear me speak.

QUEEN MARGARET Defy them then, or else hold close thy lips.

KING HENRY VI I prithee, give no limits to my tongue:

I am a king and privileged to speak.

CLIFFORD My liege, the wound that bred this meeting here

Cannot be cured by words: therefore be still 446 .

RICHARD Then, executioner, unsheathe thy sword:

By him that made us all 447 , I am resolved 448

That Clifford’s manhood lies 449 upon his tongue 450 .

EDWARD Say, Henry, shall I have my right or no?

A thousand men have broke their fasts 451 today,

That ne’er shall dine unless thou yield the crown.

WARWICK If thou deny 452 , their blood upon thy head,

For York in justice puts his armour on.

PRINCE EDWARD If that be right which Warwick says is right,

There is no wrong, but everything is right.

RICHARD Whoever got 453 thee, there thy mother stands,

For well I wot 454 , thou hast thy mother’s tongue.

QUEEN MARGARET But thou art neither like thy sire nor dam 455 ,

But like a foul misshapen stigmatic 456 ,

Marked 457 by the destinies 458 to be avoided,

As venom 459 toads or lizards’ dreadful stings.

RICHARD Iron of Naples 460 hid with English gilt 461 ,

Whose father bears the title of a king

As if a channel 462 should be called the sea

Sham’st thou not 463 , knowing whence thou art extraught 464 ,

To let thy tongue detect 465 thy base-born heart?

EDWARD A wisp of straw 466 were worth a thousand crowns,

To make this shameless callet 467 know herself.

Helen of Greece was fairer far than thou,

Although thy husband may be Menelaus 468 ;

And ne’er was Agamemnon’s brother 469 wronged

By that false woman, as this king by thee.

His father 470 revelled 471 in the heart of France,

And tamed the king, and made the dauphin stoop.

And had he 472 matched 473 according to his state 474 ,

He might have kept that glory to this day.

But when he took a beggar to his bed,

And graced thy poor sire 475 with his 476 bridal-day,

Even then that sunshine brewed a shower for him,

That washed his father’s fortunes forth 477 of France,

And heaped sedition 478 on his crown at home.

For what hath broached 479 this tumult but thy pride?

Hadst thou been meek, our title 480 still 481 had slept,

And we, in pity of the gentle king,

Had slipped 482 our claim until another age.

GEORGE But when we saw our sunshine made thy spring 483 ,

And that thy summer bred us no increase 484 ,

We set the axe to thy usurping root.

And though the edge hath something 485 hit ourselves,

Yet, know thou, since we have begun to strike,

We’ll never leave till we have hewn thee down,

Or bathed thy growing with our heated bloods.

EDWARD And in this resolution, I defy thee,

Not willing any longer conference,

Since thou denied’st 486 the gentle king to speak.

Sound trumpets, let our bloody colours wave,

And either victory or else a grave!

QUEEN MARGARET Stay, Edward.

EDWARD No, wrangling woman, we’ll no longer stay.

These words will cost ten thousand lives this day.

Exeunt
