Scene 4 513 / running scene 7

Enter Helicanus and Escanes

HELICANUS No, Escanes, know this of me:

Antiochus from incest lived not free,

For which the most high gods not minding longer 514

To withhold the vengeance that they had in store,

Due to this heinous capital 515 offence,

Even in the height and pride of all his glory —

When he was seated in a chariot

Of inestimable value, and his daughter with him —

A fire from heaven came and shrivelled up

Those bodies even to loathing. For they so stunk

That all those eyes adored 516 them ere their fall

Scorn now their hand should give them burial.

ESCANES ’Twas very strange.

HELICANUS And yet but justice: for though this king were great,

His greatness was no guard to bar 517 heaven’s shaft 518 ,

But sin had his 519 reward.

ESCANES ’Tis very true.

Enter two or three Lords

FIRST LORD See, not a man in private conference

Or council has respect 520 with him but he.

SECOND LORD It shall no longer grieve 521 without reproof.

THIRD LORD And cursed be he that will not second it.

FIRST LORD Follow me then.—

Lord Helicane, a word.

To Helicanus

HELICANUS With me? And welcome. Happy day 522 , my lords.

FIRST LORD Know that our griefs 523 are risen to the top,

And now at length they overflow their banks.

HELICANUS Your griefs, for what? Wrong not your prince, you love 524 .

FIRST LORD Wrong not yourself then, noble Helicane,

But if the prince do live let us salute him,

Or know what ground’s made happy by his breath 525 .

If in the world he live, we’ll seek him out,

If in his grave he rest, we’ll find him there.

We’ll be resolved 526 he lives 527 to govern us,

Or dead, give’s 528 cause to mourn his funeral 529

And leave us to our free election 530 .

SECOND LORD Whose 531 death’s indeed the strongest in our censure 532 .

And knowing this kingdom is without a head —

Like goodly buildings left 533 without a roof,

Soon fall to ruin — your noble self,

That best know how to rule and how to reign,

We thus submit unto: our sovereign.

ALL Live, noble Helicane!

HELICANUS Try honour’s cause 534 , forbear your suffrages 535 !

If that you love Prince Pericles, forbear 536 .

Take I your wish, I leap into the seas,

Where’s hourly trouble, for a minute’s ease.

A twelvemonth longer let me entreat you

To forbear 537 the absence of your king.

If in which time expired he not 538 return,

I shall with agèd patience bear your yoke 539 .

But if I cannot win you to this love 540 ,

Go search like nobles, like noble subjects,

And in your search spend your adventurous worth 541 ,

Whom 542 if you find, and win unto 543 return,

You shall like diamonds sit about his crown.

FIRST LORD To wisdom he’s a fool that will not yield.

And since Lord Helicane enjoineth 544 us,

We with our travels 545 will endeavour it.

HELICANUS Then you love us, we you, and we’ll clasp hands:

When peers thus knit 546 , a kingdom ever stands.

[Exeunt ]
