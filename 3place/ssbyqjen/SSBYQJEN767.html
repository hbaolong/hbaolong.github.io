Scene 3 230 / running scene 3

Enter Gloucester, with his Servingmen [in blue coats 231 ]

GLOUCESTER I am come to survey the Tower this day:

Since Henry’s death, I fear, there is conveyance 232 :

Where be these warders 233 , that they wait not here?

Servingmen knock on gates

Open the gates; ’tis Gloucester that calls.

FIRST WARDER Who’s there that knocks so imperiously?

Within

FIRST SERVINGMAN It is the noble Duke of Gloucester.

SECOND WARDER Whoe’er he be, you may not be let in.

Within

FIRST SERVINGMAN Villains 234 , answer you so the Lord Protector?

FIRST WARDER The Lord protect him, so we answer him:

Within

We do no otherwise than we are willed 235 .

GLOUCESTER Who willèd you? Or whose will stands but mine?

There’s none 236 protector of the realm but I.

Break up 237 the gates, I’ll be your warrantize 238 :

To Servingmen

Shall I be flouted 239 thus by dunghill grooms?

Gloucester’s men rush at the Tower Gates, and Woodville the Lieutenant speaks within

WOODVILLE What noise is this? What traitors have we here?

GLOUCESTER Lieutenant, is it you whose voice I hear?

Open the gates: here’s Gloucester that would enter.

WOODVILLE Have patience, noble duke: I may not open:

The Cardinal 240 of Winchester forbids:

From him I have express commandment

That thou nor none of thine shall be let in.

GLOUCESTER Faint-hearted Woodville, prizest him ’fore 241 me?

Arrogant Winchester, that haughty prelate,

Whom Henry, our late sovereign, ne’er could brook 242 ?

Thou art no friend to God or to the king:

Open the gates, or I’ll shut thee out shortly 243 .

SERVINGMEN Open the gates unto the Lord Protector,

Or we’ll burst them open, if that you come not quickly.

Enter to the [Lord ] Protector at the Tower Gates, [the Bishop of ] Winchester and his men in tawny 244 coats

WINCHESTER How now, ambitious umpire 245 ! What means this?

GLOUCESTER Peeled 246 priest, dost thou command me to be shut out?

WINCHESTER I do, thou most usurping proditor 247 ,

And not ‘Protector’, of the king or realm.

GLOUCESTER Stand back, thou manifest conspirator,

Thou that contrived’st 248 to murder our dead lord 249 :

Thou that giv’st whores indulgences 250 to sin 251 :

I’ll canvass 252 thee in thy broad cardinal’s hat 253 ,

If thou proceed in this thy insolence.

WINCHESTER Nay, stand thou back, I will not budge a foot:

This be Damascus, be thou cursèd Cain 254 ,

To slay thy brother Abel, if thou wilt.

GLOUCESTER I will not slay thee, but I’ll drive thee back:

Thy scarlet robes as a child’s bearing-cloth 255

I’ll use to carry thee out of this place.

WINCHESTER Do what thou dar’st, I beard 256 thee to thy face.

GLOUCESTER What? Am I dared 257 and bearded to my face?

Draw, men, for all this 258 privilegèd place 259 ;

All draw their swords

Blue coats to tawny coats. Priest, beware your beard,

I mean to tug it and to cuff you soundly.

Under my feet I stamp thy cardinal’s hat:

In spite of Pope or dignities 260 of Church,

Here by the cheeks I’ll drag thee up and down.

WINCHESTER Gloucester, thou wilt answer this before the Pope.

GLOUCESTER Winchester goose 261 , I cry, ‘A rope, a rope 262 !’—

Now beat them hence: why do you let them stay?—

To Servingmen

Thee I’ll chase hence, thou wolf in sheep’s array.—

To Winchester

Out, tawny coats!— Out, scarlet hypocrite!

Here Gloucester’s men beat out the [Bishop of Winchester’s ] men, and enter in the hurly-burly 263 the Mayor of London and his Officers

MAYOR Fie 264 , lords, that you, being supreme magistrates,

Thus contumeliously 265 should break the peace!

GLOUCESTER Peace, mayor, thou know’st little of my wrongs:

Here’s Beaufort, that regards nor God nor king,

Hath here distrained 266 the Tower to his use.

WINCHESTER Here’s Gloucester — a foe to citizens,

One that still 267 motions 268 war and never peace,

O’ercharging your free 269 purses with large fines 270 —

That seeks to overthrow religion,

Because he is Protector of the realm,

And would have armour here out of the Tower,

To crown himself king and suppress the prince 271 .

GLOUCESTER I will not answer thee with words, but blows.

Here they skirmish again

MAYOR Naught rests 272 for me, in this tumultuous strife,

But to make open proclamation.

Come, officer, as loud as e’er thou canst, cry.

Handing a paper to the Officer, who reads

OFFICER All manner of men assembled here in arms this day against God’s peace and the king’s, we charge and command you, in his highness’ name, to repair 273 to your several 274 dwelling-places, and not to wear, handle, or use any sword, weapon, or dagger, henceforward, upon pain of death.

The skirmish ends

GLOUCESTER Cardinal, I’ll be no break 275 er of the law:

But we shall meet, and break our minds at large 276 .

WINCHESTER Gloucester, we’ll meet to thy cost, be sure:

Thy heart-blood I will have for this day’s work.

MAYOR I’ll call for clubs 277 , if you will not away:

This cardinal’s more haughty than the devil.

GLOUCESTER Mayor, farewell: thou dost but what thou mayst.

WINCHESTER Abominable 278 Gloucester, guard thy head,

For I intend to have it ere 279 long.

Exeunt [separately, Gloucester and Bishop of Winchester with their Servingmen ]

MAYOR See the coast cleared, and then we will depart.

Good God, these nobles should such stomachs 280 bear!

I myself fight not once in forty year.

Exeunt
