Scene 2 513 ／running scene 6

Enter Queen, Bushy and Bagot

BUSHY Madam, your majesty is too much sad.

You promised, when you parted with the king,

To lay aside life-harming heaviness 514

And entertain 515 a cheerful disposition.

QUEEN To please the king I did. To please myself

I cannot do it. Yet I know no cause

Why I should welcome such a guest as grief,

Save bidding farewell to so sweet a guest

As my sweet Richard. Yet again, methinks,

Some unborn sorrow, ripe in fortune’s womb,

Is coming towards me, and my inward soul

With nothing trembles. At something it grieves,

More than with parting from my lord the king.

BUSHY Each substance of a grief hath twenty shadows 516 ,

Which shows like grief itself, but is not so.

For sorrow’s eye, glazèd 517 with blinding tears,

Divides one thing entire to 518 many objects,

Like perspectives 519 , which rightly 520 gazed upon

Show nothing but confusion: eyed awry 521

Distinguish form 522 . So your sweet majesty,

Looking awry upon your lord’s departure,

Find shapes of grief, more than himself 523 to wail 524 ,

Which, looked on as it is, is naught but shadows

Of what it is not. Then, thrice-gracious queen,

More 525 than your lord’s departure weep not. More’s not seen;

Or if it be, ’tis with false sorrow’s eye,

Which for 526 things true weeps things imaginary.

QUEEN It may be so, but yet my inward soul

Persuades me it is otherwise. Howe’er it be,

I cannot but be sad, so heavy 527 sad

As though on think 528 ing on no thought I think,

Makes me with heavy nothing faint and shrink.

BUSHY ’Tis nothing but conceit 529 , my gracious lady.

QUEEN ’Tis nothing less 530 . Conceit is still 531 derived

From some forefather grief. Mine is not so,

For nothing hath begot 532 my something 533 grief,

Or something hath the nothing that I grieve 534 .

’Tis in reversion 535 that I do possess ——

But what it is, that is not yet known —— what

I cannot name. ’Tis nameless woe, I wot 536 .

Enter Green

GREEN Heaven save your majesty! And well met, gentlemen.

I hope the king is not yet shipped for Ireland.

QUEEN Why hop’st thou so? ’Tis better hope he is,

For his designs crave haste, his haste good hope.

Then wherefore 537 dost thou hope he is not shipped?

GREEN That he, our hope, might have retired 538 his power,

And driven into despair an enemy’s hope,

Who strongly 539 hath set footing in this land.

The banished Bullingbrook repeals himself 540 ,

And with uplifted arms 541 is safe arrived

At Ravenspurgh.

QUEEN Now God in heaven forbid!

GREEN O, madam, ’tis too true. And that is worse,

The Lord Northumberland, his son young Henry Percy,

The Lords of Ross, Beaumond and Willoughby,

With all their powerful friends, are fled to him.

BUSHY Why have you not proclaimed Northumberland

And the rest of the revolted faction, traitors?

GREEN We have: whereupon the Earl of Worcester

Hath broke his staff 542 , resigned his stewardship,

And all the household 543 servants fled with him

To Bullingbrook.

QUEEN So, Green, thou art the midwife of my woe,

And Bullingbrook my sorrow’s dismal heir 544 .

Now hath my soul brought forth her prodigy 545 ,

And I, a gasping new-delivered mother,

Have woe to woe, sorrow to sorrow joined.

BUSHY Despair not, madam.

QUEEN Who shall hinder me?

I will despair, and be at enmity

With cozening 546 hope; he is a flatterer,

A parasite, a keeper-back of death,

Who gently would dissolve the bands 547 of life,

Which false hope lingers 548 in extremity 549 .

Enter York

GREEN Here comes the Duke of York.

QUEEN With signs of war about his agèd neck.

O, full of careful 550 business are his looks!

Uncle, for heaven’s sake, speak comfortable 551 words.

YORK Comfort’s in heaven, and we are on the earth,

Where nothing lives but crosses 552 , care and grief.

Your husband, he is gone to save 553 far off,

Whilst others come to make him lose at home.

Here am I left to underprop 554 his land,

Who, weak with age, cannot support myself.

Now comes the sick hour that his surfeit 555 made,

Now shall he try 556 his friends that flattered him.

Enter a Servant

SERVANT My lord, your son 557 was gone before I came.

YORK He was? Why, so! Go all which way it will!

The nobles they are fled, the commons they are cold 558 ,

And will, I fear, revolt on Hereford’s side.

Sirrah 559 , get thee to Plashy, to my sister 560 Gloucester:

Bid her send me presently 561 a thousand pound.

Hold, take my ring 562 .

SERVANT My lord, I had forgot to tell your lordship:

Today as I came by, I callèd there ——

But I shall grieve you to report the rest.

YORK What is’t, knave?

SERVANT An hour before I came, the duchess died.

YORK Heav’n 563 for his mercy! What a tide of woes

Come rushing on this woeful land at once!

I know not what to do. I would 564 to heaven ——

So 565 my untruth 566 had not provoked him to it ——

The king had cut off my head with my brother’s 567 .

What, are there posts 568 dispatched for Ireland?

How shall we do 569 for money for these wars?

Come, sister —— cousin, I would say —— pray, pardon me.——

To Queen

Go, fellow, get thee home, provide some carts

To Servant

And bring away the armour that is there.——

[Exit Servant ]

Gentlemen, will you muster 570 men?

If I know how or which way to order these affairs

Thus disorderly thrust into my hands,

Never believe me. Both are my kinsmen.

Th’one is my sovereign, whom both my oath 571

And duty bids defend: th’other again

Is my kinsman, whom the king hath wronged,

Whom conscience and my kindred 572 bids to right.

Well, somewhat 573 we must do.—— Come, cousin, I’ll

Dispose of 574 you.——

Gentlemen, go muster up your men,

And meet me presently at Berkeley Castle 575 .

I should to Plashy too,

But time will not permit. All is uneven,

And everything is left at six and seven 576 .

Exeunt [York and Queen ]

BUSHY The wind sits 577 fair for news to go to Ireland,

But none returns. For us to levy power 578

Proportionable to th’enemy

Is all impossible.

GREEN Besides, our nearness to the king in love

Is near the hate of those 579 love not the king.

BAGOT And that’s the wavering commons, for their love

Lies in their purses, and whoso empties them,

By so much fills their hearts with deadly hate.

BUSHY Wherein the king stands generally 580 condemned.

BAGOT If judgement lie in them, then so do we 581 ,

Because we have been ever near the king.

GREEN Well, I will for refuge straight 582 to Bristol Castle.

The Earl of Wiltshire is already there.

BUSHY Thither will I with you, for little office 583

Will the hateful 584 commons perform for us,

Except like curs 585 to tear us all in pieces.

Will you go along with us?

To Bagot

BAGOT No, I will to Ireland to his majesty.

Farewell. If heart’s presages 586 be not vain 587 ,

We three here part that ne’er shall meet again.

BUSHY That’s as 588 York thrives to beat back Bullingbrook.

GREEN Alas, poor duke! The task he undertakes

Is numb’ring sands and drinking oceans dry.

Where one on his side fights, thousands will fly.

BUSHY Farewell at once, for once, for all, and ever.

Well, we may meet again.

BAGOT I fear me, never.

Exeunt
