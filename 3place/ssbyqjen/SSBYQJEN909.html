Enter Antiochus, Prince Pericles 38 and Followers

ANTIOCHUS Young Prince of Tyre 39 , you have at large received 40

The danger of the task you undertake?

PERICLES I have, Antiochus, and with a soul

Emboldened with the glory of her praise

Think death no hazard in this enterprise.

ANTIOCHUS Music!

Music plays

Bring in our daughter, clothèd like a bride

For embracements 41 even of Jove 42 himself,

At whose 43 conception, till Lucina 44 reigned 45 ,

Nature this dowry gave: to glad her presence 46

The senate house 47 of planets all did sit,

To knit in her their best perfections 48 .

Enter Antiochus’ Daughter

PERICLES See where she comes, apparelled like the spring 49 ,

Graces her subjects 50 , and her thoughts the king

Of every virtue gives 51 renown 52 to men:

Her face the book of praises 53 , where is read

Nothing but curious 54 pleasures, as 55 from thence

Sorrow were ever razed 56 , and testy 57 wrath

Could never be her mild companion 58 .

You gods that made me man and sway 59 in love,

That have inflamed desire in my breast

To taste the fruit of yon 60 celestial tree 61

Or die in the adventure, be my helps,

As I am son and servant to your will,

To compass 62 such a boundless happiness.

ANTIOCHUS Prince Pericles—

PERICLES That would be son 63 to great Antiochus.

ANTIOCHUS Before thee stands this fair Hesperides,

With golden fruit 64 , but dangerous to be touched,

For deathlike 65 dragons 66 here affright thee hard.

Her face, like heaven, enticeth thee to view

Her countless 67 glory, which desert 68 must gain,

And which without desert, because thine eye

Presumes to reach 69 , all the whole heap 70 must die.

Yon 71 sometimes 72 famous princes, like thyself

Points to the heads

Drawn by report, adventurous 73 by desire,

Tell thee with speechless tongues and semblance 74 pale

That without covering save yon 75 field of stars

Here they stand, martyrs slain in Cupid’s wars,

And with dead cheeks advise thee to desist

From going on death’s net 76 , whom none resist.

PERICLES Antiochus, I thank thee, who hath taught

My frail mortality 77 to know itself,

And by those fearful objects 78 to prepare

This body, like to them, to what I must:

For death remembered 79 should be like a mirror

Who tells us life’s but breath 80 , to trust it error.

I’ll make my will, then, and as sick men do

Who know the world, see heaven, but feeling woe

Grip not at earthly joys as erst they did 81 .

So I bequeath a happy peace to you

And all good men, as every prince should do,

My riches to the earth from whence they came,—

But my unspotted 82 fire of love to you.—

To Daughter

Thus ready for the way of life or death,

To Antiochus

I wait the sharpest blow.

ANTIOCHUS Scorning advice, read the conclusion 83 then,

Gives Pericles the riddle

Which read and not expounded, ’tis decreed,

As these 84 before thee, thou thyself shalt bleed.

DAUGHTER Of all ’ssayed 85 yet, mayst thou prove prosperous,

To Pericles

Of all ’ssayed yet, I wish thee happiness.

PERICLES Like a bold champion I assume the lists 86 ,

Nor 87 ask advice of any other thought

But faithfulness and courage.

The riddle

Reads

’I am no viper, yet I feed

On mother’s flesh 88 which did me breed.

I sought a husband, in which labour 89

I found that kindness 90 in a father.

He’s father, son and husband mild,

I mother, wife and yet his child:

How they may be, and yet in two 91 ,

As you will live resolve it you.’

Sharp physic 92 is the last 93 !— But O, you powers

Aside

That gives heaven countless eyes 94 to view men’s acts,

Why cloud they not their sights 95 perpetually

If this be true, which makes me pale to read it?—

Fair glass of light 96 , I loved you, and could still

To Daughter

Were not this glorious casket 97 stored with ill.

But I must tell you, now my thoughts revolt,

For he’s no man on whom perfections wait 98 ,

That knowing sin within will touch the gate 99 100 .

You are a fair viol 101 , and your sense 102 the strings,

Who, fingered to make man his lawful music 103 ,

Would draw heaven down, and all the gods to hearken.

But being played upon 104 before your time,

Hell only danceth at so harsh a chime 105 .

Good sooth 106 ,

Pericles gestures towards the Daughter

I care not for you.

ANTIOCHUS Prince Pericles, touch not, upon thy life,

For that’s an article 107 within our law

As dangerous as the rest. Your time’s expired:

Either expound now or receive your sentence.

PERICLES Great king,

Few love to hear the sins they love to act,

’Twould braid 108 yourself too near 109 for me to tell it.

Who 110 has a book of all that monarchs do,

He’s more secure to keep it shut than shown.

For vice repeated is like the wandering wind

Blows dust in others’ eyes to spread itself 111 .

And yet the end of all is bought thus dear,

The breath is gone and the sore eyes see clear

To stop the air would hurt them 112 . The blind mole casts

Copped 113 hills towards heaven, to tell 114 the earth is thronged 115

By man’s oppression, and the poor worm 116 doth die for’t.

Kings are earth’s gods: in vice, their law’s their will,

And if Jove stray, who dares say Jove doth ill?

It is enough you know, and it is fit,

What being more known grows worse, to smother it 117 .

All love the womb that their first being bred,

Then give my tongue like leave 118 to love my head.

ANTIOCHUS Heaven, that I had thy head!

Aside

He has found the meaning,

But I will gloze with 119 him.—

Young prince of Tyre,

To Pericles

Though by the tenor 120 of your strict edict 121 ,

Your exposition 122 misinterpreting,

We might proceed to cancel of 123 your days,

Yet hope, succeeding from so fair a tree

As your fair self, doth tune us otherwise 124 .

Forty days longer we do respite you,

If by which time our secret be undone 125 ,

This mercy shows we’ll joy 126 in such a son.

And until then your entertain 127 shall be

As doth befit our honour and your worth.

[Exeunt. ] Pericles remains alone

PERICLES How courtesy would seem to 128 cover sin,

When what is done is like an hypocrite,

The which is good in nothing but in sight 129 .

If it be true that I interpret false,

Then were it certain you were not so bad

As with foul incest to abuse your soul:

Where now you’re both a father and a son

By your untimely 130 claspings with your child —

Which pleasures fits a husband, not a father —

And she an eater of her mother’s flesh

By the defiling of her parents’ bed.

And both like serpents are, who though they feed

On sweetest flowers, yet they poison breed 131 .

Antioch farewell, for wisdom sees those men 132

Blush not in actions blacker than the night

Will ’schew 133 no course to keep them from the light 134 .

One sin, I know, another doth provoke:

Murder’s as near to lust as flame to smoke.

Poison and treason are the hands of sin —

Ay, and the targets 135 to put off 136 the shame.

Then lest my life be cropped 137 , to keep you clear 138 ,

By flight, I’ll shun the danger which I fear.

Exit

Enter Antiochus

ANTIOCHUS He hath found the meaning,

For which we mean to have his head:

He must not live to trumpet forth 139 my infamy,

Nor tell the world Antiochus doth sin

In such a loathèd manner.

And therefore instantly this prince must die,

For by his fall my honour must keep high.

Who attends us there?

Enter Thaliard 140

THALIARD Doth your highness call?

ANTIOCHUS Thaliard, you are of our chamber 141 , Thaliard,

And our mind partakes 142 her private actions

To your secrecy, and for your faithfulness

We will advance you, Thaliard.

Behold, here’s poison and here’s gold:

We hate the Prince of Tyre, and thou must kill him.

It fits thee not to ask the reason why:

Because we bid it. Say, is it done?

THALIARD My lord, ’tis done.

Enter a Messenger

Running

ANTIOCHUS Enough.—

Let your breath cool yourself, telling your haste 143 .

To Messenger

MESSENGER My lord, Prince Pericles is fled.

[Exit ]

ANTIOCHUS As thou wilt live, fly after, and like an arrow

To Thaliard

Shot from a well experienced archer hits

The mark his eye doth level 144 at,

So thou never return unless thou say

Prince Pericles is dead.

THALIARD My lord, if I can get him within my pistol’s length 145 I’ll make him sure 146 enough, so farewell to your highness.

ANTIOCHUS Thaliard adieu.— Till Pericles be dead,
