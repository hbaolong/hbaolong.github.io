Scene 1 ／ running scene 1 6 The Scene: Vienna

Enter Duke, Escalus, Lords [and Attendant s]

DUKE Escalus.

ESCALUS My lord.

DUKE Of government the properties 7 to unfold 8

Would seem in me t’affect 9 speech and discourse,

Since I am put to know 10 that your own science 11

Exceeds, in that 12 , the lists 13 of all advice

My strength 14 can give you. Then no more remains

But that to your sufficiency as your worth is able,

And let them work 15 . The nature of our people,

Our city’s institutions 16 , and the terms 17

For common justice, you’re as pregnant 18 in

As art and practice 19 hath enrichèd any

That we remember. There is our commission,

Hands him a paper

From which we would not have you warp 20 . Call hither,

I say, bid come before us Angelo.

[Exit an Attendant ]

What figure of us think you he will bear? 21

For you must know, we have with special soul 22

Elected him our absence to supply 23 ;

Lent him our terror 24 , dressed him with our love 25 ,

And given his deputation 26 all the organs 27

Of our own power. What think you of it?

ESCALUS If any in Vienna be of worth

To undergo 28 such ample grace 29 and honour,

It is Lord Angelo 30 .

DUKE Look where he comes.

Enter Angelo

ANGELO Always obedient to your grace’s will,

I come to know your pleasure 31 .

DUKE Angelo,

There is a kind of character 32 in thy life

That to th’observer doth thy history 33

Fully unfold 34 . Thyself and thy belongings 35

Are not thine own so proper 36 as to waste

Thyself upon thy virtues, they on thee 37 .

Heaven doth with us as we with torches do,

Not light them for themselves: for if our virtues

Did not go forth of 38 us, ’twere all alike

As if we had them not. Spirits 39 are not finely touched 40

But to fine issues 41 , nor nature never lends

The smallest scruple 42 of her excellence

But, like a thrifty 43 goddess, she determines

Herself the glory of a creditor,

Both thanks and use 44 . But I do bend 45 my speech

To one that can my part in him advertise 46 .

Hold 47 therefore, Angelo.

In our remove 48 be thou at full 49 ourself:

Mortality 50 and mercy in Vienna

Live in thy tongue and heart. Old Escalus,

Though first in question 51 , is thy secondary 52 .

Take thy commission.

Offers a paper

ANGELO Now, good my lord,

Let there be some more test made of my mettle 53 ,

Before so noble and so great a figure 54

Be stamped upon it.

DUKE No more evasion.

We have with a leavened 55 and preparèd choice

Proceeded to you: therefore take your honours.

Angelo takes paper

Our haste from hence is of so quick condition 56

That it prefers itself 57 and leaves unquestioned 58

Matters of needful value 59 . We shall write to you,

As time and our concernings 60 shall importune 61 ,

How it goes with us, and do look 62 to know

What doth befall you here. So, fare you well:

To th’hopeful 63 execution do I leave 64 you

Of your commissions.

ANGELO Yet give leave, my lord,

That we may bring you something 65 on the way.

DUKE My haste may not admit 66 it,

Nor need you, on mine honour, have to do

With any scruple 67 . Your scope is as mine own,

So to enforce or qualify 68 the laws

As to your soul seems good. Give me your hand,

I’ll privily 69 away. I love the people,

But do not like to stage me 70 to their eyes:

Though it do well 71 , I do not relish well

Their loud applause and aves 72 vehement,

Nor do I think the man of safe discretion 73

That does affect 74 it. Once more, fare you well.

ANGELO The heavens give safety to your purposes!

ESCALUS Lead 75 forth and bring you back in happiness!

DUKE I thank you. Fare you well.

Exit

ESCALUS I shall desire you, sir, to give me leave

To have free speech with you; and it concerns me

To look into the bottom of my place 76 .

A power I have, but of what strength and nature

I am not yet instructed.

ANGELO ’Tis so with me. Let us withdraw together,

And we may soon our satisfaction have

Touching 77 that point.

ESCALUS I’ll wait upon your honour.

Exeunt
