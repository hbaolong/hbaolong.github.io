(d) Choosing Sides in a Game

Children played the same games as adults, but separately from them. In addition to the following passags, see doc. 30b where the young Egil plays a murderous game.

Source: trans. A.A. Somerville, from Flóamanna saga, in Harðar saga, ed. Þórhallur Vilmundarson and Bjarni Vilhjálmsson, Íslenzk fornrit XIII (Reykjavík, 1991), pp. 249–51.

  1. …One summer people assembled for a meeting at the sanctuary in Lon. Thorgils, who was five years old at the time, wanted to join in the boys’ game, so he marked out a pitch and said that he wanted to take part. The boys, however, said they had already made up their minds that only someone who had killed a living being could take part in the game. So Thorgils left the game, upset at being excluded. In the evening, everyone went home.

Thorgrim [Thorgils’ step-father] was rich in all kinds of property and had much livestock, both sheep and cattle. He owned a carthorse of little value called Illing. At night, when everyone was asleep, Thorgils pondered what to do, since he did not want to be excluded from the game again. He got out of bed, picked up a bridle and went outside. There he saw a horse near the yard. He caught the horse, which was Illing, and led it to one of the farm-buildings. Next, he picked up a spear and drove it into the horse’s stomach. The horse fell down dead, and Thorgils went back to bed.

In the morning, when work was being assigned, Thorgrim gave orders for Illing to be fetched. Illing, however, could not be found. When Thorgrim was informed of this, he said that the horse had been there the previous evening and they should look again. So, they looked again and found the horse dead. On hearing the news, Thorgrim said that he didn’t know anyone who had this much ill will towards him.

“I’m responsible for the death of the horse,” said Thorgils.Thorgrim asked why he had done it and Thorgils told him why.

“You and I are very different people,” said Thorgrim. “We’ll never think alike, so you had better go to your friend Lopt’s.”
